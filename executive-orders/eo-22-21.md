# Establishing the Oregon Interagency Council on Homelessness (Executive Order 22-21)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-21

    ESTABLISHING THE OREGON INTERAGENCY COUNCIL ON
    HOMELESSNESS

    Every resident of Oregon deserves a safe, decent, affordable place to live, but that
    reality is out of reach for far too many. Despite tireless efforts and significant
    progress toward ending homelessness over the last 30 years, Oregon’s current
    systems require equity-focused transformation in order to meet the challenges of
    the next decade and beyond.

    Homelessness and housing insecurity are the unintended products of decades of
    intentional policy choices across many different public systems, rooted in and
    perpetuated by structural racism at the federal, state, and local levels. These effects
    also permeate Oregon’s small town and rural communities in uniquely challenging
    ways. While Oregon’s homeless service programs often lead the nation in best
    practices, we still see unjustly disproportionate rates of homelessness among people
    who identify as Black, Native, and Latino/a/x, as well as in other historically
    marginalized communities, which is a product of our stark history of racism and its
    particular impact on people of African and Caribbean, Native, Latino/a/x,
    Hawaiian, and East Asian descent in Oregon.

    We must continue transforming our systems if we are to make tangible change
    from our state’s current trajectory, systematically and sustainably in all Oregon
    communities. In order to provide accessible, equitable, and sufficient services to
    regain security and ultimately prevent homelessness, our systems require an
    upgrade. Intentional, statewide systems transformation can lead us to an Oregon
    where everyone who wants a safe, decent, and affordable place to live can find one.

    PURSUANT TO MY AUTHORITY AS GOVERNOR OF THE STATE OF
    OREGON, I FIND THAT:

    Whereas, most Oregonians experiencing homelessness are not able to access the
    range of services and supports they need to establish safety and housing security;
    and

    Whereas, experiencing homelessness exposes individuals and families to grave
    physical and behavioral health risks that often lead to complex health issues and
    premature death; and

    Whereas, homelessness is disproportionately more likely to affect Black and Native
    Oregonians, at respective rates of three and four times their share of the total state
    population; and

    Whereas, Latino/a/x families make up at least 15% of the total number of families
    experiencing homelessness in Oregon and an increased percentage of the number of
    people living in shelter since 2017; and

    Whereas, the health and economic impacts of COVID-19 pandemic led to a 49%

    Whereas, in Oregon on a single night in January 2021, a total of 18,007 people

    4,579 people living in shelter; and

    Whereas, the experience of homelessness is exacerbated for and disparately effects
    veterans, seniors and older adults, and LGBTQIA+ Oregonians due to a myriad of
    structural and social pressures and failings; and

    Whereas, the safety, health, and well-being of an entire community is destabilized
    when individuals and families have no meaningful choice but to live outside; and

    Whereas, state agencies have a duty to lead the state and support communities in
    providing for the safety and wellness of all of its residents; and

    Whereas, disconnected and difficult-to-navigate public service systems and lacking
    services exacerbate the impact of housing insecurity and homelessness; and

    Whereas, evidence-based practices and trauma-informed care for people
    experiencing homelessness who are navigating multiple public systems can ease
    burdens and accelerate the process of regaining stable health and housing security;
    and

    Whereas, the State of Oregon honors the sovereignty of Native American Tribes
    across Oregon and recognizes and seeks to mitigate the history and harmful
    impacts of state policy on housing security, health, and communal well-being of
    Native Americans in Oregon; and

    Whereas, the State of Oregon celebrates thriving Black, Latino/a/x, Asian, Pacific
    Islander, Tribal and communities of color across the state and recognizes and seeks
    to mitigate the impact and history of state policy, including Black exclusion laws,
    land donation, restrictive covenants, redlining, and labor discrimination on housing
    security, health, intergenerational well-being and homelessness among Black,
    Indigenous and other communities of color in Oregon; and

    Whereas, several forums of state administrators, community partners, and
    representatives produced recommendations for the state to establish an interagency
    council to align the multiple state agencies that set policy, fund, and manage
    programs serving and impacting people experiencing homelessness; and

    Whereas, it has been recommended by a state task force that in order to decrease
    rates of racial disparity among people experiencing homelessness, the state should
    transform its systems to: (1) share decision-making power with people with lived
    expertise and experience of homelessness, (2) improve data collection to better
    understand the scope and impact of homelessness and how it varies by geography,
    (3) increase access through targeted strategies, and (4) adopt continuous quality
    improvement processes and an orientation that prioritizes learning,
    experimentation, and iteration in the development of all statues and rules; all of
    which can be pursued through high-level interagency collaboration within the state
    government.

    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

        1. The Oregon Interagency Council on Homelessness (“the Council”) is
            established.

       2. The charge of the Council is to improve and align state programs, funding,
          and policies to drive progress toward preventing and ending homelessness
          statewide and to transform and improve state support for communities and
            people experiencing homelessness across the state.
