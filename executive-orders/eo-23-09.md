# Expanding Homelessness State of Emergency Declared in Executive Order 23-02 to include Clatsop, Linn and Malheur Counties (Executive Order 23-09)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 23-09

    EXPANDING HOMELESSNESS STATE OF EMERGEGNCY DECLARED
    IN EXECUTIVE ORDER 23-02 TO INCLUDE CLATSOP, LINN AND
    MALHEUR COUNTIES.

            Governor Tina Kotek signed Executive Order 23-02 on January 10, 2023,
    declaring a homelessness state of emergency in Continuums of Care that
    experienced an increase in unsheltered homelessness of 50% or more between 2017
    to 2022. Although the Governor recognized that homelessness was a crisis in
    communities across Oregon, the Governor set this threshold to allow the State’s
    limited emergency response infrastructure to focus on delivering targeted results in
    areas with the highest increases in unsheltered homelessness. Four continuum of
    care regions and the Metro region met criteria for inclusion in the emergency
    declared in Executive Order 23-02: (1) Metro region Continuums of Care; (2)
    Central Oregon; (3) Eugene Springfield/Lane County; (4) Medford,
    Ashland/Jackson County; and (5) Salem/Marion Polk Counties. The circumstances
    and bases for the homelessness state of emergency declaration in those initial
    emergency areas are set forth in Executive Order 23-02, and are incorporated
    herein. Those circumstances and bases continue to exist.

            Executive Order 23-02 stated that the Governor would reassess the
   necessity of the Executive Order every two months, and would retain, modify, or
    supplement it as circumstances warranted. Separately, county governing bodies in
    areas of the state not included in Executive Order 23-02 were notified that they
   could request to be included in the state’s emergency declaration by submitting a
   request through the Oregon Department of Emergency Management. Counties in
   the Balance of State Continuum of Care requesting to be included in the emergency
   order would need to meet the following criteria:

       1.   The unsheltered population in 2022 must be greater than 30 households, and

       2.   The community has declared a local state of emergency related to
            homelessness, and

       3.   Atleast one of the following is true:

               a.   Unsheltered homelessness increased by 50% or more between 2017-
                    DOOD:

               b.   The rate of unsheltered homelessness in 2022 was 80% or greater.

           Clatsop, Linn, and Malheur counties are counties in an area of the State not
    included in Executive Order 23-02. They have each submitted a request to be
    included in the emergency order, and each meets the inclusion criteria. In
    particular:

            With respect to Clatsop County:

        1. On February 23, 2023, the Clatsop County Board of Commissioners
           declared a local state of emergency related to homelessness;

        2. The number of unsheltered people in Clatsop County was 529 in 2022,
           which exceeds the minimum threshold of 30 households experiencing
            unsheltered homelessness; and

        3. The share of people experiencing unsheltered homelessness in Clatsop
           County in 2022 was 99%, which exceeds the minimum threshold of a rate
            of unsheltered homelessness of 80% or greater.

            With respect to Linn County:

        1. On March 14, 2023, the Linn County Board of Commissioners declared a
            local state of emergency related to homelessness.

        2. The number of unsheltered people in Linn County was 137 in 2022, which
           exceeds the minimum threshold of 30 households experiencing unsheltered
            homelessness; and

        3. Unsheltered homelessness increased by 104% between 2017 and 2022,
            which exceeds the minimum threshold of 50%.

            With Respect to Malheur County:

        1. On March 8, 2023, the Malheur County declared a local state of emergency
            related to homelessness.

        2. The number of unsheltered people in Malheur County was 141 in 2022,
           which exceeds the minimum threshold of 30 households experiencing
            unsheltered homelessness; and

          ORS 401.165 et seg. empowers the Governor to declare a state of
   emergency upon determining that an emergency has occurred or is imminent.
   Pursuant to that authority, I find that unsheltered homelessness in Clatsop County,
   Linn County and Malheur County has caused and is threatening to cause
   widespread injury to people and property, widespread human suffering, and
   widespread financial loss and thus constitutes an emergency under ORS
    401.025(1).

           To reduce homelessness and its impacts, this expansion of the declaration of
   emergency is necessary to rapidly expand the state’s low-barrier shelter capacity, to
   rehouse people experiencing unsheltered homelessness, and to prevent
   homelessness to the greatest extent possible. This Executive Order adds Clatsop
   County, Linn County and Malheur County to the emergency area covered by the
   directives in Executive Order 23-02, which in turn directs the state’s emergency
   management response capacity and an initial infusion of targeted funding and other
   resources that will meaningfully reduce the level of unsheltered homelessness in
   Oregon, while establishing a framework to support the continued reduction of
   homelessness in the long term, even after the state of emergency has ended.

   NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED THAT:

           There is a state of emergency in Clatsop County, Linn County and Malheur
   County due to unsheltered homelessness. I declare this emergency for Clatsop
   County, Linn County and Malheur County for the reasons stated above, and add
   these counties to the emergency areas designated in Executive Order 23-02.

       1.   Directive in Executive Order 23-02 to Include Clatsop County, Linn
            County, and Malheur County: From the effective date of this Executive
            Order forward, the directives in Executive Order 23-02 apply to Clatsop
            County, Linn County and Malheur County, and activities therein.
