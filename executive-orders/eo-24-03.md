    PURSUANT TO MY AUTHORITY AS THE GOVERNOR OF THE STATE
    OF OREGON, I FIND THAT:

    WHEREAS, the number of individuals experiencing homelessness statewide
    increased by forty four percent between 2017 and 2023; and

    WHEREAS, in 2023, an estimated 20,100 individuals were experiencing
    homelessness according to the 2023 Point in Time Count conducted in January of
    that year; and

    WHEREAS, the Point in Time Count is an underestimate of the number of people
    experiencing homelessness and the number of people experiencing homelessness in
    the state is likely higher; and

    WHEREAS, Black, Indigenous, and Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander
    persons are overrepresented in Oregon’s homeless population compared to their
    overall proportion of the state’s population, indicating that racial disparities in
    homelessness are a direct result of structural racism and public systems designed to
    perpetuate inequity; and

    WHEREAS, Oregon is one of only five states in the nation where people are most
    likely to experience unsheltered homelessness, and where specific populations such
    as families with children, veterans, and people with disabling conditions are
    experiencing homelessness at increasing rates; and

    WHEREAS, in 2022, Executive Order No. 22-21 established the Oregon
    Interagency Council on Homelessness (“ICH”) with the goal of intentional,
    statewide systems transformation through the improvement and alignment of state
    programs, funding, and policies to drive progress toward preventing and ending
    homelessness statewide and to transform and improve state support for
    communities and people experiencing homelessness across the state; and

    WHEREAS, people living in Oregon often navigate complicated pathways to
    obtaining services needed to establish housing stability; and

    WHEREAS, homelessness is disproportionately more likely to affect Black,
    Indigenous and Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander people and few existing
    state programs are tailored to address the needs of these specific populations; and

    WHEREAS, culturally-specific or population-specific services are not easily
    accessible in all parts of the State where people of color, older adults, youth, or
    formerly incarcerated persons experience housing ins ability; and

    WHEREAS, data collection is necessary to inform statewide goals and equitable
    outcomes to drive decision-making and advance accountability of State
    investments; and

    WHEREAS, service providers offering homeless services experience barriers and
    challenges in obtaining State funding; and

    WHEREAS, the lack of coordination and alignment amongst state agencies
    administering funding for homeless services or directly administering homeless
    services may lead to redundancies and gaps in the use of State resources; and

    WHEREAS, the State must improve its programs and policies to more effectively
    reduce and prevent homelessness; and

    WHEREAS, modifying the Interagency Council on Homelessness established in
    EO 22-21 with clear direction to achieve the objectives identified through the
    implementation of EO 23-03 will improve the State’s homeless service system to
    more effectively prevent and reduce homelessness.
