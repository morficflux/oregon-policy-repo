# Proclamation of State of Emergency due to Imminent Threat of Wildfire (Executive Order 24-13)

## At a glance

Executive Order 24-13: Proclamation of State of Emergency due to Imminent Threat of Wildfire. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-13

     PROCLAMATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY DUE TO IMMINENT
     THREAT OF WILDFIRE

     Pursuant to ORS 401.165 e/ seq., | find that the State of Oregon is in a critical fire
     danger situation threatening life, safety, and property due to extreme high
     temperatures. We have experienced wildfires this season that resulted in two state
     Conflagration declarations and federal Fire Management Assistance Grant (FMAG)
     declarations, several community evacuations, threatened and damaged structures
     and critical infrastructure. Our neighboring states of California, Idaho and
     Washington continue to experience similar fires and weather. The extended climate
     forecast in Oregon calls for continued warm and dry conditions, resulting in
     imminent threat of fire over a broad area of the state.

     The Oregon State Fire Marshal, Oregon Department of Forestry, and Oregon Fire
     Mutual Aid System are heavily engaged in initial and extended wildfire attacks
     with multiple incident management teams currently deployed throughout Oregon.
     Projected weather and new and existing fire threats elevate the need for access to
     state resources, including ground support and fire-fighting aircraft. This threat is
     not likely to recede in the near future. It is critically important that state resources,
     to include the Oregon National Guard, be prepared to expedite requested resources
     and respond effectively to these dangerous and dynamic conditions, if requested.

     Therefore, beginning on July 12, 2024, and continuing through October 1, 2024 |
     hereby declare a statewide State of Emergency due to the imminent and ongoing
     threat ofwildfire.

     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

         1.   The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM) shall support
              access to and use of personnel and equipment of all state agencies necessary
              to assess, alleviate, respond to, mitigate or recover from conditions caused
              by this emergency and to meet requests by the Oregon Department of
              Forestry, the Oregon Department of State Fire Marshal and/or county and
              tribal emergency management to support response needs to respond to, or
              mitigate the effects of, the wildfire emergency as described in the state’s
              Emergency Operations Plan. All Oregonians are to heed the direction of
              emergency officials with regard to this emergency in order to protect their
              safety.

                                                                                                 Hee

    Office of the Governor
    State of Oregon

         EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-13
         PAGE TWO

|           2. The Oregon National Guard will deploy, and redeploy, firefighting
                resources as needed and in accordance with Operations Plan Smokey 2024
|               throughout the remainder of fire season based upon threat and resource
|               shortfalls. All citizens are to heed the advice of emergency officials
|               regarding this emergency to protect their safety.
|

            3. This determination ofa wildfire emergency is statewide. It is not to be
               construed as a comprehensive declaration or proclamation of emergency for
               other purposes. It is limited to the use of state resources and personnel
               required by this emergency, to request assistance through the Emergency
               Management Assistance Compact if needed, and to allow state agencies to
               suspend provision of any order or rule of the agency if strict compliance
               with the provisions of the order or rule would in any way prevent, hinder, or
               delay mitigating the effects of the emergency. Any local government
               requests for state resources must be submitted through county governing
                bodies to OEM pursuant to ORS 401.

            4. Persons who believe they have been subjected to unconscionably excessive
               prices for essential consumer goods due to this emergency are requested to
               report violations to the Oregon Department ofJustice Consumer Protection
               Services, which has the authority to investigate unlawful trade practices.

         This emergency is effective as of 8:00 a.m. on July 12, 2024, shall exist until 11:59
         p.m. on October 1, 2024, unless extended or terminated sooner by the Governor.

         Done at Salem, Oregon, this 12" day of July, 2024.
