# Continuing Oregon Commission on Autism Spectrum Disorder (Executive Order 18-30)

## At a glance

Executive Order 18-30: Continuing Oregon Commission on Autism Spectrum Disorder. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 18-30

    CONTINUING OREGON COMMISSION ON AUTISM SPECTRUM
    DISORDER

    Oregon, like many states across the nation, has experienced a rapid increase in the
    number of individuals diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Both
    public and private systems have had difficulty meeting the demand for services to
    ensure that people experiencing autism receive the support they need to maximize
    their potential. There is continued need in Oregon for accurate, up-to-date
    information for families and professionals regarding early interventions, services,
    supports, and expected outcomes.

    In 2009, Executive Order 09-07 established the Oregon Commission on Autism
    Spectrum Disorder (“Commission”) and charged it with creating a ten-year
    strategic plan on ASD, to address these issues. In 2011, Executive Order 11-05
    renewed the Commission’s authority, so it could implement the strategic plan.
    There have been several notable achievements in the ensuing years, including the
    creation of an ASD specialization credential offered by the Oregon Teacher
    Standards and Practices Commission (TSPC), which has been used statewide by
    early childhood and school programs to assess and improve their use of evidence-
    based practices. Additionally, the criteria for identifying ASD in schools have been
    reformed to closely align with current research and medical models, improving
    accurate identification and reducing costs.

    It is imperative that the Commission continue its work for the benefit of all
    Oregonians. There exists an ongoing need to implement the existing strategic plan
    and develop a new three-year strategic plan, while assisting agencies and
    communities as they incorporate lessons learned through the Commission’s work.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

        1.   The Oregon Commission on Autism Spectrum Disorder shall continue its
             work as modified by this Executive Order.

      2.   The purpose of the Commission shall be to:

           a.   Develop and monitor implementation of both its existing strategic plan
                and the new three-year strategic plan mentioned below, incorporating
                biennial benchmarks for the state of Oregon to address the growing
                number of individuals with autism who require services from one or
                more programs or agencies;

           b.   Provide leadership and serve as a forum to establish priorities, create
                performance measures, facilitate collaboration, ensure support, and
                monitor outcomes;

           c.   Engage key stakeholders to develop, promote, and implement state plan
                goals and expected outcomes, and continue expanding and
                strengthening state and community partnerships among systems serving
                individuals with ASD;

           d,   Develop recommendations to the Governor for how agencies in the
                long-term would better integrate capacities of agencies and communities
                to support individuals with ASD and their families.

           e.   Advise the Governor and agency directors on plan implementations that
                require executive or agency approval and do not require legislative
                action;

           f.   Provide regular updates regarding the status of plan goals and outcomes
                to the Governor’s Office; and    —

           g.   If legislative action is needed, develop legislative concepts in
                anticipation of the next legislative session.

      3.   The commission shall submit a new three-year strategic plan for 2019-2022
           to the Governor no later than October 30, 2019. The strategic plan shall:

           a.   Propose goals and outcomes that respond to the emerging issues for
                individuals with ASD;

           b.   Clarify the array and structure of necessary services and support that
                enable persons with ASD to function to their individual potentials;

           c.   Make recommendations to increase coordination and collaboration of
                services while identifying other policies, capacity, and strategies to
                support the ASD community (including interagency agreements and
                braiding of funding);

           d.   Define strategies for accessing and promoting ASD best practice
                standards and research;

           e.   Estimate and make recommendations regarding funding necessary for
                services, support, and coordination as described in the strategic plan;

           f.   Incorporate biennial benchmarks for improving service and support to
                individuals with ASD; and

           g.   Recommend legislation necessary for implementation of the strategic
                plan.

      4.   The Commission shall consist of up to 17 members, who are knowledgeable
           about ASD and/or about systems that serve people with ASD. Members
           can serve for up to two terms; each term is two years long. Agency
           representatives do not have term limits and may assign an alternative
           representative from their agency to attend meetings. All members serve at
           the pleasure of the appointing authority. The Commission shall include:

           a.   Two members, appointed by the Governor, each of which is either a
                parent of an individual with ASD or an individual with autism;

           b.   One member who represents autism or disability advocacy
                organizations, appointed by the Governor;

           c. Two members who represent health care providers who are licensed to
                provide services to individuals with ASD, appointed by the Governor;

            All existing appointments to the Commission shall continue until the end
            date of the members’ current terms; current members shall continue on their
            staggered terms. Any subsequent reappointments shall be for two-year
            terms.

            The Governor shall appoint a Chair and Vice Chair of the Commission from
            the membership. The Chair shall establish an agenda for the Commission
            and provide leadership and direction. The Commission shall meet at least
            quarterly at the direction of the Chair or the Governor.

            The Commission may create subcommittees, as it deems necessary to carry
            out its scope and mission.

            The Director of the Department of Human Services, the Director of the
            Oregon Health Authority, and the Director of the Department of Education
            shall provide staff and support necessary to facilitate the activities of the
            Commission and its subcommittees, subject to availability of funds.

            The Oregon Council on Developmental Disabilities may accept, on behalf
            of the Commission, contributions of moneys and assistance from the United
            States government or its agencies, or from any other source, public or
            private as allowed by state and federal law.

      10.   After submission of its three-year strategic plan to the Governor, the
            Commission shall submit a report to the Governor and to the appropriate
            legislative committee. The report shall include but not be limited to:

            a.   An update on implementation and outcome priorities identified in the
                 three-year strategic plan and a summary of stakeholder engagement;

            b.   Recommendations for any legislation necessary for implementation of
                 the goals; and
