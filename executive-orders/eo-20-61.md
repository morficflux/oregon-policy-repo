# Implementing Law Enforcement Plan to Keep the Peace, Protect Free Speech, and Prevent Violence in the City of Portland (Executive Order 20-61)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER 20-61

    IMPLEMENTING LAW ENFORCEMENT PLAN TO KEEP THE PEACE,
    PROTECT FREE SPEECH, AND PREVENT VIOLENCE IN THE CITY OF
    PORTLAND

    Voting is currently underway here in Oregon and across the United States, with
    Election Day coming on November 3, 2020. I encourage all Oregon voters to make
    their voice heard by voting in the upcoming general election.

    With Election Day approaching, law enforcement anticipates that violence and
    unrest may occur in Portland that exceeds the City’s capacity.   Following the 2016
    election, Portland experienced multiple nights of rioting. Over the past several
    years, Portland has been the location of violent clashes between groups with
    opposing political beliefs. In the last several months, there have been acts of
    violence such as arson, assault, and even death on Portland’s streets during or
    following demonstrations. It is imperative that state and local leaders do
    everything they can during the coming days, to ensure that demonstrations and
    protests remain peaceful, so Oregonians can exercise their right to vote and
    peaceably assemble without fear.

    Pursuant to ORS 401.165 et seq., I find that there is an imminent risk of civil
    disturbance this coming week that threatens injury or possible loss of life, and find
    that the actions described below are necessary to address that imminent threat. A
    coordinated law enforcement response is necessary; this Executive Order
    effectuates that response.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    Pursuant to my emergency powers under ORS 401.165, ORS 401.168,
     ORS 401.175, and ORS 401.188, I hereby order as follows:

     le     Declaring an Emergency: For the reasons described above, and pursuant to
            ORS 401.165, I proclaim and declare a state of emergency to address and
            ensure appropriate coordination of resources to control anticipated civil
            disturbance in Multnomah County.

    2       Geographic Scope: The geographic scope of this emergency declaration is
            the county of Multnomah. This area will be referred to herein as the “law
            enforcement coordination area.”

     a    Duration: This Executive Order is effective from 5:00 p.m. on Monday,
          November 2, 2020, and expires at 5:00 p.m. on Wednesday, November 4,
          2020, unless extended or terminated earlier by the Governor. This period of
          time will be referred to herein as the “law enforcement coordination
          period.”

     4.   Law Enforcement / Incident Command:

          (a)    Law Enforcement: Pursuant to ORS 401.168, ORS 401.175(2) and
                 (5), and ORS 401.188, I hereby assume control of local law
                 enforcement (including the Portland Police Bureau, the Multnomah
                 County Sheriff’s Office, the Gresham Police Department, and the
                 Port of Portland Police), as necessary, for purposes of responding to
                 the events described above, for the duration of the law enforcement
                 coordination period. Any officers not required to respond to these
                 events, as determined by the incident commanders named in
                 paragraph 4(b) of this Executive Order, remain under local control.

          (b)    Unified Incident Command: Oregon State Police Superintendent
                 Terri Davie and Multnomah County Sheriff Michael Reese will
                 jointly serve as incident commanders in the law enforcement
                 coordination area for the law enforcement coordination period, and
                 will coordinate a unified incident response, consisting of the Oregon
                 State Police, the local law enforcement listed in paragraph 4(a) of
                 this Executive Order, and other law enforcement agencies in the law
                 enforcement coordination area during the law enforcement
                 coordination period.

    3).   Delegation of Additional Authorities: In addition to other authority vested
          by law, during the law enforcement coordination period I delegate to
          incident commanders Superintendent Davie and Sheriff Reese the authority
          to exercise the following authorities, if they determine it necessary:

          (a)    Road Closures and Restrictions: Pursuant to my authority under
                 ORS 401.175, the authority to close roads and highways in the law
                 enforcement coordination area, or to limit travel on such roads.

          (b)    Curfew: Pursuant to my authority under ORS 401.168, the authority
                 to declare a curfew in the law enforcement coordination area.
