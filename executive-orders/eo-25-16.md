# Declaration of State of Emergency Due to Imminent Threat of Wildfire (Executive Order 25-16)

## At a glance

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

   EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-16

   PROCLAMATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY DUE TO IMMINENT THREAT
   OF WILDFIRE

   Pursuant to ORS 401.165 ef seq., 1 find that the State of Oregon is in a critical statewide
   fire danger situation. We have experienced wildfires this season that resulted in multiple
   State conflagrations and federal Fire Management Assistance Grant (FMAG) declarations,
   community evacuations, threatened and damaged critical infrastructure, and destroyed
   homes and other structures. Our neighboring states of California, Idaho and Washington
   continue to experience similar fires and weather. The extended climate forecast in Oregon
   calls for continued warm and dry conditions, resulting in imminent threat of fire across the
   state.

   The Oregon Department of Forestry and Oregon Fire Mutual Aid System are heavily
   engaged in initial and extended wildfire attacks and Incident Management Teams are
   currently deployed in Oregon. The Oregon Department of Forestry and the Oregon
   Department ofthe State Fire Marshal have requested a statewide declaration of emergency
   to sustain the fire response effort as fire conditions continue to escalate across the state.
   New and existing fire threats elevate the need for access to state resources, including
   ground support and firefighting aircraft. This threat is not likely to recede in the near
   future. It is critically important that state resources like the Oregon National Guard be
   prepared to expedite requested resources and respond effectively to these dangerous and
   dynamic conditions, if requested.

   Therefore, | hereby declare a statewide State of Emergency due to the imminent and
   ongoing threat of wildfire.

   NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

       1. The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM) shall activate the
          State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and coordinate necessary
          access to use of personnel and equipment of all state agencies necessary to assess,
          alleviate, respond to, mitigate, and recover from conditions caused by this
          emergency. OEM shall coordinate all essential protective measures in collaboration
          with the Oregon Department of Forestry, and the Oregon Department of State Fire
          Marshal in support of identified disaster areas to protect lives, property and
          environment. All Oregonians are to heed the direction of emergency officials
            regarding this emergency to protect their safety.

Office of the Governor

   EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-16

     2:   In conformance with the Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and ORS
          401, state agencies are directed to provide resources and support as directed by
          OEM to assist in the response to this emergency. Any local or tribal government
          requests for state resources must be submitted through county or tribal governing
          bodies to the Oregon Department of Emergency Management.

      .   The Oregon National Guard will deploy, and redeploy, firefighting resources as
          needed and in accordance with Operations Plan Smokey 2025 throughout the
          remainder of fire season based upon threat and resource shortfalls.

          The Oregon Department of the State Fire Marshall is further authorized to assign
          fire-fighting forces and equipment on my behalf pursuant to ORS 476.520 in
          response to fire, a heightened danger of fire or a significant reduction in available
          fire-fighting resources during this emergency.

          This determination of a wildfire emergency is statewide. It is not to be construed as
          a comprehensive declaration or proclamation of emergency for other purposes. It is
          limited to the use of state resources and personnel for fire management required by
          the emergency, to request assistance through the Emergency Management
          Assistance Compact if needed, and to allow state agencies to suspend provision of
          any order or rule of the agency if compliance with the provisions ofthe order or
          rule would in any way prevent, hinder or delay mitigating or, recovering from, the
          effects of the wildfire emergency.

      . This order shall remain in effect until the threat is significantly relieved, or fire
        season ends, as determined by the Governor, or December 31, 2025, whichever
          occurs first.

          Done at Salem, Oregon, this 16" day of July, 2025.
