Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 18-01

    BUILDING OREGON’S COMMITMENT TO ADDICTION PREVENTION,
    TREATMENT, AND RECOVERY PRIORITIES, AND SETTING DEADLINES
    FOR STATUTORY REQUIREMENTS, AND DECLARING A PUBLIC
    HEALTH CRISIS

    WHEREAS, Oregonians struggle with addiction to alcohol or drugs every day; and

    WHEREAS, serving people struggling with addiction is a responsibility borne by
                                                                                              many
    state agencies; and

    WHEREAS, addiction is often considered to be an acute crisis but in reality is
                                                                                         a
    chronic, but treatable, disease; and

    WHEREAS, addiction treatment and recovery support services are unfortunately
    beyond reach for many Oregonians in need, especially for Oregonians in rural
                                                                                 areas;
    and

    WHEREAS, culturally sensitive addiction treatment and recovery support services
                                                                                               are
    not sufficiently available to historically underserved Oregonians; and

    WHEREAS, more than half of youth and adults prosecuted in the criminal justice
    system enter with addiction or a dual diagnosis; and

    WHEREAS, the criminal justice system should not be Oregon’s safety net for persons
    suffering from the disease of addiction; and

    WHEREAS, almost sixty percent of children in foster care have at least one parent with
    a substance use disorder; and

    WHEREAS, a person who suffers from addiction often requires low-barrier housing to
    achieve the safety and stability necessary for recovery; and

    WHEREAS, long-term employment can be difficult to sustain when an individual is in
    the throes of an addiction; and

    WHEREAS, the Alcohol and Drug Policy Commission (hereafter, the Commissi
                                                                                             on)
    was created to establish priorities and policies for alcohol and drug preventio
                                                                                       n and
    treatment as part of a strategic state plan to reduce addiction and alleviate
                                                                                    its impacts;
    and

    WHEREAS, the Commission is able to work across state agencies to create the state’s
    strategic plan and ensure that state resources are being efficiently utilized on effective
    programs; and

    WHEREAS, improved data collection and research are important components
                                                                                      of the
    Commission’s work; and

    WHEREAS, it is critical that all state agencies work with the Commission to ensure
    that they are able to implement a strategic plan;

    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    1.   I find that for the reasons recited above it is necessary to declare Alcohol and
         Substance Abuse Addiction a Public Health Crisis in Oregon.

    2.   The Oregon Health Authority, the Oregon Health Policy Board, the Department of
         Human Services, the Department of Corrections, the Oregon Youth Authority, the
         Youth Development Council, the Department of Housing and Community Services,
         the Department of Consumer and Business Services, and the Department of
         Education (hereinafter, the Agencies) shall work with the Commission to create a
         Statewide Addiction Prevention, Treatment, and Recovery Plan (hereinafter, the
         Plan), and shall provide resources as requested. The Plan shall include the
         recommendations required by Oregon Revised Statutes 430.242(2) and any other
         recommendations the Agencies and Commission deem critical to improving
         services for Oregonians struggling from addiction.

    3.   The Agencies shall provide a single point of contact who shall be empowered to
         respond to any inquiries of the Commission, and shall direct all employees to assist
         and respond when needed.

    4.   The Agencies shall enter into data-sharing agreements with the Commission to
                                                                                          the
         extent legally permissible. If barriers to those agreements arise, the Agency will
         identify the barriers and work to overcome them to the extent practicable, for
         instance by exploring changes of policy or legislation.

    5.   With the support of the above-listed Agencies, the Commission shall:

         a.   Enter into a Memorandum of Understanding with the Oregon Health Authority
              and other relevant agencies to ensure continued funding and support of the
              Commission for at least the remainder of the biennium no later than April 1,
              2018.
