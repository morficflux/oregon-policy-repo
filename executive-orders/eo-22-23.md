# Declaration of Emergency Due to Surge in Pediatric Respiratory Infections in Oregon (Executive Order 22-23)

## At a glance

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-23

    DECLARATION OF EMERGENCY DUE TO SURGE IN PEDIATRIC
    RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS IN OREGON

    ORS 401.165 ef seg. empowers the Governor to declare a state of emergency upon
    determining that an emergency has occurred or is imminent. Pursuant to that
    authority, I find that the spread of respiratory viruses in children, including
    Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV), and the related strain on hospital capacity, has
    created a threat to public health and safety, and constitutes a statewide emergency
    under ORS 401.025(1).

    Oregon currently is experiencing a rapid increase in pediatric infections and
    hospitalizations from respiratory viruses including RSV, a common respiratory
    virus that spreads through virus-containing respiratory droplets produced from
    coughing and sneezing. Young children are especially susceptible to health effects
    from RSV, which may include severe infections such as bronchiolitis and
    pneumonia. Children under the age of two, and in particular infants under six
    months of age, are at increased risk of severe disease.

    According to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the United
    States is experiencing an early, elevated onset of respiratory disease incidence, as
    the result of RSV, influenza, SARS-CoV-2, and other respiratory viruses.
    Respiratory disease caused by RSV and other viruses is placing severe strain on
    pediatric hospital capacity in Oregon. Since Oregon’s RSV season onset on
    October 29, 2022, the pediatric hospitalization rate has more than tripled to 7.6
    hospitalizations per 100,000 children. Oregon is likely to exceed its previously
    recorded peak weekly hospitalization rate of 9.5 per 100,000 children imminently.
    The pediatric hospitalization rate is being driven by severe disease in infants under
    six months of age, who may require specialized pediatric care. Oregon’s limited
    number of pediatric intensive care unit beds have been heavily impacted, with few
    options to transfer patients out of state. Although hospital capacity is already
    severely strained, pediatric cases of RSV, influenza and other respiratory viruses
    are expected to continue to grow for many weeks.

    In order to ensure the state and local authorities have the resources needed to
    respond to pediatric respiratory infections, a declaration of emergency is necessary.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-23
    PAGE TWO

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED THAT:

    IF   The Oregon Health Authority and the state Public Health Director shall take
         all actions necessary and authorized under ORS 401.651 to 401.670, ORS
         433.443, and ORS 431A.015 to respond to, control, mitigate, and recover
         from the emergency, including but not limited to:

         (a)     Deploying emergency volunteer health care professionals under
                 ORS 401.661;

         (b)     Designating emergency health care centers under ORS 401.657.

         If needed, I will request assistance through the Emergency Management
         Assistance Compact under ORS 402.105 and the Pacific Northwest
         Management Assistance Arrangement under ORS 402.250.

         The Department of Emergency Management shall, in consultation with the
         Director of the Oregon Health Authority and state Public Health Director, if
         necessary, take any action authorized under ORS 433.441. All state
         government agencies shall utilize and employ state personnel, equipment,
         and facilities for any and all activities as requested and coordinated by the
         Department of Emergency Management.

         As necessary to respond to the emergency, I authorize all executive
         agencies of state government to take, upon further direction from me or my
         office, any actions authorized under the provisions set forth in ORS 401.168
         through ORS 401.192.

         State agencies shall develop and implement procedures, including waiving
         rules or adopting temporary rules within the agency’s authority, consistent
         with recommendations from the state Public Health Director, designed to
         prevent or alleviate the public health threat.

    To ensure that state and local authorities have access to resources during the RSV
    season, this state of emergency shall exist for sixteen weeks from the date of this
    Executive Order, expiring at 12:01 a.m. on March 6, 2023, unless extended or
    terminated earlier by the Governor.

    Done at Salem, Oregon this 14th day of November, 2022.
