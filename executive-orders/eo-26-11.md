# Determination of a State of Drought Emergency in Josephine and Wasco Counties (Executive Order 26-11)

## At a glance

Executive Order 26-11: Determination of a State of Drought Emergency in Josephine and Wasco Counties. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-11

     DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN
     JOSEPHINE AND WASCO COUNTIES.

     At the request of Josephine County (through Josephine County Board of
     Commissioners Resolution 2026-015, dated May 12, 2026) and Wasco County
     (through Wasco County Board of Commissioners Resolution 26-008, dated June 4,
     2026) and based on the recommendations of the Drought Readiness Council and input
     from the Water Supply Availability Committee dated June 17, 2026, and pursuant to
     ORS 536.740, | find that and moderate and severe drought conditions in Josephine
     County and moderate, severe and extreme drought conditions in Wasco County as
     well as the conditions described below will cause natural and economic disaster
     conditions in Josephine and Wasco Counties (collectively “Counties”).

         1.   Above normal temperature conditions, well below normal snowpack
              conditions, just below normal precipitation conditions and long-term
              precipitation deficits, below normal streamflow conditions, and drier-than-
              normal soil moisture conditions in Josephine County.

        2. Above normal temperature conditions, well below normal snowpack
           conditions, just below normal precipitation conditions, below normal
           streamflow conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions in
              Wasco County.

     Seasonal climate and drought outlooks indicate that drought conditions in the Counties
     are likely to persist. Drought is likely to have a significant economic impact on the
     agricultural, livestock, natural resource and recreational tourism sectors in the
     Counties.

     Conditions continue to be monitored by the State’s natural resource and public safety
     agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department, the Oregon Department
     of Emergency Management, the Oregon Office of the State Fire Marshal, and the
     Oregon Department of Forestry’s Fire Protection Division.

     Preparation and resiliency to drought are vital to the health and safety of persons,
     property, and the economic security ofthe citizens and businesses of these Counties.
     I, therefore, declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists and is likely to
     continue to exist in Josephine and Wasco Counties.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-11
     PAGE TWO

     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

       1        The Oregon Department ofAgriculture is directed to coordinate and
                provide assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought
                conditions and assist in agricultural recovery in the Counties.

       in,     The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
               Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water
               users in the Counties as the Department and Commission determine
               necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.

       Ill.   | The Oregon Water Resources Department is directed to seek information
                from the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife to help understand the
                impacts of water availability on Oregon’s fish and wildlife, as necessary
                and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.

       IV.    | The Oregon Department of Emergency Management is directed to
                coordinate and assist as needed with assessment and mitigation activities to
                address current and projected conditions in the Counties.

       V.       All other state agencies are directed to coordinate with the above agencies
                and provide appropriate state resources as needed to assist affected political
                subdivisions and water users in the Counties.

       VI.      This Executive Order expires on December 31, 2026.

                Done at Salem, Oregon, this 22" day ofJune, 2026.

                                                      8
                                                  Tina Kotek
                                                  GOVERNOR
