    Increasing the percentage of state-funded contract dollars awarded to COBID
    Certified Firms, paired with other equitable economic initiatives, will support
    Oregon’s efforts to improve entrepreneurial opportunities for minority-owned,
    women-owned, service-disabled-veteran-owned, and emerging small
                                                                             businesses,
    either as prime contractors or subcontractors. These efforts reflect Oregon’s
    commitment to oppose all forms of discrimination and demonstrate its intent to
    create an environment that supports economic growth in all sectors.

    It is imperative to maintain accurate data on the participation of COBID
                                                                             Certified
    Firms, track progress, and ensure that Oregon’s entrepreneurial opportunities are

    The policy announced in this Executive Order shall not result in any preferential -

                                                                           obtaining
    contracts with the State of Oregon.

    Section 3. Responsibilities.

    Governor’s Office of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion’s Policy Advisor for
    Economic and Business Equity:

        a)   The Governor’s Policy Advisor for Economic & Business Equity (Office of
             Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion), established by ORS 200.025—hereinafter
             referred to as the “Governor’s Policy Advisor”—shall monitor the progress
             of each agency on a quarterly basis and request annual reports from each
             agency for review. The Governor’s Policy Advisor will provide feedback
             on the progress of the goals and initiatives set by agency directors, and will
             be in communication on an ongoing basis to continue collaborative work in
             obtaining increased utilization of COBID Certified Firms.

        b)   The Governor’s Policy Advisor shall provide assistance to agency directors
             in the development of initiatives, and business or community engagement
             plans, as necessary.

        ¢)   The Governor’s Policy Advisor shall provide Executive Order guideline
                                                                                       s
             and report templates to agencies to ensure consistency in reporting.
