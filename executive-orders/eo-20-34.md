# Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Mosier Creek Fire in Wasco County (Executive Order 20-34)

## At a glance

Executive Order 20-34: Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Mosier Creek Fire in Wasco County. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20-34

    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    MOSIER CREEK FIRE IN WASCO COUNTY

    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:

    The fire known as the “Mosier Creek Fire” is burning in Wasco County.

    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Mosier Creek Fire
    are beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire
    protection was requested by Fire Chief Robert Palmer and the Fire Defense Board
    Chief for Wasco County. The State Fire Marshal concurs with that request.

    In accordance with ORS 476.510 et seg., | have determined that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to a fire known as the Mosier Creek Fire in Wasco
    County and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting
    personnel and equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency
    Conflagration Act.

    These findings were made at 6:20 p.m. on August 12, 2020, and I now confirm
    them with this Executive Order.

   _ NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    i      The Oregon State Police and the Office of State Fire Marshal shall mobilize
           fire resources statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense
           Chiefs for the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the
           Emergency Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources
           responding to Mosier Creek Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire
           Marshal.

    2.     This conflagration is declared only for the Mosier Creek Fire threatening
           structures in Wasco County.                 |

Oftice of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20-34
    PAGE TWO

    3,   This order was made by verbal proclamation at 6:20 p.m. the 12th day of
         August 2020 and signed this 14" day of August, 2020, in Salem, Oregon.

    4.   This order will remain in effect until the Office of State Fire Marshal has
         demobilized all OSFM resources off this incident.

         Done at Salem, Oregon, this 14" day of August, 2020.

                                                 Kit. Leow

                                                Kate Brown
