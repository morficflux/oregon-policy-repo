# Determination of State of Emergency in Multiple Counties Due to the Need for Statewide Coordination to Address Persistent Extreme Drought Conditions (Executive Order 22-10)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

   EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-10

   DETERMINATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN MULTIPLE
   COUNTIES DUE TO THE NEED FOR STATEWIDE COORDINATION TO
   ADDRESS PERSISTENT EXTREME DROUGHT CONDITIONS.

   Pursuant to ORS 401.165, I find a potential threat to life, health, and safety due to
   persistent extreme drought conditions that have caused and continue to cause a
   state of emergency in 17 Counties: Klamath, Morrow, Crook, Jefferson, Gilliam,
   Harney, Jackson, Deschutes, Grant, Lake, Malheur, Baker, Douglas, Wallowa,
   Wheeler, Union and Wasco. I anticipate that the geographic scope of this
   emergency will continue to expand during this calendar year, and the geographic
   scope of this emergency declaration will be expanded as additional counties declare
   county-level drought emergencies and seek state-level drought declarations
   pursuant to ORS 536.740.

   Beginning on or before March 2, 2022, and continuing, extreme drought conditions
   have and continue to cause private drinking water sources to run dry and many
   community and municipal water systems have historically low water supply and
   will likely require the implementation of curtailment measures. Reservoirs
   throughout Southern, Central, and Eastern Oregon are less than 25% full, with
   many stream flows substantially below average. The US Drought Monitor indicates
   88% of the state is experiencing drought with more than half of the state under
   extreme drought conditions. These conditions are likely to persist with a seasonal
   climate outlook that indicates a high likelihood for above average temperatures and
   below average precipitation in the Oregon counties covered by this order during the
   months of June, July, and August.

   Prolonged drought with little indication that relief is likely through the summer will
   continue to impact agriculture and industry and create a threat to health and life
   safety for communities without a consistent and stable source of clean drinking
   water or for critical emergency services like firefighting.

   Individual severe and continuing drought declarations pursuant to ORS 536.740
   have been provided to assist the 17 counties mentioned above. However, due to the
   geographic scope, duration, and severity of the drought emergency, more is needed,
   and this emergency declaration is intended to help provide an additional layer of
   state support. In particular, due to the scope of the emergency, it is imperative that
   the state provide a coordinated response to support the efforts of local governments
   and make tools and resources available to address the critical life safety needs of
   communities that are being impacted by this prolonged period of drought. This
   emergency declaration will facilitate that coordinated response.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-10
    PAGE TWO

    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED AND DIRECTED:

      Ik   Pursuant to ORS 401.165, I declare a state of emergency due to extreme
           drought. The geographic scope of the emergency includes all counties for
           which a declaration of drought emergency pursuant to ORS 536.740 has
           issued during calendar year 2022. This geographic scope will expand to
           include counties covered by any additional ORS 536.740 drought
           declarations issued by the Governor during calendar year 2022, as of the
           date of those declarations.

           The Office of Emergency Management (OEM) shall activate the State’s
           Emergency Operations Plan and shall coordinate access to and use of
           personnel and equipment of all state agencies necessary to assess, alleviate,
           respond to, mitigate or recover from threats to health and life safety caused
           by this emergency. OEM shall coordinate all essential protective measures
           in support of declared emergency areas to protect lives, property, and the
           environment.

           The Oregon Department of Human Service, Oregon Health Authority,
           Department of Agriculture, Water Resources Department, and other state
           agencies are directed to provide any assistance as requested by OEM that is
           deemed necessary to assist in the response to this emergency and to provide
           all necessary support to statewide response, recovery, and mitigation
           efforts.

           This determination of emergency is not to be construed as a comprehensive
           declaration or proclamation of emergency for other purposes. It is limited
           to the use of state resources and personnel required by this emergency in
           accordance with the State Emergency Management Plan, to request
           assistance through the Emergency Management Assistance Compact if
           needed, and to allow state agencies to suspend provisions of any order or
           rule of the agency if strict compliance with the provisions of the order or
           rule would in any way prevent, hinder or delay mitigating the effects of the
           drought emergency.

           Any local government requests for state resources must be submitted
           through county governing bodies to the Office of Emergency Management
           pursuant to ORS Chapter 401.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

           terminated sooner by the governor.
