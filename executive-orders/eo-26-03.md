# Third Extension of Executive Order 25-08 Declaring a State of Emergency in Wasco County due to the Rowena Wildfire (Executive Order 26-03)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-03

     THIRD EXTENSION OF EXECUTIVE ORDER 25-08 DECLARING A
     STATE OF EMERGENCY IN WASCO COUNTY DUE TO THE ROWENA
     WILDFIRE

     WHEREAS, On June 18, 2025, pursuant to ORS 401.165 ef seq., | found that a
     threat to life, safety, and property exists due to the wildfire that has caused a natural
     disaster in Wasco County and declared a state of emergency in Wasco County.

     WHEREAS, Beginning June 11, 2025, and continuing, severe wildfire rolled
     through Wasco County causing extensive damage to homes and critical
     infrastructure; threatening life, safety, and property resulting in displacement of
     personnel; and a loss of power and communications within the region.

     WHEREAS, On June 11, 2025, the fire resulted in a state conflagration and federal
     Fire Management Assistance Grant (FMAG) declarations, community evacuations,
     and threatened and damaged critical infrastructure, destroying homes and other
     structures. On June 18, 2025, the Wasco County Board of Commissioners declared
     a state of emergency due to the Rowena Wild fire and the threat to life, safety, and
     property that exists in Wasco County. Commission Order 25-048 further declared
     local resources were exhausted and immediate state and federal assistance was
     requested.

     WHEREAS, On July 16, 2025, the Wasco County Board of Commissioners
     extended the county declaration of a state of emergency due to the Rowena
     Wildfire. The order declared that the hazardous conditions resulting from the
     Rowena Fire, including but not limited to threats to life, property, and natural
     resources, continue to pose a significant risk to the health and safety of Wasco
     County residents. State and local agencies continue to respond to requests for mass         |
     care and hazardous debris removal in support of this emergency. Damage                      |
     assessment reports from this jurisdiction include major damage to homes and
     critical infrastructure resulting in significant hazardous debris posing a threat to life
     and safety.

     WHEREAS, On November 5, 2025 the Wasco County Board of Commissioners
     extended the county declaration of a state of emergency as hazardous conditions
     resulting from the Rowena  Fire remain  and continues to pose a significant risk to
     health and safety of Wasco County residents. State and local resources are still
     required to provide human services and hazardous debris removal assistance in
     support of this emergency.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-03
     PAGE TWO

     WHEREAS, Hazardous conditions resulting from the Rowena Fire remain and
     continues to pose a significant risk to the health of Wasco County residents and the
     need for continued local and state human services and debris removal technical
     assistance is still required.

     WHEREAS, Due to continuing need, it is anticipated that the Wasco County Board
     of Commissioners will vote to extend the county declaration of a state of
     emergency, and I find an extension is necessary.

     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

         1.   The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM) shall continue
              coordination under the State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management
              Plan for the use of personnel, equipment, technical expertise contracting       |
              authority of all state agencies necessary to alleviate, respond to, mitigate,
              and recover from conditions caused by this emergency.

         2.   In conformance with the Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and
              ORS 401, state agencies are directed to provide resources and support
              requested by OEM to assist in the response to this emergency. Any local
              government requests for state resources must be submitted through county
              governing bodies to the Oregon Department of Emergency Management
              pursuant to ORS chapter 401. The Oregon Department of Environmental
              Quality and other state agencies are directed to work expeditiously to
              provide any assistance as directed by OEM that is deemed necessary to
              assist in the response to and recovery from this emergency, remove barriers
              to recovery, and provide all necessary support to Wasco County response,
              recovery, and mitigation efforts.

         3. Persons who believe they have been subjected to unconscionably excessive
            prices for essential consumer goods due to this emergency are requested to
            report violations to the Oregon Department of Justice Consumer Protection
            Services, which has the authority to investigate unlawful trade practices.

         4. This extension is limited to Wasco County at this time.

         5. Executive Order 25-08 was made by verbal proclamation at 7:10 p.m. on
            June 18, 2025 and signed on June 20, 2025, in Salem, Oregon.

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-03

       6. This Executive Order supersedes and replaces 25-28 and the state of
          emergency is continued and shall exist and remain in effect until 11:59 p.m.,
          June 30, 2026, unless extended or terminated sooner by the Governor.

          Done at Salem, Oregon, this 29" day of January, 2026.
