# Extending Executive Order 20-61 (Executive Order 20-62)

## At a glance

Executive Order 20-62: Extending Executive Order 20-61. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER 20-62

    EXTENDING EXECUTIVE ORDER 20-61

    On November 2, 2020, in Executive Order 20-61, I declared a state of emergency
    for Multnomah County, to address and ensure appropriate coordination of law
    enforcement resources to respond to the threat of civil disturbance in the City of
    Portland.

    Following consultation with the incident commanders regarding the situation on the
    ground, I am extending the duration of the state of emergency until 5:00 p.m.,
    Friday, November 6, 2020. Accordingly, on page two of Executive Order 20-61,
    paragraph three is amended to provide as follows:

           Duration: This Executive Order is effective from 5:00 p.m. on Monday,
           November 2, 2020, and expires at 5:00 p.m. on Friday, November 6, 2020,
           unless extended or terminated earlier by the Governor. This period of time
           will be referred to herein as the “law enforcement coordination period.”

    Done at Salem, Oregon, this 4‘ day of November, 2020.

                                           Ket. Low
                                                  —
                                         Kate Brown
                                         GOVERNOR
