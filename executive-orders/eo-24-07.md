## Full text

    Office of the Governor
    State of Oregon

        EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-07

        DECLARING STATE OF EMERGENCY DUE TO FENTANYL USE IN
        PORTLAND CITY CENTER IN MULTNOMAH COUNTY

        A fentanyl use crisis exists across the nation and in Oregon. In Portland City
        Center ', fentany! use has precipitated a state of public emergency. This emergency
        exists due to injuries and deaths from fentanyl use; harms to people with unmet
        housing, treatment and health care needs; trauma to downtown residents who wish
        to avoid fentanyl; and economic and reputational harm to the City of Portland,
        Multnomah County, and the State of Oregon.

        The State, County, and City have expended significant resources attempting to
        address fentany] use through a variety of programs and approaches. These
        independent efforts have improved conditions but have not abated the fentany]
        emergency. Coordinated action is required to increase the effectiveness of separate
        programs that address the harms of fentanyl and polysubstance use, to ensure that
        all available resources are allocated in a coordinated way to achieve maximum
        benefit, and to reduce the harms that fentanyl use imposes on the Portland City
        Center

        Today the Multnomah County Chair and the Mayor of the City of Portland jointly
        requested a State of Emergency to address fentanyl use in Portland’s City Center.
|
|
        Pursuant to 401.165 et seq., I find that a state of emergency exists in Portland City
|
        Center due to the harms of widespread fentanyl use, and the need for a coordinated
        response.

        NOW THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED AND DIRECTED:

        A state of emergency exists in Portland City Center due to widespread fentanyl use.

            1.   Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM). OEM shall
                 activate the State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and
                 coordinate use of personnel and equipment of all state agencies necessary to
                 assess, alleviate, respond to, mitigate, or recover from this emergency.

|

        Downtown, and South Waterfront Neighborhoods, as defined by the Portland Bureau of Planning
        and Sustainability. It also includes up to 500’ around the perimeter of the mapped area. A
        reference map is posted at https://www.portland.gov/bps/documents/citywide-neighborhood-
