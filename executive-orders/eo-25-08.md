# Declaration of State of Emergency in Wasco County Due to the Rowena Wildfire (Executive Order 25-08)

## At a glance

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

   EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-08

   DECLARATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN WASCO COUNTY DUE
   TO THE ROWENA WILDFIRE

   Pursuant to ORS 401.165 ef seq., I find that a threat to life, safety, and property
   exists due to the wildfire that has caused a natural disaster in Wasco County.

   Beginning June 11, 2025, and continuing, severe wildfire rolled through Wasco
   County causing extensive damage to homes and critical infrastructure; threatening
   life, safety, and property resulting in displacement of personnel; and a loss of power
   and communications within the region. State and local agencies continue to
   respond to requests for mass care and medical services. Early damage assessment
   reports from this jurisdiction include major damage to homes and businesses
   resulting in significant debris and infrastructure damage.

   On June 11, 2025 the fire resulted in a state conflagration and federal Fire
   Management Assistance Grant (FMAG) declarations, community evacuations, and
   threatened and damaged critical infrastructure, destroying homes and other
   structures. On June 18, 2025, the Wasco County Board of Commissioners declared
   a state of emergency due to the Rowena Wildfire and the threat to life, safety, and
   property that exists in Wasco County. Commission Order 25-048 further declared
   local resources were exhausted and immediate state and federal assistance was
   requested.

   The Oregon Department of Forestry is engaged in initial and extended wildfire
   attack and Incident Management Teams are currently deployed in Oregon.

   NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

       1.   The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM) shall activate
            the State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and coordinate
            necessary access to use of personnel and equipment of all state agencies
            necessary to assess, alleviate, respond to, mitigate, and recover from
            conditions caused by this emergency. OEM shall coordinate all essential
            protective measures in collaboration with the Oregon Department of
            Forestry and Oregon Department of State Fire Marshal in support of
            identified disaster areas to protect lives, property and environment. All
            Oregonians are to heed the direction of emergency officials with regard to
            this emergency in order to protect their safety.

State of Oregon

      2. In conformance with the Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and
         ORS chapter 401, state agencies are directed to provide resources and
         support requested by OEM to assist in the response to this emergency.

           The Oregon Department of Human Services, Oregon Health Authority,
           Department of Environmental Quality and other state agencies are directed
           to work expeditiously to provide any assistance as directed by OEM that is
           deemed necessary to assist in the response to and recovery from this
           emergency, remove barriers to recovery, and to provide all necessary
           support to Wasco County response, recovery, and mitigation efforts.

           Persons who believe they have been subjected to unconscionably excessive
           prices for essential consumer goods due to this emergency are requested to
           report violations to the Oregon Department of Justice Consumer Protection
           Services, which has the authority to investigate unlawful trade practices.

      3.   This determination of a wildfire emergency is limited to Wasco County at
           this time. It is not to be construed as a comprehensive declaration or
           proclamation of emergency for other purposes. It is limited to the use of
           state resources and personnel required by the emergency, to request
           assistance through the Emergency Management Assistance Compact if
           needed, and to allow state agencies to suspend provision of any order or
           rule of the agency if compliance with the provision of the order or rule
           would in any way prevent, hinder, or delay mitigating the effects of the
           wildfire emergency. Any local government requests for state resources
           must be submitted through county governing bodies to the Oregon
           Department of Emergency Management pursuant to ORS chapter 401.

      4.   This order was made by verbal proclamation at 7:10 p.m. on June 18, 2025,
           and signed on June 20, 2025, in Salem, Oregon.

                                                                                        HE

     5. This state of emergency shall exist until 11:59 p.m., July 30, 2025, unless
        extended or terminated sooner by the Governor.
