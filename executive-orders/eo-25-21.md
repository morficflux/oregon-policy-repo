Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-21

     TOBACCO PREVENTION AND EDUCATION PROGRAM COUNCIL

     WHEREAS, Despite progress, tobacco use is still the leading cause of preventable
     death in Oregon, accounting for more than four times the number of deaths from
     motor vehicle accidents, suicide, AIDS, and homicide combined. Tobacco kills
     approximately 8,000 people each year and costs people in Oregon an estimated
     $5.7 billion a year in medical expenses and lost productivity.

     WHEREAS, The burden of tobacco use is not distributed equally. The tobacco
     industry has channeled higher levels of advertising, discounts and displays oftheir
     dangerous products toward people facing systemic racism and discrimination,
     people who are stressed or struggling, people with lower incomes, people living
     with mental illness or addiction, and youth. Consequently, there are
     disproportionate rates of tobacco-related illness and death.

     WHEREAS, The Oregon Tobacco Prevention and Education Program (TPEP) is a
     comprehensive program that works to decrease tobacco use across the state.
     Together with partners, TPEP works to improve policies and environments that
     prevent youth use, help adults quit, and counter targeted marketing by the tobacco
     industry.

     WHEREAS, TPEP has two governance bodies that provide external perspectives
     on how tobacco tax revenues should be allocated for tobacco prevention and
     cessation. These include the Tobacco Reduction Advisory Committee (TRAC)
     established by Executive Order 97-10 (EO 97-10) and the Ballot Measure 108
     Tobacco Tax Allocation Advisory Group.

     WHEREAS, EO 97-10 directed the TRAC to provide TPEP with
     recommendations and guidance on program goals, strategies, and desired
     outcomes. The TRAC is comprised of state agencies, health associations, the
     Coalition of Local Health Officials, and national advocacy organizations.

     WHEREAS, Oregon voters passed Ballot Measure 108 in 2020, which increased
     the cigarette tax resulting in an increase in state tobacco tax revenues. Ten percent
     of the tobacco tax was dedicated to funding for Tribes, community partners,
     Regional Health Equity Coalitions, and community-based organizations. In 2021,
     the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) convened an advisory group to provide
     recommendations on how to most equitably allocate a portion of the new tobacco
     tax revenues generated through Ballot Measure 108.

     WHEREAS, In order to improve efficiency and operate more effectively, the
     TRAC created by executive order and the Ballot Measure Advisory Committee
     convened by OHA should be integrated and expanded into a single body that
     represents all partners in Oregon’s tobacco prevention system. Formation of a
     single advisory body will assist OHA in its commitment to end health inequities
     and effectively and equitably decrease statewide tobacco use across all Oregon
     communities.

     NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AND DIRECTED:

         1. Executive Order 97-10 is rescinded, and therefore the Tobacco Reduction
            Advisory Committee is dissolved.

        2. The Ballot Measure 108 Advisory Committee is dissolved.

        3. Following the effective date of this Executive Order, OHA is directed to
           convene a Tobacco Prevention and Education Program Council (Council) to
           advise it on all tobacco-related programs. The OHA Director shall appoint
           members based on recommendations from OHA programs and partners
            involved in decreasing tobacco use.

        4. The members of the Council must include at least two representatives from
