## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-28

    DIRECTING COORDINATED AND TRANSPARENT IMPLEMENTATION
    OF OREGON’S COMMITMENTS TO THE COLUMBIA BASIN
    RESTORATION INITIATIVE AND THE RESILIENT COLUMBIA BASIN
    AGREEMENT

    WHEREAS, The Columbia River Basin and its iconic salmon and steelhead have
    been part of the ecological, cultural, economic and spiritual identity of Oregon
    since 1859, and the Native Peoples of the Pacific Northwest since time
    immemorial, and must remain so for future generations.

    WHEREAS, The State of Oregon recognizes the impacts of Columbia River dams
    on tribal communities and the need to strengthen sovereign collaboration in support
    of comprehensive and inclusive salmon restoration efforts across the Columbia
    River Basin system.

    WHEREAS, In 2023, the states of Oregon and Washington and the Yakama, Nez
    Perce, Umatilla and Warm Springs Tribes (the “Six Sovereigns”) came together to
    develop the Columbia Basin Restoration Initiative (“CBRI”) to chart a path
    forward to restore the salmon and steelhead fisheries and restore ecosystem
    function in the Columbia and Snake rivers.

    WHEREAS, On September 27, 2023 a Presidential Memorandum was issued
    establishing the federal policy to restore wild Columbia River Basin salmon and
    steelhead and other native fish to “healthy and abundant” levels, and to “...secure a
    clean and resilient energy future for the region...support local agriculture and its
    role in food security domestically and globally...and to invest in the communities
    that depend on the services provided by the Basin’s Federal dams to enhance
    resilience to...climate change.” The Presidential Memorandum directed all federal
    executive agencies to align with these goals and determine shortcomings in their
    ability to do so.

    WHEREAS, On December 14, 2023, the United States government, Six
    Sovereigns and the National Wildlife Federation thereafter entered into the
    “Resilient Columbia Basin Agreement” (RCBA), that stays ongoing litigation
    related to the operation of the Columbia River System for up to ten years to enable
    fulfillment of commitments, collaboration, and partnerships to advance the
    objectives of both the Presidential Memorandum and the CBRI.

    WHEREAS, The RCBA, coupled with the newly unified and cohesive leadership
    from the Six Sovereigns, represent a unique moment in time and unprecedented
    window of opportunity to resolve the salmon crisis and associated litigation while
    honoring treaties, rebuilding healthy and abundant wild fish runs, enhancing
    community and watershed resilience and adaptation to climate change, meeting
    regional energy and decarbonization goals, and ensuring continuity of services
    provided by the Columbia River.

    WHEREAS, It is my Administration’s policy to take full advantage of this window
    of opportunity through coordinated State leadership and support for full
    implementation of the CBR/ and the RCBA, and in doing so, to help complement
    other efforts by the State to meet our decarbonization and climate goals, secure a
    resilient, reliable, and affordable energy system, and to modernize our
    infrastructure. Oregon’s communities will benefit from these actions by being
    resilient and adaptive to climate change and will be best positioned for a prosperous
    economic future.

    NOW, THEREFORE, I, TINA KOTEK, Governor of the State of Oregon, by
    virtue of the power and authority vested in me by the Constitution and statutes of
    the State of Oregon, do hereby issue this order to become effectively immediately:

    IT IS HEREBY ORDERED THAT:

    Named executive branch entities shall take actions necessary within their existing
    authority, and identify necessary additional authorities they do not presently have,
    in support of fulfilling Oregon’s commitments to the CBRI and the RCBA.

    1.   This order applies to:
         a.   The following executive branch agencies (“Agencies,” or individually
              “Agency”:
              e   Department of Fish and Wildlife
              e   Department of Environmental Quality
              e   Water Resource Department
              e   Oregon Watershed Enhancement Board
              e   Department of Energy
              e   Department of Agriculture

         a. No later than February 1, 2025, each Agency must provide a written report

            i.        Identify and describe all program commitments that identify the Agency,

           ii.       | Describe the agency’s existing statutory and administrative authority

                      subsection 1;
          ili.       | Describe any gaps in statutory or administrative (rule) authority that
                       would be necessary to accelerate full and effective program
                      implementation;
          iv.         Identify opportunities within existing programs and authorities to
                      accelerate effective program implementation; and
           v.         Identify any resources the Agency currently has to implement the CBR
                      and RCBA and all resources and funding the Agency does not currently
                      have but would need to accelerate effective CBR/ and RCBA
                      implementation.
         b. No later than March 15, 2025, and no less than twice annually, the Agencies
            must convene with the Governor’s office to review coordination among
            Oregon, the USG, and the Six Sovereigns for the purpose of assessing their
            work to achieve and accelerate full implementation of the CBR/ and RCBA
            and to recommend to the Governor any actions needed to optimize the
                 State’s participation in the CBR/J and RCBA.
         c. No later than June 1, 2025, and annually thereafter, each Agency will report
            to their Board or Commission on the Agency’s implementation of the CBR/
            and RCBA, including identifying opportunities to enhance the agency’s role
                 in CBRI and RCBA implementation.
         d. No later than November 1, 2025, and annually thereafter, the Agencies and
            the Governor’s office will convene a meeting with the Six Sovereigns to
            discuss ongoing implementation efforts and opportunities for collaboration

              to optimize implementation by Oregon and to establish a clear partnership

    3.   The Oregon representatives to the NWPCC will:
         a.   Coordinate and collaborate with other members and staff of the NWPCC to

    4. Transparency and collaboration must be recognized as important to successfully
       accomplishing the goals and objectives of the Six Sovereigns’ CBRI and the
       federal commitments of the RCBA. Agencies subject to this order will develop
       and implement a coordinated approach to undertake the following consistent
       with this spirit and incorporated into reporting requirements of this Executive
         Order:
         a.   For Oregon energy, transportation, agriculture, water and recreation sectors
              potentially impacted by Lower Snake River restoration associated with the
              CBRI and RCBA, seek input and collaboration from these sectors to help
              ensure efforts moving forward are consistent with the CBR/ objectives to:
                      » Ensure continuity of services provided by the lower Snake River
                         dams are secured prior to dam breaching;
                     »   Ensure that efforts to secure those replacement services
                         minimize additional rate pressures and financial burdens on
                         potentially impacted sectors;
                     =   Ensure collaboration with sovereigns and stakeholders to secure
                         the necessary Federal investments for lower Snake River
                         restoration and salmon and steelhead recovery, in a manner that
                         does not disproportionately burden any single sector;
                     =   Ensure advancing the CBRI and associated RCBA are
                         complementary to, and help leverage resources toward, an
                         equitable clean energy transition; a reliable, resilient and
                         affordable energy system; and statewide efforts to modernize
                         and enhance transportation, water and climate security.

        b. For Oregon transportation, fishery, tourism and economic development
           sectors, seek stakeholder input and collaboration regarding opportunities to
           optimize and integrate how the CBR/ and RCBA activities approach
           restoring fish runs, enhancing natural functioning watershed conditions, and
           enhancing resilience and adaptation of the basin to impacts arising from

        c. Incorporate appropriate input from these collaborations with Oregon

           signatories to the CBR/ and RCBA, as well as the Regional Energy Needs

    EXPIRATION: The RCBA stays litigation for up to 10 years (December 2033)
    subject to specific terms for withdrawal by non-federal parties. This Executive
    Order will be reconsidered at the end of the 10-year stay, or at the time any non-
    Federal party withdraws from the MOU agreement and/or the RCBA is terminated.
    The duration of this Executive Order is to be guided by its intent to affirm and
    promote full implementation of the CBRI until salmon and steelhead are restored to
    healthy and abundant numbers.

    Done at Salem, Oregon, this 27" day of September, 2024.
