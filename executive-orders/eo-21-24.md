# Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Elbow Creek Fire in Wallowa County (Executive Order 21-24)

## At a glance

Executive Order 21-24: Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Elbow Creek Fire in Wallowa County. (Title as printed in the Governor's office listing of record; see Provenance.)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 21-24

    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    ELBOW CREEK FIRE IN WALLOWA COUNTY

    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:

    The fire known as the “Elbow Creek Fire” is burning in Wallowa County.

    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Elbow Creek Fire
    are beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire
    protection was requested by the Wallowa County Fire Defense Board Chief and the
    State Fire Marshal concurs with that request.

    In accordance with ORS 476.510-610, I have determined that a threat to life, safety,
    and property exists due to the fire known as the Elbow Creek Fire in Wallowa and
    the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting personnel and
    equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency Conflagration Act.

    These findings were made at 4:26 pm on July 16, 2021, with the Office of the State
    Fire Marshal, and I now confirm them with this Executive Order. The Office of the
    State Fire Marshal Blue Incident Management Team will be briefed at 5:00 pm on
    July 17, 2021, and will be entering into a unified command with an Oregon
    Department of Forestry Incident Management Team immediately thereafter.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    IE     The Oregon State Police and the Office of State Fire Marshal shall mobilize
           fire resources statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense
           Board Chiefs for the use ofpersonnel and equipment in accordance with the
           Emergency Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources
           responding to the Elbow Creek Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire
           Marshal.

    A,     This conflagration is declared only for the Elbow Creek Fire threatening
           structures in Wallowa County.

    Go     This order was made by verbal proclamation at 4:26 pm on the 16th day of
           July 2021, and signed this 17" day of July 2021, in Salem, Oregon.
