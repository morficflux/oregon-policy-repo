# Expanding Declaration Of Emergency Due To Surge In Respiratory Infections In Oregon; Repealing and Replacing Executive Order 22-23 (Executive Order 22-24)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-24

    EXPANDING DECLARATION OF EMERGENCY DUE TO SURGE IN
    RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS IN OREGON; REPEALING AND
    REPLACING EXECUTIVE ORDER 22-23

    ORS 401.165 ef seg. empowers the Governor to declare a state of emergency upon
    determining that an emergency has occurred or is imminent. Pursuant to that
    authority, on November 13, 2022, in Executive Order 22-23, I declared an
    emergency due to the spread of respiratory viruses in children, including
    Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV), and the related strain on pediatric hospital
    capacity.

    Since that time, the conditions described in Executive Order 22-23 have persisted
    and have continued to deteriorate due to ongoing surges in respiratory infections,
    including but not limited to RSV, Influenza, and COVID-19 in Oregon, and now,
    adult hospital capacity, in addition to pediatric hospital capacity, are both critically
    strained.

    In addition to those findings described in Executive Order 22-23, which continue,
    the following conditions have emerged:

        e   RSV pediatric hospitalizations have surpassed previous records, with more
            than 90 pediatric hospital admissions during the week ending on November
            ID, 2O22X,
        e   Influenza hospitalization rates are rising rapidly with anticipated rapid
            increases sustained over many weeks. These hospitalizations will
            disproportionately impact young children, elderly and pregnant adults, and
            communities of color and tribal communities.
        e   COVID-19 hospitalizations have seen steep increases, returning to levels
            unseen since July 2022, and hospitalizations are modeled to continue to
            increase through at least mid-December.
        e   Statewide borders in emergency rooms (people who are being treated in
            Emergency Rooms for lack of an available inpatient hospital bed to which
            they can be transferred) are at record levels.
        e   Statewide overall hospital bed vacancy rates and intensive care unit vacancy
            rates are each below 5%.

                                                                                               He

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-24
    PAGE TWO

    In order to ensure the state and local authorities have the resources needed to
    respond to both adult and pediatric respiratory infections, I find it necessary to
    expand the declaration of emergency I made in Executive Order 22-23.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED THAT:

        1.   Pursuant to ORS 401.165 et seq., I find that the spread of respiratory viruses
             in children and adults in Oregon, including but not limited to Respiratory
             Syncytial Virus (RSV), Influenza, and COVID-19, and related strain on
             hospital capacity, has created a threat to public health and safety, and
             constitutes a statewide emergency under ORS 401.025(1). This declaration
             continues and expands the emergency declaration made in Executive Order
             22723;

       2.    The Oregon Health Authority and the state Public Health Director shall take
             all actions necessary and authorized under ORS 401.651 to 401.670, ORS
             433.443, and ORS 431A.015 to respond to, control, mitigate, and recover
             from the emergency, including but not limited to:

             (a)      Deploying emergency volunteer health care professionals under
                      ORS 401.661;

             (b)      Designating emergency health care centers under ORS 401.657.

       3.    Ifneeded, I will request assistance through the Emergency Management
             Assistance Compact under ORS 402.105 and the Pacific Northwest
             Management Assistance Arrangement under ORS 402.250.

       4.    The Department of Emergency Management shall, in consultation with the
             Director of the Oregon Health Authority and state Public Health Director, if
             necessary, take any action authorized under ORS 433.441. All state
             government agencies shall utilize and employ state personnel, equipment,
             and facilities for any and all activities as requested and coordinated by the
             Department of Emergency Management.

                                                                                              HE

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-24
    PAGE THREE

       5.   As necessary to respond to the emergency, I authorize all executive
            agencies of state government to take, upon further direction from me or my
            office, any actions authorized under the provisions set forth in ORS 401.168
            through ORS 401.192.

       6.   State agencies shall develop and implement procedures, including waiving
            rules or adopting temporary rules within the agency’s authority, consistent
            with recommendations from the state Public Health Director, designed to
            prevent or alleviate the public health threat.

       7.   Executive Order 22-23 is repealed and replaced with this Executive Order.

       8.   Nothing in this Executive Order is intended to exempt employers from the
            requirements of any collective bargaining agreements they may have
            entered into. To the extent practicable, this Executive Order should be
            interpreted consistently with the requirements of any applicable and existing
            collective bargaining agreements.

    To ensure that state and local authorities have access to resources during the RSV
    season, this state of emergency shall exist until 12:01 a.m. on March 6, 2023,
    unless extended or terminated earlier by the Governor.

    Done at Salem, Oregon this 7 day of December, 2022.

                                             Kate Leeuw
