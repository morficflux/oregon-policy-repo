# Determination of State of Emergency in Umatilla, Union and Wallowa Counties due to a Severe Storm that includes Heavy Rain, Snowmelt, Flooding and Landslides (Executive Order 20-01)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20-01

    DETERMINATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN UMATILLA, UNION
    AND WALLOWA COUNTIES DUE TO A SEVERE STORM THAT
    INCLUDES HEAVY RAIN, SNOWMELT, FLOODING, AND
    LANDSLIDES.

    Pursuant to ORS 401,165, I find a potential threat to life, safety, property, and
    significant damage to infrastructure exists due to severe storms that have caused
    and continue to cause a disaster throughout the state. Beginning February 6, 2020,
    and continuing, these severe storms are causing flooding, increased snowmelt,
    landslides, and erosion at various locations throughout Northeast Oregon, resulting
    in persons needing rescue, damage to homes, critical transportation failures, loss of
    power and communications capabilities, and emergency mass care needs,

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

        1.   The Office of Emergency Management (OEM) shall activate the State’s
             Emergency Operations Plan and Emergency Coordination Center, and shall
             coordinate access to and use of personnel and equipment of all state
             agencies necessary to assess, alleviate, respond to, mitigate or recover from
             conditions caused by this emergency. OEM shall coordinate all essential
             protective measures in support of identified disaster areas to protect lives,
             property, and the environment.

       2.    OEM shall work with neighboring states where needed to ensure
             coordinated response and mitigation actions.

       3.    The Oregon National Guard, Oregon Department of Transportation, Oregon
             State Police, Oregon Water Resources Department, Oregon Department of
             Human Services, and Oregon Health Authority provide any assistance that
             is deemed necessary to assist in the response to this emergency and to
             provide all necessary support to statewide response, recovery, and
             mitigation efforts. All other state agencies shall be prepared to assist as
             requested by OEM.

       4.    The Oregon Department of Transportation shall provide appropriate
             assistance and seek federal resources to effect repair and reconstruction of
             damage and impacts to the federal highway system in above identified
             jurisdictions.

       5.    This state of emergency shall exist for thirty days unless terminated sooner
             by the governor.

                                                                                             HE
                                                                                             §

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20-01
    PAGE TWO

    This order was made by verbal proclamation at 1:05 PM this 7" day of February,
    2020 and signed this 1 ot tay of February, 2020 in Salem, Oregon.

                                                 Kut Jem —
                                               Kate Brown
                                               GOVERNOR

                                               ATTEST:

                                               Bev Clarno
                                               SECRETARY OF STATE
