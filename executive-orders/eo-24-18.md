# Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Durkee Fire in Baker County (Executive Order 24-18)

## At a glance

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-18

     INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
     DURKEE FIRE IN BAKER COUNTY

     Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, | find that:

     The fire known as the “Durkee Fire” is burning in Baker County. The Durkee Fire
     is now combined with Cow Valley Fire burning in Malheur County.

     The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Durkee Fire are
     beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire protection
     was requested by the Baker Fire Defense Board and the State Fire Marshal concurs
     with that request.

     In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
     safety, and property exists due to the fire known as the Durkee Fire in Baker
     County and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting
     personnel and equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency
     Conflagration Act.

     These findings were made at 11:13 a.m. on July 20, 2024, with the Department of
     the State Fire Marshal, the team will be working with the resources at Cow Valley
     Fire in Malheur County. I now confirm them with this Executive Order.

     NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

     iE     The Department ofthe State Fire Marshal shall mobilize fire resources
            statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense Board Chiefs for
            the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the Emergency
            Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources responding to
            the Durkee Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire Marshal.

     2.     This conflagration is declared only for the Durkee Fire threatening
            structures in Baker County.

     3,     This order was made by verbal proclamation at 11:13 am on the 20th day of
            July 2024, and signed this 22" day ofJuly 2024, in Salem, Oregon.
