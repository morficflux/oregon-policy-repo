# Invocation of Emergency Conflagration Act for the Sturgill Fire in Wallowa County (Executive Order 22-19)

## Full text

Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-19

    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    STURGILL FIRE INWALLOWA COUNTY

    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:

    The fire known as the “Sturgill Fire” is burning in Wallowa County.

    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from Sturgill Fire are
    beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire protection
    was requested by the Wallowa County Fire Defense Board Chief and the State Fire
    Marshal concurs with that request.

    In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to the fire known as the Sturgill Fire in Wallowa
    County and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting
    personnel and equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency
    Conflagration Act.

    These findings were made at 7:02 p.m. on September 5, 2022. The Office of State
    Fire Marshal Green Incident Management Team is currently in place to manage the
    Double Creek Fire. They will also enter into unified command for the Sturgill Fire.
    I now confirm these findings with this Executive Order.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    Ile    The Oregon State Police and the Office of State Fire Marshal shall mobilize
           fire resources statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense
           Board Chiefs for the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the
           Emergency Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources
           responding to the Sturgill Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire
           Marshal.

    2:     This conflagration is declared only for the Sturgill Fire threatening
           structures in Wallowa County.

    ar     This order was made by verbal proclamation at 7:02 p.m. on September 5,
           2022, and signed on September 6, 2022, in Salem, Oregon.
