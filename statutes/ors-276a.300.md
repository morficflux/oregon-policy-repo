# Information systems security in executive department; rules (ORS 276A.300)

## At a glance

**[SUMMARY]** The statutory root of Oregon's executive-branch information security
program: it gives the State Chief Information Officer responsibility and authority over
information systems security in the executive department and requires the State CIO to
establish a state information systems security plan with associated standards, policies,
and procedures. Everything in the DAS/EIS security stack — OAR 128-030 (formerly
125-800), policy 107-004-052, and the Statewide IT Control Standards — rests on this
section.

## Key provisions

### Definitions (subsection 1)

> **[VERBATIM]** "“Information systems” means computers, hardware, software, storage
> media, networks, operational procedures and processes used in collecting, processing,
> storing, sharing or distributing information within, or with any access beyond ordinary
> public access to, the state's shared computing and network infrastructure."

**[SUMMARY]** "Executive department" takes its meaning from ORS 174.112. (Source:
subsection (1)(a).)

### State CIO authority and the security plan (subsection 2)

> **[VERBATIM]** "The State Chief Information Officer has responsibility for and authority
> over information systems security in the executive department, including responsibility
> for taking all measures that are reasonably necessary to protect the availability,
> integrity or confidentiality of information systems or the information stored in
> information systems. The State Chief Information Officer shall, after consultation and
> collaborative development with agencies, establish a state information systems security
> plan and associated standards, policies and procedures."

**[SUMMARY]** The plan must align with and support the Enterprise Information Resources
Management Strategy described in ORS 276A.203. Subsection (3) lets the State CIO
coordinate with DAS to review and verify the security of agency information systems.
(Source: subsections (2)–(3).)
