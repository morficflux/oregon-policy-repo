# Right to donate blood (ORS 109.670)

## At a glance

ORS 109.670 — Right to donate blood. Chapter 109 (Chapter 109), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

109.670 Right to donate blood.

(1) Any person 16 years of age or over may donate blood to any blood program without obtaining permission of a parent or guardian.

(2) As used in subsection

(1) of this section, “blood program” means any voluntary and noncompensatory program for the drawing of blood which is approved by the American Association of Blood Banks or the American Red Cross. [1977 c.533 §1] Note: 109.670 was enacted into law by the Legislative Assembly but was not added to or made a part of ORS chapter 109 or any series therein by legislative action. See Preface to Oregon Revised Statutes for further explanation.
