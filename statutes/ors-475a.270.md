# Duty to request land use compatibility statement (Licensees in General) (ORS 475A.270)

## At a glance

ORS 475A.270 — Duty to request land use compatibility statement (Licensees in General). Chapter 475A (Chapter 475A), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

475A.270 Duty to request land use compatibility statement.

(1) Prior to receiving a license under ORS 475A.290 or 475A.305, an applicant shall request a land use compatibility statement from the city or county that authorizes the land use. The land use compatibility statement must demonstrate that the requested license is for a land use that is allowable as a permitted or conditional use within the given zoning designation where the land is located. The Oregon Health Authority may not issue a license if the land use compatibility statement shows that the proposed land use is prohibited in the applicable zone.

(2) Except as provided in subsection

(3) of this section, a city or county that receives a request for a land use compatibility statement under this section must act on that request within 21 days of: (a) Receipt of the request, if the land use is allowable as an outright permitted use; or (b) Final local permit approval, if the land use is allowable as a conditional use.

(3) A city or county that receives a request for a land use compatibility statement under this section is not required to act on that request during the period that the authority discontinues licensing those premises pursuant to ORS 475A.718 (4).

(4) A city or county action concerning a land use compatibility statement under this section is not a land use decision for purposes of ORS chapter 195, 196, 197, 197A, 215 or 227. [2021 c.1 §19] (Licensees in General)
