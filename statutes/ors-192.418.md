# Effect of failure of Attorney General, district attorney or elected official to take timely action on inspection petition (ORS 192.418)

## At a glance

ORS 192.418 — Effect of failure of Attorney General, district attorney or elected official to take timely action on inspection petition. Chapter 192 (Records; Reports and Meetings), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

192.418 Effect of failure of Attorney General, district attorney or elected official to take timely action on inspection petition.

(1) The failure of the Attorney General or district attorney to issue an order under ORS 192.401, 192.411 or 192.415 denying, granting, or denying in part and granting in part a petition to require disclosure within seven days from the day of receipt of the petition shall be treated as an order denying the petition for the purpose of determining whether a person may institute proceedings for injunctive or declaratory relief under ORS 192.401, 192.411 or 192.415.

(2) The failure of an elected official to deny, grant, or deny in part and grant in part a request to inspect or receive a copy of a public record within seven days from the day of receipt of the request shall be treated as a denial of the request for the purpose of determining whether a person may institute proceedings for injunctive or declaratory relief under ORS 192.401, 192.411 or 192.415.
