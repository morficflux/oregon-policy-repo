## Full text

431A.890 Prescription Monitoring Program Advisory Commission; purposes; members.

(1) The Prescription Monitoring Program Advisory Commission is created for the purposes of: (a) Studying issues related to the prescription monitoring program established under ORS 431A.855; (b) Reviewing the program’s annual report and making recommendations to the Oregon Health Authority regarding the operation of the program; and (c) Developing criteria used to evaluate program data.

(2) The commission shall consist of 12 members appointed by the authority as follows: (a) A person nominated by the Pain Management Commission; (b) A person who dispenses controlled substances nominated by an association representing pharmacists; (c) A practicing dentist nominated by an association representing dentists; (d) A practicing doctor of medicine nominated by an association representing doctors of medicine; (e) A practicing doctor of osteopathic medicine nominated by an association representing osteopathic physicians and surgeons; (f) A nurse authorized to prescribe controlled substances nominated by an association representing nurses; (g) A practicing naturopathic physician nominated by an association representing naturopathic physicians; (h) A practicing optometrist, nominated by an association representing optometrists; (i) A representative of the authority with expertise in administering addiction services; and (j) Three members of the public, one of whom must be an expert in information technology. [Formerly 431.976; 2017 c.409 §13; 2023 c.438 §7]
