## At a glance

ORS 164.045 — Theft in the second degree. Chapter 164 (Chapter 164), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

164.045 Theft in the second degree.

(1) A person commits the crime of theft in the second degree if: (a) By means other than extortion, the person commits theft as defined in ORS 164.015; and (b) The total value of the property in a single or aggregate transaction is $100 or more and less than $1,000.

(2) Theft in the second degree is a Class A misdemeanor. [1971 c.743 §124; 1987 c.907 §3; 1993 c.680 §19; 2009 c.11 §12; 2009 c.16 §2] 164.050 [Repealed by 1965 c.253 §153]
