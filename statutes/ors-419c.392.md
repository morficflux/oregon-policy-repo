# Effect of findings (Restorative Services) (ORS 419C.392)

## At a glance

ORS 419C.392 — Effect of findings (Restorative Services). Chapter 419C (Chapter 419C), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

419C.392 Effect of findings.

(1) If the court finds that the youth is fit to proceed, the court shall vacate the stay under ORS 419C.378.

(2) If the court finds that the youth is unfit to proceed and that there is not a substantial probability that the youth will gain or regain fitness to proceed in the foreseeable future if provided restorative services under ORS 419C.396, the court shall: (a) Immediately enter a judgment that dismisses the petition alleging jurisdiction under ORS 419C.005 without prejudice; or (b) If necessary for planning or instituting an alternative proceeding, then not more than five days after the findings are made enter a judgment that dismisses the petition without prejudice. (3)(a) If the court finds that the youth is unfit to proceed and that there is a substantial probability that the youth will gain or regain fitness to proceed in the foreseeable future if provided restorative services under ORS 419C.396, the court shall continue the order under ORS 419C.378 staying the proceedings and order that the youth receive restorative services under ORS 419C.396. (b) The court shall forward the order for restorative services to the Oregon Health Authority. (c) Unless otherwise specifically ordered, the court’s order for restorative services does not commit the youth to the custody of the authority or alter the guardianship of the youth. [2013 c.709 §8; 2024 c.96 §10] Note: See note under 419C.378. (Restorative Services)
