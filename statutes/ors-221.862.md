# “Historic ghost town” defined (ORS 221.862)

## At a glance

ORS 221.862 — “Historic ghost town” defined. Chapter 221 (Organization and Government of Cities), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

221.862 “Historic ghost town” defined. As used in ORS 221.862 to 221.872, “historic ghost town” means an incorporated city within this state that:

(1) Is on land acquired under a United States patent;

(2) Does not have a sufficient number of registered electors permanently residing within the city to fill all offices provided for under its charter; and

(3) Is of historic interest. [1983 c.355 §1] Note: 221.862 to 221.872 were enacted into law by the Legislative Assembly but were not added to or made a part of ORS chapter 221 or any series therein by legislative action. See Preface to Oregon Revised Statutes for further explanation. 221.865 [1955 c.475 §2; renumbered 190.550]
