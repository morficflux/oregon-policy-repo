# Authority of Governor to enter into agreements with other states for crime prevention purposes (Exclusion from Public Property) (ORS 131.685)

## At a glance

ORS 131.685 — Authority of Governor to enter into agreements with other states for crime prevention purposes (Exclusion from Public Property). Chapter 131 (Preliminary Provisions; Limitations; Jurisdiction; Venue; Criminal Forfeiture; Crime Prevention), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

131.685 Authority of Governor to enter into agreements with other states for crime prevention purposes. The Governor of Oregon may enter into agreements or compacts with the Governor of any or all the States of Washington, Idaho, California and Nevada, each acting on behalf of the own state of the Governor, in order to effectuate cooperative effort and mutual assistance in the prevention of crime in those states and in the enforcement of their respective criminal laws and policies. [Formerly 145.060] (Exclusion from Public Property)
