# Discovery of property, writings and information (ORS 114.425)

## Full text

114.425 Discovery of property, writings and information.

(1) The court may order any person to appear and give testimony by deposition if it appears probable that the person: (a) Has concealed, secreted or disposed of any property of the estate of a decedent; (b) Has been entrusted with property of the estate of a decedent and fails to account therefor to the personal representative; (c) Has concealed, secreted or disposed of any writing, instrument or document pertaining to the estate; (d) Has knowledge or information that is necessary to the administration of the estate; or (e) As an officer or agent of a corporation, has refused to allow examination of the books and records of the corporation that the decedent had the right to examine.

(2) If a person cited as provided in subsection

(1) of this section fails to appear or to answer questions asked as authorized by the order of the court, the person is in contempt and may be punished as for other contempts. [1969 c.591 §139; 1979 c.284 §106] 114.430 [Repealed by 1969 c.591 §305]
