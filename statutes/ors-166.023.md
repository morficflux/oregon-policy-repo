## At a glance

## Full text

166.023 Disorderly conduct in the first degree.

(1) A person commits the crime of disorderly conduct in the first degree if, with intent to cause public inconvenience, annoyance or alarm, or knowingly creating a risk thereof, the person initiates or circulates a report, knowing it to be false: (a) Concerning an alleged hazardous substance or an alleged or impending fire, explosion, catastrophe or other emergency; and (b) Stating that the hazardous substance, fire, explosion, catastrophe or other emergency is located in or upon a court facility or a public building, as those terms are defined in ORS 166.360. (2)(a) Disorderly conduct in the first degree is a Class A misdemeanor. (b) Notwithstanding paragraph (a) of this subsection, disorderly conduct in the first degree is a Class C felony if the defendant has at least one prior conviction for violating subsection

(1) of this section. [2005 c.631 §3; 2015 c.361 §1]
