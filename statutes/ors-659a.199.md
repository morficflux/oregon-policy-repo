# Prohibited conduct by employer (Disclosures by Public and Nonprofit Employees) (ORS 659A.199)

## At a glance

ORS 659A.199 — Prohibited conduct by employer (Disclosures by Public and Nonprofit Employees). Chapter 659A (Chapter 659A), 2025 Edition.

## Full text

659A.199 Prohibited conduct by employer.

(1) It is an unlawful employment practice for an employer to discharge, demote, suspend or in any manner discriminate or retaliate against an employee with regard to promotion, compensation or other terms, conditions or privileges of employment for the reason that the employee has in good faith reported information that the employee believes is evidence of a violation of a state or federal law, rule or regulation.

(2) The remedies provided by this chapter are in addition to any common law remedy or other remedy that may be available to an employee for the conduct constituting a violation of this section. [2009 c.524 §2] (Disclosures by Public and Nonprofit Employees)
