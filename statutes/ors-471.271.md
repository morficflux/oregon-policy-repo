## Full text

471.271 Requirement to report human trafficking at licensed premises; rules.

(1) As used in this section, “law enforcement agency” means: (a) A city or municipal police department; (b) A county sheriff’s office; or (c) The Oregon State Police.

(2) An employee of a premises licensed for full on-premises sales or limited on-premises sales who is a permittee shall: (a) Report to a law enforcement agency and to the Oregon Liquor and Cannabis Commission if the permittee has a reasonable belief that sex trafficking or other human trafficking is occurring at the licensed premises; and (b) Report to the commission if the permittee has a reasonable belief that a minor is employed or contracted as a performer at the licensed premises in a manner that violates commission rules.

(3) A permittee making a report under this section in good faith is immune from any criminal or civil liability for making the report.

(4) The commission may adopt rules to carry out this section. [2021 c.44 §2; 2023 c.217 §8] SHIPMENT AND DISTRIBUTION OF MALT BEVERAGES, WINE AND CIDER (Distribution to Malt Beverage, Wine and Cider Retail Licensees)
