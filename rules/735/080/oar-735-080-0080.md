## Full text

735-080-0080 Temporary Duplicate Permits

(1) DMV may issue a temporary duplicate permit placard, as described in ORS 811.602, to an applicant who needs a second permit to travel outside of the person’s county of residence if the applicant holds a valid Disabled Person Parking Permit placard or decal or a valid temporary Disabled Person Parking Permit placard.

(2) The applicant must submit to DMV a Disabled Person Parking Permit Application/Renewal: Individuals (DMV form 735-265) requesting a Temporary Duplicate Permit that includes: (a) The applicant’s name and address; and (b) The applicant’s driver license, driver permit, identification card, or parking identification card number, if one has been assigned by DMV.

(3) A Temporary duplicate travel permit is valid for a period of up to 120 days from the date of issuance. DMV will not issue a temporary duplicate travel permit with an expiration date that is later than the expiration date of the applicant’s primary Disabled Person Parking Permit placard, decal or temporary Disabled Person Parking Permit.

(4) DMV may issue a temporary duplicate travel permit prior to the expiration of a current, valid temporary duplicate permit issued to an applicant. In addition to the requirements in section

(2) of this rule, the applicant must return the current, valid temporary duplicate permit prior to issuance of the new temporary duplicate permit.

Statutory/Other Authority: ORS 184.619, 802.010 & 811.602 Statutes/Other Implemented: ORS 811.602
