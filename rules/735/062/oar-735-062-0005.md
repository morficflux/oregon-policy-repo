## Full text

735-062-0005 SSN — Requirements for Collection and Verification of SSN or a Statement No SSN Assigned

(1) An applicant who has been assigned a SSN is required to provide the SSN assigned to the applicant by SSA to DMV when applying for a driver license, driver permit or identification card.

(2) An applicant applying for a non-Real ID non-commercial driver license or driver permit or identification card who has not been assigned a SSN must provide a written statement that says the person has not been assigned a SSN.

(3) When an applicant provides a SSN as required by section

(1) of this rule, DMV will submit the SSN to the SSA for verification, unless the SSN was previously verified under this rule. An applicant’s SSN is verified when SSA notifies DMV that the applicant’s SSN, name and date of birth are confirmed by SSA’s records.

(4) Notwithstanding section

(3) of this rule, a SSN is not considered verified if the SSA notifies DMV that the SSN provided is inactive, invalid, impossible, not verifiable for other reasons or if there is a system error.
