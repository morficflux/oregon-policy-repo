## Full text

586-030-0076 Appeals

(1) Any party may appeal the final order of the panel by filing a petition in the Court of Appeals in accordance with the statutory timelines following the date the final order is served or within the statutory timeline after denial of a petition for rehearing or petition for reconsideration.

(2) The record will be made available to the parties for inspection, upon reasonable advance request and at a reasonable time and place, if there is an appeal of the final order. The record shall include the following items: (a) All pleadings, motions and intermediate rulings; (b) Evidence received or considered; (c) Stipulations; (d) Correspondence from and to parties or their legal counsel; (e) A statement of any ex parte communications on a fact in issue made to a panel member; (f) Any portion of the hearing record transcribed for the Panel (g) The reporting/recording firm telephone number and address; and (f) The Final Order.

(3) All communications between legal counsel and the Panel members or Board representatives shall not be made available.
