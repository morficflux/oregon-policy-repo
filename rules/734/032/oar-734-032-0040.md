## Full text

734-032-0040 Oregon Scenic Byway Designation Process — Initial Screening of Scenic Byway Proposal

(1) To be considered as a scenic byway the proponent must submit the following: (a) Written narrative statement of the route identifying items of significance or interest in relation to the established criteria; (b) Documentation of conceptual support by jurisdictional agencies in the corridor; (c) Map of route, showing beginning and ending points, length and width of corridor; and (d) Definition of the location of points of interest or significance.

(2) The Oregon Scenic Byway Committee shall review the proposal against the criteria and determine whether it shall proceed through the designation process. The committee shall also review the proposed route against currently designated routes and other pending proposals to assure that the pattern of designated routes shall not be confusing and are not becoming saturated. If the committee recommends not to proceed it shall provide comments to the applicant on weaknesses of the proposal and possible improvements where applicable.

Statutory/Other Authority: ORS 184.617 & 184.619 Statutes/Other Implemented: Intermodal Surface Transportation Efficiency Act of 1991
