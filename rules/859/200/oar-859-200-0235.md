## Full text

859-200-0235 Monthly Reporting

(1) The person designated by the Board as having primary reporting responsibility shall submit monthly progress reports no later than the 10th day of the month for the previous month’s progress. These reports shall be submitted on the form provided by and prepared by Board staff.

(2) All serious incidents must be communicated as soon as possible via a phone call to the PSRB’s office during business hours or the PSRB emergency phone after business hours. Serious incidents must be followed-up with a written incident report. A serious incident includes, but is not limited to: (a) Unauthorized leave; (b) Arrest; (c) Positive urine or oral drug screen or other substance use testing; (d) Repeated refusals to take psychotropic medications or other medications prescribed to manage the person’s mental health; (e) Psychiatric or medical hospitalization; (f) Violent or dangerous behaviors; or (g) Other circumstances deemed to be serious by the reporting party.
