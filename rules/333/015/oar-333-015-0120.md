## Full text

333-015-0120 Oregon Menu Labeling Act: Nutrition Labeling of Variable Menu Items For variable menu items, the chain restaurant shall provide required nutrition information as follows:

(1) If both the highest and lowest value of the variable menu item is within 0 to 10 percent of the median value, the median value alone of the required nutrition information may be listed.

(2) If both the highest and lowest value of the variable menu item is within 11 to 20 percent of the median value, the range of values of the required nutrition information must be listed.

(3) If neither section

(1) or

(2) of this rule applies, each flavor or variety of the menu item must be listed as a separate menu item and accompanied by required nutrition information.

(4) In lieu of sections

(1) through

(3) of this rule, each flavor or variety of a menu item may be listed as a separate menu item and accompanied by required nutrition information.

Statutory/Other Authority: 2009 OL Ch. 314 Statutes/Other Implemented: 2009 OL Ch. 314
