## Full text

170-080-0001 Notice Rule for Rulemaking, Model Rules of Procedure

(1) Notice Rule for Rulemaking. Before adopting, amending or repealing any permanent rule, the Board will give notice of the intended action: (a) In the Secretary of State's Bulletin referred to in ORS 183.360 at least 21 days before the effective date of the rule; (b) By e-mailing a copy of the notice to persons on the Board's mailing list established pursuant to ORS 183.335(8) at least 28 days before the effective date of the rule; (c) By mailing a copy of the notice to the legislators specified in ORS 183.335(15) at least 49 days before the effective date of the rule; and (d) By mailing or furnishing a copy of the notice to the list of interested parties compiled and maintained by the State Treasurer.

(2) Model Rules of Procedure. The Attorney General’s Model Rules of Procedure as set forth in OAR 137, Divisions 1 through 4, are adopted as rules of procedure for administrative rulemaking and other administrative law functions as exercised by the Board in respect to the Program.

(3) Collaborative Dispute Resolution Model Rules. The Attorney General's Collaborative Dispute Resolution Model Rules, as set forth in OAR 137 Division 5, to the extent not inconsistent with the Act or the Code, are adopted by the Board as its rules for dispute resolution.
