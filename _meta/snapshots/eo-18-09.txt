    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 18-09


    DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN
    BAKER COUNTY DUE TO LOW SNOWPACK, PRECIPITATION, AND
    STREAMFLOW, AND WARMING TEMPERATURES


    Pursuant to ORS 536.740, I find that the absence of snowpack, lack of
    precipitation, low streamflows, and warming temperatures are likely to cause
    natural and economic disaster conditions in Baker County, resulting in a severe,
    continuing drought emergency in that county during 2018.


    Forecasted water conditions are not expected to improve, and drought is likely to
    have significant economic impacts on agriculture, livestock, and other natural
    resources in Baker County. Dry conditions will place agricultural and livestock
    investments at risk; affect irrigators and domestic users; threaten recreational and
    tourism activities; increase the potential for early onset fire danger; and subject the
    regional economy to widespread and severe damage.


    Conditions are being monitored by the state’s natural resource and public safety
    agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department and the Oregon
    Office of Emergency Management.


    Preparation and timely response to drought are vital to the safety of persons,
    property, and the economic security of the citizens and businesses of Baker County.
    I therefore declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists in Baker
    County.


    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


     I.     The Oregon Department of Agriculture is directed to coordinate and
           provide assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought
            conditions and to assist in agricultural recovery in Baker County.


    II.     The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
            Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water users
            in Baker County as the Department and Commission determine is necessary
            and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.
