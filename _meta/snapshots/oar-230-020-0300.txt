 Oregon State Athletic Commission - Chapter 230 
 
 
 
 
 Division 20 LICENSES AND APPLICATIONS 
 230-020-0300 
 Medical Requirements for Competition
 
 
 (1) An unarmed combat sports competitor shall undergo a comprehensive physical examination conducted by licensed medical personnel (MD, DO, NP or PA-C) on a state or tribal athletic commission form approved by the commission. The comprehensive physical examination must meet the following requirements: 

 (a) Examination must take place within one year of scheduled competition; 

 (b) Additional testing or an updated physical exam may be required by medical personnel or an authorized representative of the superintendent to resume competition. 

 (2) An unarmed combat sports competitor must submit laboratory blood work demonstrating negative (non-reactive) test results for: 

 (a) Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV ) 4th generation or ½ serum AB, EIA with confirmation 

 (b) Hepatitis B (HBsAg) Hepatitis B Surface Antigen 

 (c) Hepatitis C (HCAb) Hepatitis C antibody 

 (3) Laboratory results must have been drawn within 6 months of the date of scheduled competition. 

 (4) Laboratory results shall include: 

 (a) Competitors Legal Name 

 (b) Competitors Date of Birth 

 (c) Blood Collection Date and Time 

 (d) Laboratory Name 

 (e) Laboratory Address 

 (f) Laboratory Phone Number 

 (5)  For competitors Forty (40) years of age or older, or who will turn 40 years of age within the licensing year: 

 (a) ECG on initial licensure and annually thereafter. 

 (b) MRI of the brain with susceptibility weighted imaging (SWI) or gradient echo imaging (GRE) at initial licensure and every three (3) years thereafter. 

 (c) MR Angiogram of the brain at initial licensure. 

 (d) Neuropsychological (neurocognitive) evaluation at initial licensure and every three (3) years thereafter. 

 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 463.113 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 463.025 & 463.113
