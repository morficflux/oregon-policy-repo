    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-02


    EXPANDING STATE OF EMERGENCY DECLARED IN EXECUTIVE
    ORDER 25-32 TO INCLUDE LINN, POLK, UMATILLA, UNION, AND
    WALLOWA COUNTIES


    WHEREAS, Governor Tina Kotek signed Executive Order 25-32 on December
    30, 2025, declaring a state of emergency for Clackamas, Clatsop, Coos, Curry,
    Douglas, Hood River, Jackson, Klamath, Lane, Lincoln, Marion, Multnomah,
    Tillamook, Washington, and Yamhill counties.


    WHEREAS, Upon review of additional damage assessment information to public
    infrastructure and state roads on the federal-aid highway system and the severe
    winter storms also caused disaster conditions in Linn, Polk, Umatilla, Union, and
    Wallowa Counties.


    NOW, THEREFORE, |, Tina Kotek, Governor ofthe State of Oregon, by virtue
    ofthe authority vested in me under ORS 401.165, do hereby find that a threat to
    life, safety, property, and significant damage to the state roads on the federal-aid
    highway system was caused by severe winter storms and have caused disaster
    conditions in the above-listed counties beginning December 8, 2025, and
    continuing through December 31, 2025.


    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED AND DIRECTED:


        1.   Executive Order 25-32 be amended to include Linn, Polk, Umatilla, Union,
             and Wallowa Counties.


       2. The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM). OEM shall
          activate the State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and
             coordinate necessary access to use of personnel and equipment of all state
             agencies necessary to assess, alleviate, respond to, mitigate, and recover
             from conditions caused by this emergency. In conformance with the
             Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and ORS chapter 401, state
             agencies are directed to provide resources and support requested by OEM to
             assist in the response to this emergency.


       3. The Oregon Department ofTransportation (ODOT). ODOT shall provide
          appropriate assistance and seek federal resources to effect repair and
          reconstruction for damages and impacts to the federal aid highway system
             in the above-listed counties.
