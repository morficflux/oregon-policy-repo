    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-14


    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    FALLS FIRE INHARNEY COUNTY


    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:

    The fire known as the “Falls Fire” is burning in Harney County.


    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Falls Fire are
    beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire protection
    was requested by the Harney County Judge Bill Hart and the State Fire Marshal
    concurs with that request.


    In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to the fire known as the Falls Fire in Harney County
    and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting personnel
    and equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency Conflagration Act.

    These findings were made at 10:56 am on July 14, 2024, with the Department of
    the State Fire Marshal, the Oregon State Fire Marshal Blue Incident Management
    Team will be entering into unified command the Northwest Team 8. The Oregon
    State Fire Marshal Blue Incident Management Team will be briefed at 7:00 pm on
    July 14, 2024, and I now confirm them with this Executive Order.

    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


     I,    The Department of the State Fire Marshal shall mobilize fire resources
           statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense Board Chiefs for
           the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the Emergency
           Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources responding
           to the Falls Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire Marshal.


    De     This conflagration is declared only for the Falls Fire threatening structures
            in Harney County.


    3,     This order was made by verbal proclamation at 10:56 a.m. on the 14th day
           of July 2024, and signed this 15" day of July 2024, in Salem, Oregon.
