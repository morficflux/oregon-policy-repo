    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-25


    NAMING THE SALEM CAMPUS OF THE STATE HOSPITAL AFTER
    OREGON SENATE PRESIDENT PETER COURTNEY


    WHEREAS, the Honorable Peter Courtney has served in the Oregon Legislative
    Assembly, both in the House of Representatives and the Senate, for 38 years,
    making him the longest-serving legislator in Oregon’s history; and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney has served a record 20 years as President of the
    Senate, after having also served as House Democratic Leader; and

    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney previously served as a member of the Salem City
    Council, and has served on the boards of Salem Area Mass Transit, Salem YMCA,
    Salem United Way, and has coached basketball for the Salem Boys & Girls Club;
    and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney has provided leadership on many issues vital to
    Oregon’s government and way of life, including but not limited to seismic safety
    projects for schools and other public buildings, and the constitutional amendment
    that authorizes annual legislative sessions; and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney has long been known as a champion for mental health
    services, leading the charge to increase funding for community mental health
    treatment, and winning passage of an increase in the cigarette tax which is
    dedicated to funding mental health treatment; and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney continued his efforts to improve mental health care in
    Oregon in 2004, when he was amonga group that discovered in Building 60 of the
    Oregon State Hospital the unclaimed cremated remains of nearly 3,500 Oregonians
    who had died while patients at the Oregon State Hospital and other state
    institutions; and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney was instrumental in the effort to replace the original
    1883 Oregon State Hospital with new and refurbished facilities in 2011, including a
    public memorial to those whose remains were found in Building 60, which became
    known as the “Room of Forgotten Souls”; and


    WHEREAS, more than 1,250 of those cremated remains subsequently have been
    reunited with families and loved ones; and

    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney has been a talented leader, a valued colleague, and a
    steady presence in the Oregon State Capitol and in Oregon’s state government for
    four decades; and


    WHEREAS, Peter Courtney has been supported in a lifetime of extraordinary
    service to Oregon by his wife, Margie, his three sons, Peter, Sean, and Adam, his
    three daughters-in-law, and his five grandchildren; and


    WHEREAS, it is entirely fitting to honor Peter Courtney for his extraordinary
    service to the State of Oregon;


    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


    The Oregon Health Authority is directed to name the state hospital facility in Salem
    the Peter Courtney Salem Campus of the Oregon State Hospital.

    Done at Salem, Oregon, this 13th day of December, 2022.
