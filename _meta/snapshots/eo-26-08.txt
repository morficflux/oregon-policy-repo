    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-08


    DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN COOS,
    KLAMATH AND WHEELER COUNTIES


    At the request of Coos County (through Coos County Board of Commissioners
    Resolution 26-04-029L, dated April 7, 2026), Klamath County (through Klamath County
    Board of Commissioners Resolution RE2026-025, dated April 14, 2026), and Wheeler
    County (through Wheeler County Court Resolution 2026-008, dated April 15, 2026), and
    based on the recommendations of the Drought Readiness Council and input from the
    Water Supply Availability Committee dated May 5, 2026, and pursuant to ORS 536.740,
    I find that moderate and severe drought conditions in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler
    Counties (Counties) and extreme drought conditions in Wheeler County as well as the
    conditions described below will cause natural and economic disaster conditions in the
    Counties.


        1.   Well above normal temperature conditions,    just below normal precipitation
             conditions and long-term precipitation deficits, below normal streamflow
             conditions, and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions in Coos County.

       2. Well above normal temperature conditions,    just below normal precipitation
          conditions and long-term precipitation deficits, well below normal snowpack
          conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions in Klamath County.

       3. Well above normal temperature conditions, below normal precipitation conditions
             and long-term precipitation deficits, below normal streamflow conditions, well     |
             below normal snowpack conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture
             conditions in Wheeler County.                                                      |

    Forecasted snowpack, temperature, precipitation and water supply conditions suggest that        |
    drought conditions in the Counties are unlikely to improve in the near-term. Drought is
    likely to have a significant economic impact on the agricultural, livestock, natural
    resource and recreational tourism sectors in the Counties.

    Conditions continue to be monitored by the State’s natural resource and public safety
    agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department, the Oregon Department of
    Emergency Management, the Oregon Office of the State Fire Marshal, and the Oregon
    Department of Forestry’s Fire Protection Division.

    Preparation and resiliency to drought are vital to the health and safety of persons,
    property, and the economic security ofthe citizens and businesses ofthese counties.
    I, therefore, declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists and is likely to
    continue to exist in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler Counties.

    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


            The Oregon Department of Agriculture is directed to coordinate and provide
            assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought conditions and
            assist in agricultural recovery in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler.


      U.    The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
            Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water users
            in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler Counties as the Department and Commission
            determine necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to
            536.780.


      Hl.   The Oregon Water Resources Department is directed to seek information from
            the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife to help understand the impacts of
            water availability on Oregon’s fish and wildlife, as necessary and appropriate
            in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.


            The Oregon Department of Emergency Management is directed to coordinate
            and assist as needed with assessment and mitigation activities to address
            current and projected conditions in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler Counties.


            All other state agencies are directed to coordinate with the above agencies and
            provide appropriate state resources as needed to assist affected political
            subdivisions and water users in Coos, Klamath and Wheeler Counties.


      Vi.   This Executive Order expires on December 31, 2026.


            Done at Salem, Oregon, this 11'" day of May, 2026.
