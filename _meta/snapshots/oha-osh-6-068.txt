 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. This policy establishes a process for random drug screening of patients at
      Oregon State Hospital (OSH) to screen for potential illicit drugs or diverted
      medication.
   B. This policy applies to all direct care staff.

II. POLICY
   A. OSH will run patient random urine drug screening on every business day on each
      campus as follows:
      1. 10 samples will be selected in Salem
      2. 3 samples will be selected in Junction city
   B. For sample ordering, collection, and processing, see Procedures A.
   C. For results review and response, see Procedures B.
   D. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   E. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
      may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Confirmed Unexpected Positive” means a result from the reference lab,
      reflecting the confirmed presence of a non-prescribed drug.
   B. “Illicit Drug” means a controlled substance not prescribed to the patient or
      prescribed at OSH.
   C. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at OSH.
   D. “Unexpected Negative Screen” means a result from any lab reflecting the
      absence of a prescribed drug expected to show up on the urine drug screen.
