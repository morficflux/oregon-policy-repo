 Oregon Department of Education 
 Chapter 581 
 
 
 
 
 
 Division 21 SCHOOL GOVERNANCE AND STUDENT CONDUCT 
 581-021-0582 
 Establishment 
 
 (1) The State Seal of Biliteracy is established to recognize high school graduates who have attained a high level of proficiency in reading, writing, listening, and speaking in one or more World languages in addition to English. The State Seal of Biliteracy shall be awarded by the Superintendent of Public Instruction. School district participation in this program is voluntary.  (2) The purposes of the State Seal of Biliteracy are as follows:  (a) To encourage students to study languages.  (b) To certify attainment of biliteracy.  (c) To provide employers with a method of identifying people with language and biliteracy skills.  (d) To provide post-secondary institutions with a method to recognize and give academic credit to applicants seeking admission.  (e) To prepare students to be college and career ready.  (f) To recognize and promote world language instruction in public schools.  (g) To strengthen intergroup relationships, affirm the value of diversity, and honor the multiple cultures and languages of a community. 
 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 326.051 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 326.051
