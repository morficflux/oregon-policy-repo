I.         PURPOSE

           To provide accurate, factual information to prospective employers about the work
           performance of current and former employees of the Department of Corrections (DOC).

II.        DEFINITION

           DOC employee: Any person employed full-time, part-time, or under temporary
           appointment by the Department of Corrections.

III.       POLICY

           A.            It is the policy of the Department of Corrections to respond in good faith to
                         employment references for former and current employees.

           B.            All requests for reference checks shall be routed through the appropriate Senior
                         Human Resources (HR) Business Partner assigned to an institution or division. This
                         requirement ensures the agency can appropriately respond, ensures there is no
                         settlement agreement preventing, restricting, or dictating the release of
                         information, and prevents any claim of retaliation which may arise from less than
                         positive information.

           C.            For general reference checks, the Senior HR Business Partner shall identify the
                         appropriate person to respond to employment references, upon receipt of a
                         written release from the subject employee. An emailed or faxed copy of the
                         release will suffice.

           D.            For reference checks sent directly to DOC employees, the employees shall contact
                         their respective Senior HR Business Partner for guidance, prior to responding to
                         any reference check inquiries.

           E.            The designated reference shall:

                         1.     Take detailed notes about the conversation, including what was
                                specifically said.

                         2.     Answer only in response to the prospective employer’s questions and not
                                volunteer any information.

                         3.     Assess each question to determine whether the reference has firsthand
                                knowledge before responding. If the reference is not certain of the
                                answer, they should not speculate as to the response.

                         4.     Restrict comments to the employee’s job performance (that is, the
                                employee’s skill in relationship to the employee’s position and
                                responsibilities the employee performed.

                         5.     Base responses on factual, objectively reasonable grounds and refrain
                                from making subjective statements about the employee to the extent
                                possible.

                         6.     Provide any pertinent, factual information, whether positive or negative.
                                Do not mislead the prospective employer by revealing only some
                                information if it is not the whole picture. In situations where the employee
                                may have engaged in illegal or criminal acts or has demonstrated
                                dangerous behavior, the reference shall contact the assigned Senior HR
                                Business Partner who will seek legal advice before a response is made.

                         7.     Not provide a negative reference regarding an employee who left because
                                of circumstances that might suggest a civil rights claim such as whistle
                                blowing or sexual harassment. The negative reference could be evidence
                                of retaliation. The supervisor shall refer this matter to the Senior HR
                                Business Partner who will seek legal advice before a response is made.

                         8.     In the event of uncertainty, only provide a neutral reference; specifically,
                                only dates of employment, position(s) held, and job responsibilities.
                                Include nothing disparaging and give no reason for separation from the
                                agency.

                         9.     Retention of the signed release from the subject employee and all notes
                                of the employment reference in email form is sufficient.

           F.            Requests for personnel files and other public records pertaining to current and
                         former employees shall be directed to the Employee Services Division.

IV.        POLICY CLARIFICATION

           A signed release is not required for reference checks upon promotion, transfer, or
           voluntary demotion within the department.

V.         IMPLEMENTATION

           This policy will be adopted immediately without further modification.
