     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 24-09


     INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
     DARLENE 3 FIRE IN DESCHUTES COUNTY


     Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, | find that:


     The fire known as the “Darlene 3 Fire” is burning in Deschutes County.


     The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Darlene 3 Fire are
     beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire protection
     was requested by the Tri-County (Jefferson, Deschutes, and Crook) Fire Defense
     Board Chief and the State Fire Marshal concurs with that request.


     In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
     safety, and property exists due to the fire known as the Darlene 3 Fire in Deschutes
     County and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local firefighting
     personnel and equipment. Accordingly, | have invoked the Emergency
     Conflagration Act.


     These findings were made at 3:38 pm on June 25, 2024, with the Department of the
     State Fire Marshal, the Oregon State Fire Marshal Red Incident Management Team
     will be entering into unified command the Central Oregon Fire Management
     Service Type 3 Incident Management Team. The Oregon State Fire Marshal Red
     Incident Management Team will be briefed at 9:00 pm on June 25, 2024, and I now
     confirm them with this Executive Order.

     NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

     IE     The Department ofthe State Fire Marshal shall mobilize fire resources
            statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense Board Chiefs for
            the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the Emergency
            Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources responding
            to the Darlene 3 Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire Marshal.

     De     This conflagration is declared only for the Darlene 3 Fire threatening
            structures in Deschutes County.


     3      This order was made by verbal proclamation at 3:38 pm on the 25" day of
            June 2024, and signed this 5" day of July 2024, in Salem, Oregon.
