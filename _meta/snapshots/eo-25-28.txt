    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-28



    SECOND EXTENSION OF EXECUTIVE ORDER 25-08 DECLARING A
    STATE OF EMERGENCY IN WASCO COUNTY DUE TO THE ROWENA
    WILDFIRE


    On June 18, 2025, pursuant to ORS 401.165 e¢ seq., I found that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to the wildfire that has caused a natural disaster in
    Wasco County and declared a state of emergency in Wasco County.


    Beginning June 11, 2025, and continuing, severe wildfire rolled through Wasco
    County causing extensive damage to homes and critical infrastructure; threatening
    life, safety, and property resulting in displacement of personnel; and a loss of power
    and communications within the region.


    On June 11, 2025, the fire resulted in a state conflagration and federal Fire
    Management Assistance Grant (FMAG) declarations, community evacuations, and
    threatened and damaged critical infrastructure, destroying homes and other
    structures. On June 18, 2025, the Wasco County Board of Commissioners declared
    a state of emergency due to the Rowena Wild fire and the threat to life, safety, and
    property that exists in Wasco County. Commission Order 25-048 further declared
    local resources were exhausted and immediate state and federal assistance was
    requested.


    On July 16, 2025, the Wasco County Board of Commissioners extended the county
   declaration of a state of emergency due to the Rowena Wildfire. The order declared
   that the hazardous conditions resulting from the Rowena Fire, including but not
   limited to threats to life, property, and natural resources, continue to pose a
   significant risk to the health and safety of Wasco County residents. State and local
   agencies continue to respond to requests for mass care and hazardous debris
   removal in support of this emergency. Damage assessment reports from this
   jurisdiction include major damage to homes and critical infrastructure resulting in
   significant hazardous debris posing a threat to life and safety.

    On October 30, 2025, in coordination with Wasco County, it was determined that
    hazardous conditions resulting from the Rowena Fire still remain and continues to
    pose a significant risk to health and safety of Wasco County residents and thus
    constitutes an emergency under ORS 401.025(1).

   NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


      IL.   The Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM) shall continue
            coordination under the State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management
            Plan for the use of personnel, equipment, technical expertise contracting
            authority of all state agencies necessary to alleviate, respond to, mitigate,
            and recover from conditions caused by this emergency.


            In conformance with the Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan and
            ORS 401, state agencies are directed to provide resources and support
            requested by OEM to assist in the response to this emergency. Any local
            government requests for state resources must be submitted through county
            governing bodies to the Oregon Department of Emergency Management
            pursuant to ORS chapter 401. The Oregon Department of Human Services ,
            Oregon Health Authority, and Department of Environmental Quality and
            other state agencies are directed to work expeditiously to provide any
            assistance as directed by OEM that is deemed necessary to assist in the
            response to and recovery from this emergency, remove barriers to recovery,
            and provide all necessary support to Wasco County response, recovery, and
            mitigation efforts.


            Persons who believe they have been subjected to unconscionably excessive
            prices for essential consumer goods due to this emergency are requested to
            report violations to the Oregon Department of Justice Consumer Protection
            Services, which has the authority to investigate unlawful trade practices.

            This extension is limited to Wasco County at this time.

            Executive Order 25-08 was made by verbal proclamation at 7:10 p.m. on
            June 18, 2025 and signed on June 20, 2025, in Salem, Oregon.
