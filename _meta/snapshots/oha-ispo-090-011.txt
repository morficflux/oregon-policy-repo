Purpose
The Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) are
committed to protecting agency data in all forms and formats from being lost or misused. Improper
disposal of digital and physical media containing protected information increases the risk for data loss
or compromise. Implementation and maintenance of security measures is necessary to protect digital
and physical media.

Description
This policy establishes the requirements for the protection, disposal, sanitization, and destruction of
ODHS and OHA information stored on digital or physical media to prevent unauthorized access to
agency data.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff including employees, volunteers, trainees and interns
as well as contractors and partners.

As keepers of the public trust, all agency employees have a responsibility to comply with state and
agency policies, administrative rule, and state and federal law. The agency takes this responsibility
seriously and failure to fulfill this responsibility is not treated lightly. Employees who fail to comply with
state or agency policy, administrative rule, or state and federal law may face progressive discipline,
up to and including dismissal from state service.

Policy
1. Digital media includes any form of electronic media designed to store data in a digital format.
     This includes but is not limited to digital media in laptops, computers, and mobile devices, and
     any removable, transportable electronic media, such as magnetic tape or disk, optical disk, flash
     drives, external hard drives, or digital memory cards.1

2.      Physical media includes, but is not limited to, printed documents, printed imagery, and printed
        facsimile.2
3.      ODHS and OHA staff shall ensure digital and physical media used to process, store, or transmit
        protected information is securely stored within physically secure locations or controlled areas,3
        with access restricted to authorized staff. Protected information includes, but is not limited to:
        a. Personally identifiable information (PII).
        b. Protected health information (PHI)
        c. Federal tax information (FTI).
        d. Criminal Justice Information (CJI).
        e. Social Security Administration (SSA) information.
        f. Payment Card Industry (PCI) information.
        g. Data covered by Title 42 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Part 2 (Confidentiality of
             Substance Use Disorder Patient Records).
4.      Transporting media containing protected information shall be:
         a. Restricted to authorized staff who have been appropriately vetted and trained.
         b. Only when necessary to limit risk of loss or theft.
         c. Protected and secured when the media is transported outside of controlled areas; and
         d. Documented when transporting media for disposal, including:
             A. What media was transported.
             B. The method of transportation.
             C. The date and time the media was collected.
             D. The names of authorized individuals who handled the media at each stage of transport
                 and disposal.
             E. The date, time and location where the media was received at the end of transport.
         e. Documentation shall be stored in an agreed upon location.4
5.      Digital and physical media containing CJI shall be marked indicating the distribution limitations,
        handling caveats, and applicable security markings of the information, if any.
6.      Digital and physical media containing CJI that remains within physically secure locations or
        agency-controlled areas does not require marking. Controlled areas are spaces that provide
        physical and procedural controls to meet the requirements established for protecting information
        and systems.5
7.      ODHS and OHA staff shall restrict the use of digital and non-digital media on agency owned
        systems that have been approved for use in the storage, processing, or transmission of CJI by
        using technical, physical, or administrative controls. (Refer to ODHSOHA 090-009
        Administrative, Technical and Physical Safeguards of Information Policy)
         a. The use of personally owned digital media devices on agency-owned or controlled systems
             that store, process, or transmit CJI is prohibited.6

          transmit CJI is prohibited when such devices do not have an identifiable owner.7
8. Digital media containing protected information shall be sanitized using defined sanitization
    techniques8 in accordance with state and federal requirements prior to reuse, disposal, or
    release from the agency’s control.
     a. Sanitization is defined as overwriting at least three times or degaussing digital media prior to
          disposal or release for reuse by unauthorized individuals.
     b. Sanitization mechanisms shall be employed with the strength and integrity equal to the
          security category or classification of the information.
     c. Verification of the sanitization process is required.
9. Physical media containing protected information shall be disposed of according to the
    ODHSOHA 090-011-01 Media Disposal Process.
10. ODHS and OHA staff working remotely shall comply with the agency’s human resources remote
    work requirements and follow privacy and security policies to protect agency information.
     a. Managers shall direct staff to ensure the appropriate level of security for protecting agency
          information and equipment in transit or at the alternate worksite.
     b. Staff shall have manager approval to have an authorized ODHS and OHA provided printer
          at an alternate worksite, if printing is required. Approval includes an assessment of the data
          to be printed.9
     c. Staff shall secure printed documents containing protected information in locked drawers or
          bins until they can be returned to the office for shredding or other secure disposal.
11. ODHS and OHA staff shall maintain records documenting all digital media disposal activities in
    compliance with the ODHSOHA 010-018 Records Retention and Management Policy and
    litigation hold requirements. Documentation shall include:
     a. Information about the media (type, serial number, other unique identifiers).
     b. Date of media disposal.
     c. Persons performing the disposal activity.
     d. Method used to render all data unusable, such as the software tool used or physical
          destruction of the media.
     e. Signature of the person responsible for ensuring that all data on the storage media has
          been rendered unusable.
