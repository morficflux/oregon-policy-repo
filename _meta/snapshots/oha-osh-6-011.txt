 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) uses treatment teams made up of staff from different
   areas (called an Interdisciplinary Treatment Team or IDT). These teams work
   together to give patients care that is focused on recovery, safety, and respect.
B. This policy applies to all staff on IDTs.

II. GUIDELINES
A. Every patient must have their own treatment care plan (TCP). This plan is made by
   the IDT in collaboration with the patient. The plan lists all treatments the patient will
   receive and is based on staff assessments. The team looks at risks, treatment
   needs, and goals for recovery and discharge.
B. For the first 10 days in the hospital, patients follow an Initial Plan of Care (IPoC) as
   specified in Procedures A.
C. Staff must follow the rules in this policy for adding special treatments and programs
   to a patient's care plan.
D. The patient, their legal guardian, or chosen representative must be included in
   planning. If applicable, other persons important to the patient may also be included,
   at the patient's request or with their consent.
E. A psychiatrist, psychiatric nurse practitioner (PMHNP), or a designated psychologist
   must lead the making and updating of the TCP.
   1. The plan must be updated the next business day if there are big changes in the
      patient's condition (for example, a new medical issue). Discipline representatives
