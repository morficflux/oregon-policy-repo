Purpose
The Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) are
committed to identifying, analyzing, remediating, documenting, and reporting any information security
incident that compromises ODHS and OHA information and systems.

Description
This policy describes the responsibility of ODHS and OHA staff to report known or suspected
information security incidents. This includes sensitive, confidential, or protected information.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff including employees, volunteers, trainees and interns
as well as contractors and partners.

As keepers of the public trust, all agency employees have a responsibility to comply with state and
agency policies, administrative rule, and state and federal law. The agency takes this responsibility
seriously and failure to fulfill this responsibility is not treated lightly. Employees who fail to comply with
state or agency policy, administrative rule, or state and federal law may face progressive discipline,
up to and including dismissal from state service.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA staff have a responsibility to promptly report any known or suspected information
   security incidents. Reports made in good faith will be taken seriously and handled appropriately, in
   alignment with agency policies and procedures. Examples of an information security incident
   include, but are not limited to:
   a. Attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, destruction of
       information, or interference with system operations in an information system.
   b. Violations of ODHS and OHA information security policies and processes. For example, staff
       sharing their login credentials.
   c. Violations of the Statewide Information Technology Control Standards. For example, editing or
       modifying system records without approval.
2. When ODHS and OHA staff discover an information security incident or potential information
   security incident, staff shall follow the reporting process as outlined in the ODHSOHA 090-005-01
   Information Security Incident Reporting Process.
