 Oregon Health Authority 
 Public Health Division - Chapter 333 
 
 
 
 
 Division 46 VACCINES FOR CHILDREN (VFC) PROGRAM AND VACCINE ACCESS PROGRAM (VAP) 
 333-046-0100 
 Clinic Probation
 
 
 (1) Except in cases of suspected fraud, when a clinic is sanctioned by the Oregon Health Authority (Authority) for the first time under OAR 333-046-0090 sections (1), (6) or (7), the Authority will offer a clinic the opportunity to voluntarily sign a probationary agreement before removing the clinic from the Vaccines for Children (VFC) program in accordance with OAR 333-046-0110. 

 (2) The terms of the probationary agreement will be determined by the violations that have been identified by the Authority. The final probationary agreement will be approved by the Oregon Immunization Program manager or their designee. 

 (3) Failure to return a signed copy of the final probationary agreement within 14 calendar days of receipt of the agreement by the clinic will forfeit any right to a probationary agreement under section (1) of this rule. 

 (4) A probationary agreement may be in effect for no longer than 12 months before a final resolution is determined. If the clinic has complied with all clauses of the agreement during the probationary period, the clinic will be returned to regular status with the VFC program or Vaccine Access Program (VAP). If the clinic has not complied with all clauses of the agreement during the probationary period, the clinic will be removed from the VFC or VAP program under OAR 333-046-0110. 

 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 413.042 & 431.250 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 413.042 & 431.250
