     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-07


     DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN
     CROOK, GRANT, JACKSON, JEFFERSON, MORROW AND WALLOWA
     COUNTIES.


     At the request of Crook County (through Crook County Board of Commissioners
     Resolution and Order 2026-10, dated March 4, 2026), Grant County (through Grant
     County Court Resolution 26-10, dated April 1, 2026), Jackson County (through
     Jackson County Board of Commissioners Order No. 78-26, dated April 1, 2026),
     Jefferson County (through Jefferson County Board of Commissioners Order No. 0-
     033-26, dated March 18, 2026), Morrow County (through Morrow County Board
     of Commissioners Resolution No. R-2026-05, dated March 4, 2026) and Wallowa
     County (through Wallowa County Board of Commissioners Resolution 2026-012,
     dated March 18, 2026), and based on the recommendations of the Drought
     Readiness Council and input from the Water Supply Availability Committee dated
     April 16, 2026, and pursuant to ORS 536.740, I find that moderate and severe
     drought conditions exist in addition to:

         1) well above normal temperature conditions, below normal precipitation and
            streamflow conditions, well below normal snowpack conditions and drier-
            than-normal soil moisture conditions have caused or will cause natural and
            economic disaster conditions in Crook County;

        2) well above normal temperature conditions, long-term precipitation deficits,
           below normal streamflow conditions, well below normal snowpack
           conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions have caused or
            will cause natural and economic disaster conditions in Grant County;

        3) well above normal temperature conditions, long-term precipitation deficits,
           below normal streamflow conditions, well below normal snowpack
           conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions have caused or
           will cause natural and economic disaster conditions in Jackson County;

        4) well above normal temperature conditions,just below normal precipitation
           conditions, well below normal snowpack conditions and drier-than-normal
            soil moisture conditions have caused or will cause natural and economic
            disaster conditions in Jefferson County;


        5) well above normal temperature conditions, long-term precipitation deficits,
           below normal streamflow conditions, well below normal snowpack
           conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture conditions have caused or
            will cause natural and economic disaster conditions in Morrow County;

         6) above normal temperature conditions, long-term precipitation deficits, well
            below normal snowpack conditions and drier-than-normal soil moisture
            conditions have caused or will cause natural and economic disaster
               conditions in Wallowa County.


     Forecasted snowpack, temperature, precipitation and water supply conditions
     suggest that drought conditions in the counties are unlikely to improve in the near-
     term. Drought is likely to have a significant economic impact on the agricultural,
     livestock, natural resource and recreational tourism sectors in Crook, Grant,
     Jackson, Jefferson, Morrow and Wallowa Counties.

     Conditions continue to be monitored by the State’s natural resource and public
     safety agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department, the Oregon
     Department of Emergency Management, the Oregon Office of the State Fire
     Marshal, and the Oregon Department of Forestry’s Fire Protection Division.

     Preparation and resiliency to drought are vital to the health and safety of persons,
     property, and the economic security ofthe citizens and businesses of these
     counties. I, therefore, declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists
     and is likely to continue to exist in Crook, Grant, Jackson, Jefferson, Morrow and
     Wallowa Counties.


     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

        Ik,       The Oregon Department of Agriculture is directed to coordinate and
                  provide assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought
                  conditions and assist in agricultural recovery in Crook, Grant, Jackson,
                  Jefferson, Morrow and Wallowa Counties.


        lil       The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
                  Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water
                  users in Crook, Grant, Jackson, Jefferson, Morrow and Wallowa
                  Counties as the Department and Commission determine necessary and
                  appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.

        Ill.      The Oregon Water Resources Department is directed to seek
                  information from the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife to help
                  understand the impacts of water availability on Oregon’s fish and
                  wildlife, as necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700
                  to 536.780.
