   THIS EXECUTIVE ORDER SUPERSEDES EXECUTIVE ORDER 23-01 TO
   ADD MALHEUR COUNTY.


   DETERMINATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN CLACKAMAS,
   COOS, CURRY, DOUGLAS, HOOD RIVER, JACKSON, JOSEPHINE,
   LANE, LINCOLN, MULTNOMAH, MALHEUR, WASHINGTON, AND
   UMATILLA COUNTIES DUE TO A SEVERE WINTER STORM THAT
   INCLUDES HIGH WINDS, HEAVY RAIN, ICE ACCUMULATION,
   FLOODING, AND LANDSLIDES


   Pursuant to ORS 401.165, I find that a threat to life, safety, property, and
   significant damage to the state roads on the federal-aid highway system was caused
   by severe winter storms and have caused disaster conditions in Clackamas, Coos,
   Curry, Douglas, Hood River, Jackson, Josephine, Lane, Lincoln, Multnomah,
   Malheur, Washington, and Umatilla counties. Beginning December 22, 2022, and
   continuing through January 6, 2023, these severe storms resulted in heavy rain,
   high winds, flooding, ice accumulation, landslides, and erosion at various locations
   within these counties, leading to critical transportation failures and disruption to
   energy and communications infrastructure. This storm system damaged state
   highways throughout these counties with scour, washouts, debris flows and
   mudslides.


   IT IS ORDERED AND DIRECTED:


       1.   Pursuant to ORS 401.165, and based on the facts described above, an
            emergency has occurred, and a state of emergency is declared for the above-
            listed counties from December 22, 2022 through January 6, 2023.

      2. The Oregon Department of Transportation shall provide appropriate
         assistance and seek federal resources to effect repair and reconstruction of
         $2 million dollars or more in damages and impact to the federal aid
            highway system in the above-listed counties.
