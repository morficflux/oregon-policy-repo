    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-18


    DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN
    MORROW, DOUGLAS, AND UNION COUNTIES.


    At the request of Morrow County (by Morrow County Board of Commissioners
    Resolution No. R-2025-08, dated July 2, 2025), Douglas County (by Order of
    Douglas County Board of County Commissioners, 2025-0832, dated July 16,
    2025), and Union County (by Union County Board of Commissioners Resolution
    2025-15, dated July 16, 2025) and based on the recommendations of the Drought
    Readiness Council and input from the Water Supply Availability Committee dated
    July 20, 2025 (Morrow County) and July 31, 2025 (Douglas and Union Counties)
    and pursuant to ORS 536.740, I find that persistent moderate and severe drought
    conditions as well as below normal precipitation, above normal temperature and
    below normal streamflows and soil moisture conditions have caused or will cause
    natural and economic disaster conditions in Morrow County, Douglas County, and
    Union County (hereinafter “Counties”).


    Forecasts suggest that below normal precipitation and streamflow conditions are
    likely to continue in the Counties following an extended period during spring and
    early summer with drier and warmer than normal conditions. Without sustained
    precipitation, drought conditions are likely to persist or intensify in the Counties.
    Drought is likely to have a significant economic impact on the agricultural,
    livestock, natural resource and recreational tourism sectors and increase the risk of
    wildfire in the Counties.


    Conditions continue to be monitored by the State’s natural resource and public
    safety agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department, the Oregon
    Office of Emergency Management, the Oregon Office of the State Fire Marshal,
    and the Oregon Department of Forestry’s Fire Protection Division.


    Preparation and resiliency to drought are vital to the health and safety of people,
    property, and the economic security of the citizens and businesses of these
    counties. I, therefore, declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists
    and is likely to continue to exist in the Counties.


    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


    I          The Oregon Department of Agriculture is directed to coordinate and
               provide assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought
               conditions and assist in agricultural recovery in Morrow, Douglas, and
               Union Counties.

      Il.    The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
             Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water
             users in Morrow, Douglas, and Union Counties as the Department and
             Commission determine necessary and appropriate in accordance with
             ORS 536.700 to 536.780.


      Ill.   The Oregon Water Resources Department is directed to seek
             information from the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife to help
             understand the impacts of water availability on Oregon’s fish and
             wildlife, as necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700
             to 536.780.


      IV.    The Office of Emergency Management is directed to coordinate and
             assist as needed with assessment and mitigation activities to address
             current and projected conditions in Morrow, Douglas, and Union
             Counties.


             All other state agencies are directed to coordinate with the above
             agencies and provide appropriate state resources as needed to assist
             affected political subdivisions and water users in Morrow, Douglas, and
             Union Counties.


      VI.    This Executive Order expires on December 31, 2025.


             Done at Salem, Oregon, this 1" day of August, 2025.
