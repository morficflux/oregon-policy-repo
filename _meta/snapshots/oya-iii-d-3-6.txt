I.         PURPOSE

           This policy provides OYA staff direction regarding family planning services
           available to OYA youth who are on parole or probation.

II.        POLICY DEFINITIONS

           None.

III.       POLICY

           OYA has identified diversity, equity and inclusion as an agency priority and
           initiative, with a goal to build a respectful, diverse, equitable, and inclusive
           environment for youth and staff that is free from harassment, discrimination and
           bias. Data shows youth of color and LGBTQ+ youth are disproportionately
           represented in the juvenile justice system. While OYA is only one part of that
           system, we play a critical role in addressing the historical and systemic inequities
           it perpetuates. We recognize that youth from historically marginalized
           communities have limited access to proper health care. For this reason, it is
           imperative that staff provide youth with culturally relevant information on family
           planning resources.

           Decisions involving family planning are personal and may involve the youth, the
           youth’s parents/guardians, and the youth’s physician.
           OYA case management services include the assurance that information
           regarding family planning resources is available to requesting youth. Case

IV.    GENERAL STANDARDS

       A.     OYA staff must not interfere with or restrict the right of a consenting
              youth’s access to safe and effective methods of contraception.

       B.     OYA staff will provide requesting youth information on culturally relevant
              community resources related to family planning.

       C.     OYA staff must refer a pregnant youth to a healthcare provider for
              information about available choices regarding family planning. OYA staff
              must not provide their personal opinion or advice to youth regarding family
              planning.

       D.     If OYA staff have information about use or abuse of substances by a
              pregnant youth, a report must be made to the Department of Human
              Services by calling the child abuse reporting phone number
              1-855-503-SAFE (7233).

              Treatment services must be made available to chemically dependent
              pregnant youth in OYA custody.

       E.     Staff must follow OYA policy 0-2.3 Mandatory Reporting of Abuse if they
              know or suspect a pregnant youth has been subjected to domestic
              violence or abuse.
