Office of the Governor
State of Oregon

  EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 21-01


  DETERMINATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN CLATSOP, CURRY, HOOD
  RIVER, LINCOLN, MULTNOMAH, AND TILLAMOOK COUNTIES DUE TO A
  SEVERE WINTER STORM THAT INCLUDED HIGH WINDS, HEAVY RAIN,
  FLOODING, AND LANDSLIDES


  Pursuant to ORS 401.165, I find that a threat to life, safety, property, and significant damage to
  the state roads on the federal-aid highway system was caused by severe winter storms, resulting
  in a state of emergency in Clatsop, Curry, Hood River, Lincoln, Multnomah, and Tillamook
  Counties. Beginning January 1, 2021, and continuing through January 15, 2021, these severe
  storms resulted in heavy rain, high winds, flooding, landslides, and erosion at various locations
  within these jurisdictions, leading to critical transportation failures, loss of power and
  communications capabilities, and evacuations and sheltering needs. This storm system
  damaged state highways throughout the jurisdictions with scour, washouts, sinkholes, serious
  debris flows, and mudslides.


  NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

      1.   The Oregon Department of Transportation shall provide appropriate assistance and seek
           federal resources to effect repair and reconstruction of the estimated $2,000,000 or
           more of damages and impact to the federal-aid highway system in the above identified
           jurisdictions. This declaration is limited to the above mentioned counties and is not to
           be construed as a comprehensive declaration or proclamation of emergency for other
           purposes. It is limited to the provision of assistance by the Oregon Department of
           Transportation to repair and reconstruct eligible roadways that are part of the federal-
           aid highway system in the above mentioned counties.

      2.   This order shall pertain only to the time period beginning January 1, 2021 and ending
           January 15, 2021.


           Done at Salem, Oregon, this 4" day of February, 2021.
