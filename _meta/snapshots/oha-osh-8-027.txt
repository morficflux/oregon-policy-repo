I. POLICY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) is committed to providing a safe worksite for staff,
      patients, and visitors. In addition to the provisions of this policy, OSH has adopted
      Department of Administrative Services policy 50-010-05, “Weapons in the
      Workplace”.
   B. With the exception of mace or oleoresin capsicum (OC), staff may not possess or
      use a concealed or unconcealed weapon during the course their work shift or on
      an OSH campus.
   C. Mace or OC self-defense spray, including less-than-lethal flashlights with mace
      or OC combination, must be secured away from patient-care areas in locked
      storage such as a desk or locker. Staff may transport self-defense spray to and
      from the lockable storage but may not carry it during the course of their work
      shift. They will be held liable for the cost of property damage, clean up, or injury
      to others should their self-defense spray discharge.
   D. Before entering an OSH building, law enforcement and Department of
      Corrections (DOC) transport officers must secure weapons in an area designated
      by the Security Department. Law enforcement may enter OSH with weapons
      during an emergency situation.
   E. DOC personnel may carry DOC-issued tasers and mace or OC self-defense
      spray during Forensic Evaluation Services’ high-risk evaluations.
   F. This policy should be read in conjunction with the following policies: OSH Policy
      and Procedure 8.024, “Tools and Sharp Security;” 8.044, “Contraband and
      Prohibited Items;” 8.019, “Alleged Criminal Acts and Contraband;” and 8.033,
      “Workplace Violence Response.”
   G. Staff must report a found or seen weapon on campus to Security in accordance
      with those same policies.

   H. This policy applies to all staff, including employees, volunteers, trainees, interns,
      contractors, vendors, and other state employees assigned to work at OSH. Staff
      who fail to comply with this policy or related procedures may be subject to
      disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.
   I. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services, Shared Services, and
      Oregon Health Authority policies; and relevant accreditation standards. Such
      regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this policy is more
      restrictive.
