Department of Fish and Wildlife

Chapter 635

Division 42
COLUMBIA RIVER SYSTEM COMMERCIAL FISHERIES

635-042-0180
Deep River Select Area Commercial Fishery

(1) Salmon (except Chum) may be taken with gillnets for commercial purposes during open fishing periods in Deep River as described below:

(a) During the winter, spring, and summer seasons, the Deep River Select Area is defined as waters bounded by a line at the mouth of Deep River from USCG marker “16” southwest to a marker on the Washington shore, upstream to the Highway 4 Bridge.

(b) During the fall season, the Deep River Select Area is defined as waters bounded by a line at the mouth of Deep River from USCG marker “16” southwest to a marker on the Washington shore, upstream to the West Deep River Road Bridge at the town of Deep River.

(2) Open fishing periods are established in four segments categorized as the winter season in subsection (2)(a); the spring season in subsection (2)(b); the summer season in subsection (2)(c); and the fall season in subsection (2)(d).

(a) Winter Season:

(A) Open area includes the Deep River Select Area defined in sub-section (1)(a) above. 

(B) The minimum mesh size is 7-inches.

(C) Open fishing periods are as follows:

(i) February 15 through March 20, each Sunday, Tuesday, and Thursday from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m. the following day; and

(ii) March 23 through April 14, each Monday and Thursday from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m. the following day.

(b) Spring Season:

(A) Open area includes the Deep River Select Area defined in sub-section (1)(a) above. 

(B) The maximum mesh size is 9.75-inches.

(C) Open fishing periods are as follows:

(i) April 16 through April 24, each Monday and Thursday from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m. the following day;

(ii) April 27 through May 1, each Monday, Wednesday, and Thursday from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m. the following day; and

(iii) May 4 through May 29, each Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m. the following day.

(c) Summer Season:

(A) Open area includes the Deep River Select Area defined in sub-section (1)(a) above. 

(B) The maximum mesh size is 9.75-inches.

(d) Fall Season:

(A) Open area includes the Deep River Select Area defined in sub-section (1)(b) above.  

(B) The maximum mesh size is 9.75-inches prior to the second Sunday in September, and 6-inches thereafter.

(3) Additional gear restrictions include:

(a) The maximum net length is 600 feet (100 fathoms).

(b) There is no lead line weight limit and attachment of additional weight or anchors directly to the lead line is permitted.

(c) Nets may not be tied off to stationary structures.

(d) Nets may not fully cross the navigation channel.

(e) It is unlawful to operate any gillnet gear longer than three-fourths the width of the river which is defined as bank-to-bank, where the water meets the banks, regardless of the time of tide or the water level.

(f) Nets not specifically authorized for use may be onboard a vessel if properly stored.  A properly stored net is defined as a net on a drum that is fully covered by a tarp (canvas or plastic) and bound with a minimum of ten revolutions of rope with a diameter of 3/8 (0.375) inches or greater.

Statutory/Other Authority: ORS 183.325, ORS 506.109 & ORS 506.119
Statutes/Other Implemented: ORS 506.129 & ORS 507.030
