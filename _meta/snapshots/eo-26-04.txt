     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-04



     ENSURING STATE COORDINATION FOR IMMIGRANT AND REFUGEE
     COMMUNITIES TO PROMOTE DIGNITY AND SAFETY

     WHEREAS, Oregon’s strength lies in the richness of the State’s diverse cultures,
     backgrounds, and perspectives, which together creates community, creativity, and
     innovation; and


     WHEREAS, immigrant and refugee communities are a vital part of Oregon’s
     social, economic, and cultural fabric; and

     WHEREAS, Oregon is home to approximately 404,000 immigrants, including
     19,200 immigrant children; and

     WHEREAS, more than 67,000 refugees have resettled in Oregon since 1975; and

     WHEREAS, Oregon has been a sanctuary state since 1987, affirming that state and
     local government agencies shall not use public resources to assist in federal
     immigration enforcement without a judicial warrant; and

     WHEREAS, the Oregon Legislature passed the Sanctuary Promise Act (HB 3265)
     in 2021 to strengthen and clarify protections for immigrant communities, requiring
     transparency and accountability in interactions with federal immigration
     authorities; and


     WHEREAS, federal immigration enforcement activity in Oregon has risen sharply
     since January 2025; and


     WHEREAS, increased federal immigration enforcement activity in Oregon,
     specifically tactics that contradict state law enforcement practices, has created
     heightened fear and uncertainty among immigrant and refugee communities; and

     WHEREAS, six Oregon cities have declared a state of emergency or otherwise
     acted in response to intensified federal immigration enforcement operations, and
     two Oregon counties have moved to strengthen sanctuary protections; and

     WHEREAS, Oregon’s Attorney General Dan Rayfield joined Washington and
     Multnomah County District Attorneys in a letter condemning federal immigration
     enforcement actions and threatening to sue the federal government should any
     actions violate Oregon law; and

     WHEREAS, the federal government has expanded immigration enforcement
     activity and restricted access to essential services and assistance for many
     immigrants and refugees, including those with humanitarian status; and

     WHEREAS, immigration-related developments at the federal level can create
     sudden and significant impacts on Oregon’s service systems and effective
     interagency coordination is necessary to ensure rapid and consistent responses that
     protect access to state services and community well-being; and

     WHEREAS, programs need shared protocols, consistent guidance, and
     coordinated support for families who face disruptions; and

     WHEREAS, all state agencies share responsibility for upholding Oregon’s
     commitment to diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility; and

     WHEREAS, Oregon's Office of Immigrant and Refugee Advancement (OIRA) is
     statutorily charged with coordinating immigrant and refugee immigration
     strategies, monitoring service delivery, and promoting equity across state agencies;
     and


     WHEREAS, OIRA has engaged with more than 40 community-based
     organizations since July 2025 to understand local impacts of federal immigration
     enforcement activity and to gather input on statewide needs for coordination and
     support.


     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:
       e The Interagency Council for Immigrant and Refugee Coordination (the
               “Council’) is established.
           e   The Council’s purpose is to coordinate internal agency efforts in response to
               increased federal immigration enforcement activity using existing resources
               to ensure alignment with Oregon sanctuary law and practices with the goal
               of strengthening existing state programs to support immigrant and refugee
               communities.
           e   The membership should include:
                  o The Governor, or her designee,
                  o Directors of:
                          ¢   Department of Administrative Services (DAS);

       e   The Council will be led by the Director of OIRA.
       e   The Council shall;
              o   Coordinate and identify the infrastructure necessary for sustained
                  cross-agency collaboration, including establishing consistent
                  processes and ensuring reliable communication among state
                  agencies and with communities across Oregon.
              o   Develop a partner engagement plan, in coordination with partner
                  organizations representing impacted communities, to ensure that the
                  Council’s work is informed by immigrant and refugee communities
                  on an on-going basis.
              o   Develop a proposed scope of work, including establishing
                  measurable objectives and goals to support immigrant and refugee
                  communities with a coordinated organization-wide approach no later
                  than March 31, 2026.
              o   Begin implementing a work plan to improve agency response to
                  federal immigration enforcement activity by aligning and
                  strengthening state programs using existing resources and within
                  Sanctuary Promise Law by May 30, 2026.
