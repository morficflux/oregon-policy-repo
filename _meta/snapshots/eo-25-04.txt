     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-04


     DETERMINATION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY IN CLACKAMAS,
     COOS, CURRY, DOUGLAS, HARNEY, JACKSON, JOSEPHINE, LANE,
     MULTNOMAH, AND MALHEUR COUNTIES DUE TO FLOODING AND
     LANDSLIDE INCIDENTS.


     Pursuant to ORS 401.165, I find a potential threat to life, safety, property, and
     significant damage to infrastructure exists due to flooding and landslides that have
     caused and continue to cause a state of emergency in Clackamas, Coos, Curry,
     Douglas, Harney, Jackson, Josephine, Lane, Multnomah, and Malheur counties.
     Beginning February 23, 2025, and continuing, these storms caused significant
     flooding and landslides throughout the State resulting in critical transportation
     failures and loss of power and communications capabilities.


     NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


             |.   Pursuant to ORS 401.165, and based on the facts described above, an
                  emergency has occurred, and a state of emergency is declared for
                  Clackamas, Coos, Curry, Douglas, Harney, Jackson, Josephine, Lane,
                  Multnomah, and Malheur Counties from February 23, 2025, and
                  continuing through March 17, 2025.


             2.   The Oregon Department of Transportation shall provide appropriate
                  assistance and seek federal resources to effect repair and reconstruction
                  of ten (10) million dollars or more in damages and impact to the federal
                  aid highway system in the above-listed counties.


             3.   This declaration is limited to the provision of assistance by the Oregon
                  Department of Transportation to repair and reconstruct eligible
                  roadways that are part of the federal highway aid system in the above-
                  listed counties. This declaration is not to be construed as a
                  comprehensive declaration or proclamation of emergency for other
                  purposes.
