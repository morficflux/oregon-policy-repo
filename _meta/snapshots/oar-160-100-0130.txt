 Secretary of State 
 Corporation Division - Chapter 160 
 
 
 
 
 Division 100 NOTARIES PUBLIC 
 160-100-0130 
 Use of an Embosser 
 
 (1) A notary public may use an embosser in the performance of a notarial act but only in addition to the notary public's official stamp.  (2) A notary public shall not place the embossment over any signature in a record to be notarized, or in a notarial certificate or over any writing in a notarial certificate.  (3) When a notarial certificate is on a separate piece of paper attached to the record to be notarized or when there are attachments to the record to be notarized, such as pictures, a notary public may use an embossment to mark for identification the record or attachment, if the embossment does not make any part of the record or attachment illegible.  (4) A notary public shall not use any other notary public’s embosser or any other object in lieu of the notary public’s official stamp to perform a notarial act. 
 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 194.290 & 194.360 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 194.290 & 194.360
