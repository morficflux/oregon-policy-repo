    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20-39


    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    BEACHIE CREEK AND LIONSHEAD FIRES IN MARION COUNTY


    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:


    The fire known as the “Beachie Creek Fire” and portions of the “Lionshead Fire”
    are burning in Marion County.


    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from the Beachie Creek
    and Lionshead Fires are beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and
    structural fire protection was requested by the Marion County Commissioners and
    Marion County Fire Defense Board Chief Jim Trierweiler and the State Fire
    Marshal concurs with that request.


    In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to fires known as the Beachie Creek Fire and
    Lionshead Fire in Marion County and the threat exceeds the firefighting
    capabilities of local firefighting personnel and equipment. Accordingly, I have
    invoked the Emergency Conflagration Act.


    These findings were made at 10:15 p.m. on September 7, 2020 with Office of State
    Fire Marshal and I now confirm them with this Executive Order.


    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


    lle    The Oregon State Police and the Office of State Fire Marshal shall mobilize
           fire resources statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense
           Board Chiefs for the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the
           Emergency Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources
           responding to the Beachie Creek and Lionshead Fires may be redistributed
           by the State Fire Marshal.


    2,     This conflagration is declared only for the Beachie Creek and Lionshead
           Fires threatening structures in Marion County.


    3,     This order was made by verbal proclamation at 10:15 p.m. the 7th day of
           September 2020 and signed this 8" day of September, 2020, in Salem,
           Oregon.
