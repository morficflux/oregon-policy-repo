I. SUPERVISION GUIDELINES
   A. Supervision During Episodes of Seclusion or Restraint
       1. Title If a patient is placed in seclusion (kept alone in a room) or restraint
          (held with equipment or by staff), they must always be watched 1:1 (one-
          on-one) or 2:1 (two-on-one). This is required, no matter what level of
          supervision they had before.
        2. If the patient was already on Enhanced Supervision, staff must write the
           start and end time of the seclusion or restraint on the Enhanced
           Supervision Flowsheet. While the patient is in seclusion or restraint, staff
           must also fill out the Emergency Seclusion or Restraint Flowsheet.
        3. An order for Enhanced Supervision cannot be stopped just because the
           patient was in seclusion or restraint. Once the patient is released, they
           must go back to the supervision level ordered prior to seclusion, unless the
           practitioner changes the order. Staff must return to using the Enhanced
           Supervision Flowsheet once the patient is out of seclusion or restraint.
    B. Supervision After Transfer Between Units or Facilities
        1. When a patient is first admitted or transferred from another unit, the
           practitioner must review the type and level of supervision the patient had at
           the previous unit or facility.
        2. If a patient on Enhanced Supervision is being transferred to a correctional
           facility, the social worker (or another assigned staff) must:
                  •    Tell the Sheriff’s Deputy and the medical/mental health staff at
                       the correctional facility about the patient’s supervision needs.
                  •    Give this information both verbally and in writing on the Discharge
                       Plan Details form.
                  •    Include the patient’s supervision status, the reasons for
