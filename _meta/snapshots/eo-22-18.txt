    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 22-18


    INVOCATION OF EMERGENCY CONFLAGRATION ACT FOR THE
    DOUBLE CREEK FIRE IN WALLOWA COUNTY


    Pursuant to my authority as Governor of the State of Oregon, I find that:

    The fire known as the “Double Creek Fire” is burning in Wallowa County.

    The resources necessary for protecting life and property from Double Creek Fire
    are beyond local capabilities. Assistance with life, safety, and structural fire
    protection was requested by the Wallowa County Fire Defense Board Chief and the
    State Fire Marshal concurs with that request.


    In accordance with ORS 476.510-476-610, I have determined that a threat to life,
    safety, and property exists due to the fire known as the Double Creek Fire in
    Wallowa County and the threat exceeds the firefighting capabilities of local
    firefighting personnel and equipment. Accordingly, I have invoked the Emergency
    Conflagration Act.


    These findings were made at 10:50 a.m. on September 3, 2022. The Office of State
    Fire Marshal Green Incident Management Team takes unified command on
    September 3, 2022. The Office of State Fire Marshal Green Incident Management
    Team is mobilizing and will be briefed at 8:00 p.m. on September 3, 2022, with
    transfer to unified command. I now confirm these findings with this Executive
    Order.


    NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:

    Ik       The Oregon State Police and the Office of State Fire Marshal shall mobilize
             fire resources statewide and coordinate with all appropriate Fire Defense
             Board Chiefs for the use of personnel and equipment in accordance with the
             Emergency Conflagration Act to suppress and contain this fire. Resources
             responding to the Double Creek Fire may be redistributed by the State Fire
             Marshal.


    De       This conflagration is declared only for the Double Creek Fire threatening
             structures in Wallowa County.


    3        This order was made by verbal proclamation at 10:50 a.m. on September 3,
             2022, and signed the same day in Salem, Oregon.
