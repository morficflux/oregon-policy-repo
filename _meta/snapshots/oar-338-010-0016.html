Oregon Health Authority

Health Licensing Office, Environmental Health Registration Board - Chapter 338

Division 10
REGISTRATION OF ENVIRONMENTAL HEALTH SPECIALISTS, WASTE WATER SPECIALISTS, AND TRAINEES

338-010-0016
Waste Water Specialist Application Requirements

An individual applying for a waste water specialist registration must:

(1) Meet the requirements of OAR 331 Division 30;

(2) Submit a completed application form prescribed by the Office, which must contain the information listed in OAR 331-030-0000 and be accompanied by payment of the required application fees;

(3) Provide documentation of one of the following qualification pathways:

(a) Registration Pathway 1 — Qualification through Education and Experience:

(A) Bachelor’s Degree — An applicant must submit:

(i) An official transcript, as defined in OAR 338-005-0020, demonstrating attainment of a bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university with at least 45 quarter hours or the equivalent semester hours in soil science courses; and

(ii) Proof of the equivalent of two years of experience in waste water treatment, disposal and reuse within this state under the supervision of a registered waste water specialist or equally qualified person as approved by the Board pursuant to OAR 338-010-0065(3); and

(iii) Proof of having passed the Oregon Waste Water Specialist examination as set forth in OAR 338-010-0030(2); and

(iv) Pay registration fees.

(B) Graduate Degree — An applicant must submit:

(i) An official transcript, as defined in OAR 338-005-0020, demonstrating attainment of a graduate degree in soil science from an accredited college or university; and

(ii) Proof of the equivalent of one year of experience in waste water treatment, disposal and reuse within this state under the supervision of a registered waste water specialist or by an equally qualified person pursuant to OAR 338-010-0065(3); and

(iii) Proof of having passed the Oregon Waste Water Specialist examination as set forth in OAR 338-010-0030(2); and

(iv) Pay registration fees.

(b) Registration Pathway 2 — Reciprocity. An applicant must submit:

(A) An official transcript, as defined in OAR 338-005-0020, demonstrating attainment of either a qualifying bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university with at least 45 quarter hours or equivalent semester hours in soil science courses; or

(B) An official transcript, as defined in OAR 338-005-0020, demonstrating attainment of qualifying graduate degree in soil science from an accredited college or university; and

(C) An affidavit of licensure pursuant to OAR 331-030-0040, demonstrating proof of active registration as a waste water specialist with no current or pending disciplinary action. The registration must have been issued by a regulatory body of another state; and

(D) Pay all registration fees.

(c) Registration Pathway 3 — Graduate Degree and Certification. An applicant must submit:

(A) An official transcript, as defined in OAR 338-005-0020, demonstrating attainment of qualifying graduate degree in soil science from an accredited college or university; and

(B) Proof of current certification as a professional soil scientist; and

(C) Proof of having passed the Oregon Waste Water Specialist examination as set forth in OAR 338-010-0030(2); and

(D) Pay registration fees.

Statutory/Other Authority: ORS 676.560, ORS 700.005-700.240, ORS 700.995 & Senate Bill 844 (2025)
Statutes/Other Implemented: ORS 676.560, ORS 700.005-700.240, ORS 700.995 & Senate Bill 844 (2025)
