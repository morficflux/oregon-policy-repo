 Board of Examiners for Engineering and Land Surveying 
 Chapter 820 
 
 
 
 
 
 Division 10 LICENSING 
 820-010-3020 
 Photogrammetry Examination
 
 
 (1) The Oregon approved photogrammetry examination is the Colonial States Board of Surveyor Registration (CSBSR) Examination and is offered once a year. To sit for the CSBSR Examination, a completed Examination form and required examination fee must be received by the Board office. 

 NOTE: Please contact the Board office for examination dates, times, costs, and details. 

 (2) To withdraw from the CSBSR Examination, and forward the Examination form and examination fees to the next examination administration, a written request to withdraw and forward the Examination form must be received by the Board offices no later than 45 days before the administration of the examination. Examination forms may only be forwarded upon withdrawal once, and only to the next examination administration. After one withdrawal, the original Examination form will no longer be forwarded. 

 (3) The cutoff score for the CSBSR Examination is established by the Colonial States Board of Surveyor Registration. 

 (4) Examinees may request reasonable accommodations to the examination’s administration. 

 (a) Reasonable accommodations will be provided for examinees who have a documented disability within the meaning of the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990. Reasonable accommodations may be provided for examinees whose religious convictions prohibit them from testing on the scheduled examination dates. 

 (b) Requests for reasonable accommodations must be submitted to the Board, and accompanied by supporting documentation. 

 (5) Examination Review. As the CSBSR does not facilitate examination reviews and appeals, CSBSR examination reviews are not permitted. 

 (6) Examination Subversion. 

 (a) Any Applicant who is under investigation for examination subversion, as defined in OAR 820-005-0040 will not be considered by the Board until the investigation and any ensuing disciplinary action are complete. 

 (b) An Applicant disciplined for examination subversion is subject to imposition of civil penalties and denial of registration. A Registrant who is disciplined for examination subversion is subject to imposition of civil penalties and suspension or revocation of registration. 

 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 670.310 & 672.255 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 672.002 - 672.325
