PURPOSE

This policy is for consistent management of the names of buildings on the Capitol Mall and of all Department
of Administrative Services’ (DAS) buildings for public convenience and clarity. This policy is also for exterior
signs for buildings on the Capitol Mall.

APPLICABILITY

All agencies as defined in ORS 276.004

GENERAL INFORMATION

Original or existing building names should not change except to avoid serious confusion for the public. The
name will be changed only by the Department of Administrative Services Enterprise Asset Management (DAS
EAM) or the agency owning the building, if not DAS-EAM.

A name change should only be considered if the following occurs:

(1)    The original tenant or entity for which the building was named has its legal name changed.
(2)    The building is completely and permanently converted to another use from that for which it was named.
       The original name should be retained if it has historical value unless its continued use would create
       serious confusion to the public.
(3)    Through action of the Oregon Legislature.

Requests for a specific name must be submitted in writing to DAS EAM who will coordinate with the Capitol
Planning Commission (CPC) to review the request and provide opportunity for public comment at a public
meeting according to ORS 276.054-276.062. Email facilities.helpdesk@das.oregon.gov, call 503-378-3664 or
visit the DAS facilities operations and maintenance website.

Final approval of property naming rests with the DAS director. Individuals may not commit the agency to a
building name before formal approval by the director, unless otherwise directed by the Oregon Legislature.

Guidelines:

(1)    The CPC will ensure uniformity and consistency of names on the buildings within the Capitol Mall and
       other areas in the City of Salem and will make suggestions for names when requested; act as the
       formal conduit for names to be submitted, researched and approved; notify requesters of the status
       once the DAS director has acted upon a request; and maintains records of approved building names.
(2)    The naming of a building after a prime tenant agency will be avoided because often there are multiple
       agency tenants occupying a building and when the prime tenant relocates, confusion results.
(3)    Consideration will be given to:

       a)      Specific names proposed;
       b)      Conditions, concerns and impacts of the naming. Names should use respectful terms and take
               Oregon’s main languages into account to promote understanding across a wide array of
               Oregonians.

Capitol Mall building signs:

Building name signs on the Capitol Mall and other areas in the City of Salem shall conform to the standard
design, approved by the CPC. Their purpose is to help drivers find the building they are looking for without
searching for its name and street number.

(1)    Each sign will be 5 feet high and 3.5 feet wide. It will have a powder-coated steel frame and
       background, powder-coated aluminum casting of the state seal, and building name with street address
       number in brush finished aluminum letters. Signs will include accessibility features for high contrast
       and easy reading for people with disabilities or different reading levels.
(2)    DAS EAM will install, maintain and make all changes to the standard signs.
(3)    Building name signs will only display the official name of the building or an abbreviated version thereof
       and the street number of the building. Each sign will be located on the street where the building is
       located. Signs are as follows:
