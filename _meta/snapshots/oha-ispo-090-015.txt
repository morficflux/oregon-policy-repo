Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and the
Oregon Health Authority (OHA) staff use email appropriately and safely to prevent the introduction of
computer viruses and security threats, and protect organizational data from unauthorized data
access, data loss, and loss of productivity.

Description
This policy outlines ways to ensure secure, safe, and effective use of email.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff including full-time, part-time, contingent, temporary,
and limited duration employees, volunteers, trainees, and interns.

As keepers of the public trust, all ODHS and OHA staff play a vital role in upholding state and agency
policies, administrative rules, and state and federal laws. ODHS and OHA are committed to
exceptional public and customer service. The agencies are dedicated to maintaining standards of
exceptional service and trust staff to do the same. Failure to follow policies, rules and laws may result
in beginning a personnel process to consider formal action as appropriate, including progressive
discipline up to and including dismissal from state service.

If this policy is intended to apply to contractors or grantees the necessary language must incorporated
into the contract.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA staff shall protect email by ensuring emails and attachments containing
     confidential and sensitive information are sent to authorized recipients. Authorized recipients are
     individuals or entities who need access to the information to carry out a specific, legitimate
     purpose and only to the extent necessary to fulfill that purpose
2. Federal Tax Information (FTI) shall not be sent by email or fax.
3. Criminal Justice Information (CJI) shall only be transmitted to Criminal Justice Information
     Service (CJIS)-authorized staff using secure methods such as secure file transfer or encrypted
     email.

4.      Staff using state provided email systems for messages containing sensitive and confidential
        information shall use approved encryption standards to send the email securely.
         a. Staff shall manually secure outgoing email using #secure# in the subject line.
         b. Staff shall not use the Encrypt button to secure outgoing email.1
         c. Staff shall add a sensitivity label when appropriate by using the Sensitivity button under the
             Message in Outlook2 in compliance with DAS 107-004-050 Information Asset Classification
             Policy.
         d. Sensitive or confidential information in the subject line is strictly prohibited.
5.      Use of email shall comply with DAS 107-004-110 Acceptable Use of State Information Assets.
6.      Staff shall not forward emails containing sensitive or confidential information from their state
        email accounts to their personal or non-state related email accounts.
7.      Auto-forwarding ODHS and OHA email to any email address outside of the .gov domain is
        prohibited and can only be configured by an administrator.
8.      Suspicious emails or emails suspected to be phishing attempts shall be reported immediately
        using the Phish Alert Report button.
9.      If Criminal Justice Information (CJI), Federal Tax Information (FTI), Payment Card Industry
        (PCI), Protected Health Information (PHI), or Social Security Administration (SSA) data is
        accidentally disclosed by email or fax, staff are required to report the incident immediately upon
        identification in accordance with ODHSOHA 090-005-01 Information Security Incident Reporting
        Process.
