     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-01


     EXTENSION OF EXECUTIVE ORDER 24-02


     WHEREFORE: In response to significant increases in unsheltered homelessness
     in the State of Oregon, Governor Kotek declared a homelessness state of
     emergency on January 10, 2023, in Continuum of Care regions that experienced an
     increase in unsheltered homelessness of 50% or greater between 2017 and 2022.


     On March 31, 2023, the Governor issued EO 23-09, which expanded the
     homelessness state of emergency declared in Executive Order 23-02 to include
     Clatsop, Linn and Malheur counties. These three counties in the Balance of State
     Continuum of Care requested to be included and demonstrated that they met the
     criteria for inclusion.


     On January 9, 2024, the Governor issued EO 24-02, which merged and extended
     EO 23-02 and EO 23-09.


     On January 9, 2025, at the time of this emergency declaration, the most recent
     complete statewide Point in Time Count with data about both sheltered and
     unsheltered homelessness continues to be from January 2023. Updated complete
     statewide Point in Time Count data will not be available until the completion ofthe
     2025 Point in Time Count.


     The 2023 Point in Time Count estimated that:

         NOW THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED THAT:


         There is a state of emergency in the emergency areas due to unsheltered
         homelessness. The emergency areas include:


.                    e   Metro region Continuums of Care’;
                     e   Central Oregon;
                     e   Eugene, Springfield/Lane County;
                     e   Medford, Ashland/Jackson County;
                     e   Salem/Marion, Polk Counties;
                     e   Linn County;
                     e   Clatsop County; and
                     e   Malheur County.


         I declare this emergency for these areas because they have experienced an increase
         in unsheltered homelessness of 50% or greater between 2017 and 2022, or met the
         opt-in criteria for counties in the Balance of State CoC in EO 23-09. Areas shall
         maintain previously achieved measurable goals established by EO 23-02, EO 23-
         09, and EO 24-02 including homelessness prevention, rehousing, and additional
         shelter capacity.


         Oregon Department of Emergency Management, Oregon Housing and Community
         Services, Oregon Health Authority, and Executive State Agencies shall continue to
         take the actions to address the state of emergency as previously described and
         directed in EO 24-02.
