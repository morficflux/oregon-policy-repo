I.     PURPOSE

       The purpose of this policy is to establish appropriate guidelines and procedures for the receipt
       and search of Native American medicine bags.

II.    DEFINITIONS

       A.   Medicine Bag: A small leather pouch secured by a leather strap, which secures the bag
            around the neck of the owner. The medicine bag is worn around the neck under the shirt
            or placed in the pocket. The bag may contain a variety of sacred object; such as, small
            stones, numerous herbal compounds, small bones or teeth, a very small amount of
            tobacco, or small tufts of animal fur or hair.

III.   POLICY

       It is the policy of DOC to allow inmates to practice their faith traditions without jeopardizing the
       safety and security of the facility. Further, it is the policy of DOC that all staff show courtesy
       and respect for each faith tradition practiced by inmates in DOC facilities.

       A.   Transport and Receipt of Medicine Bags

            1.     Inmates who are adherents of the Native American faith tradition are authorized to
                   possess one medicine bag. The medicine bag is a sacred object. Thus staff should
                   not touch the bag or its contents during a search, except as absolutely necessary
                   to ensure the safety and security of the facility.

            2.     Medicine bags may be introduced into the facility only by the facility chaplain or by
                   recognized DOC volunteers of the Native American faith tradition. The facility
                   chaplain shall provide written authorization for an approved medicine bag on an
                   inmate’s property list. All medicine bags will be searched according to the
                   procedures in this policy prior to being given to the inmate recipient.

            3.     An inmate placed in disciplinary segregation may keep his/her medicine bag, but
                   staff shall ensure the thong is placed in the inmate’s property while the inmate is in
                   disciplinary segregation.

            4.     When an inmate is transferred to another facility, the medicine bag shall be
                   transported with the inmate’s property. After the inmate is in receipt of the medicine

      B.   Procedures for Searching Medicine Bags

           1.      In accordance with the DOC rules on Religious and Spiritual Activities (OAR 291-
                   143) and Searches (Institutions) (OAR 291-041), searches of inmate religious
                   activity areas and religious or spiritual items shall be conducted in a manner that
                   reflects an awareness for and sensitivity to individual religious or spiritual beliefs,
                   practices, and with respect for the objects or symbols used in the religious practice.

           2.      Medicine bags may be searched only by authorized DOC staff. Since the medicine
                   bag is sacred, staff should avoid touching the bag and its contents during a search.
                   If staff handle the medicine bag or its contents at any time during a search, it shall
                   be documented.

           3.      Staff will escort the inmate to a private indoor location to complete the search. Staff
                   shall direct the inmate to empty the contents of the bag onto a clean sheet of paper
                   to ensure adequate visual inspection.

           4.      Staff shall direct the inmate to turn the bag inside out to permit a thorough view of
                   the bag’s interior surfaces. When the search is complete, staff shall direct the inmate
                   to replace the contents of the bag.

           5.      If contraband is found, the medicine bag and its contents will be confiscated. If
                   appropriate, the inmate may be issued a misconduct report, in accordance with the
                   rule on Prohibited Inmate Conduct and Processing Disciplinary Actions (OAR
                   291-105).

           6.      If an inmate refuses to allow a search of his/her medicine bag, staff shall
                   immediately escort the inmate to a location isolated from other inmates. The
                   medicine bag and its contents will be confiscated. If appropriate, the inmate may be
                   issued a misconduct report, in accordance with the rule on Prohibited Inmate
                   Conduct and Processing Disciplinary Actions (OAR 291-105).

IV.   IMPLEMENTATION

      This policy will be adopted immediately without further modification.
