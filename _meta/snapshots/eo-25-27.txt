    EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 25-27



    DECLARING A HUNGER EMERGENCY TO PROTECT OREGONIANS FROM
    THE SUSPENSION OF            FEDERAL SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION ASSISTANCE
    PROGRAM (SNAP)


    WHEREAS, under ORS 401.165, the Governor of Oregon may declare a state of
    emergency by proclamation upon determining that an emergency has occurred or is
    imminent; and


    WHEREAS, on October 10, 2025, the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)
    Secretary Brooke Rollins, appointed by President Donald J. Trump, directed states to halt
    processing November Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits due
    to the federal government shutdown; and


    WHEREAS, this directive immediately jeopardizes food access for 757,000
    Oregonians—one in six residents—including 210,000 children and 130,000 seniors
    statewide and threatens loss of life, injury to persons and human suffering; and

    WHEREAS, the interruption of SNAP benefits will significantly increase hunger and
    food insecurity across Oregon, disproportionately impacting vulnerable populations such
    as children, seniors, individuals with disabilities, and those with access and functional
    needs; and


    WHEREAS, on October 27, 2025, the Governor of Oregon, Secretary of State Tobias
    Read, Attorney General Dan Rayfield, State Treasurer Elizabeth Steiner, Labor
    Commissioner Christina Stephenson, Senate President Rob Wagner, House Speaker Julie
    Fahey, Senate Majority Leader Kayse Jama, House Majority Leader Ben Bowman, and
    House and Senate budget Co-Chairs Senator Kate Lieber and Representative Tawna
    Sanchez sent a letter to USDA Secretary Rollins to demand available contingency funds
    be used to continue SNAP benefits; and


    WHEREAS, on October 28, 2025, Oregon Attorney General Dan Rayfield joined 21
    other states in filing a lawsuit against the USDA and its Secretary for unlawfully
    suspending SNAP benefits; and


    WHEREAS, the State of Oregon must act swiftly to mitigate the effects of the
    suspension of SNAP benefits, and provide essential nutrition assistance for Oregonians in
    every corner of the state;


    NOW, THEREFORE, I, Tina Kotek, Governor of the State of Oregon, by virtue of the
    authority vested in me under ORS 401.165, do hereby proclaim that a statewide food
    insufficiency emergency exists across Oregon, effective immediately.

    This declaration authorizes the Oregon Department of Emergency Management to
    coordinate and direct other state agencies to take all necessary actions to respond to and
    mitigate the impacts of this emergency, including coordination with local governments,
    nonprofit organizations, and private sector partners


    NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


        1.   Oregon Department of Emergency Management (OEM). OEM shall activate the
             State’s Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan as needed to coordinate
             necessary access to use of personnel and equipment of all state agencies to assess,
             alleviate, respond to, mitigate, and recover from conditions caused by this
             emergency. OEM coordination efforts may include as necessary, but not be
             limited to:
                 a.   Management of information requirements to inform statewide operations
                      to OEM identified State Policy Group;
                b.    Coordination of essential protective measures requested by the Oregon
                      Department of Human Services (ODHS) to address food insecurity and
                      alleviate human suffering.
       2.    Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS). ODHS shall, in coordination
             with OEM and local government, provide supplemental services, supplies, and
             equipment to local governments and community-based organizations to provide
             for the health and safety of affected residents and mitigate human suffering from
             conditions caused by this emergency. ODHS efforts shall:
                 a. Coordinate with Oregon’s human services and food security organizations
                     to identify strategies to address unmet hunger needs.
                 b. Provide available supplemental financial assistance to address food
                     insecurity impacts due to the loss of SNAP benefits.
                 c. Coordinate communication with state agencies, local governments and
                      food security organizations regarding state actions.
                d. Provide situational updates through the Oregon Department of Emergency
                   Management regarding operations executed to address food insecurity
                      impacts.


       3. All Other State or Other Agencies. All state or other public or quasi-public
          agencies are directed to provide resources and support as directed by OEM to
             assist in the response to this emergency as requested.
