     EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 26-09


     DETERMINATION OF A STATE OF DROUGHT EMERGENCY IN
     DOUGLAS, GILLIAM, HARNEY, LAKE and MALHEUR COUNTIES.


     At the request of the Burns Paiute Tribe (through Burns Paiute Tribal Council
     Resolution 2026-06 and letter dated May 8, 2026), Douglas County (through
     Douglas County Board of Commissioners Order 2026-0375, dated April 29, 2026),
     Gilliam County (through Gilliam County Court Resolution R2026-05, dated May 6,
     2026), Harney County (through Harney County Court Resolution 2026-16, dated
     May 6, 2026), Lake County (through Lake County Board of Commissioners
     Resolution 2026-01, dated May 5, 2026), and Malheur County (through Malheur
     County Court Declaration R26-19, dated May 27, 2026) and based on the
     recommendations ofthe Drought Readiness Council and input from the Water
     Supply Availability Committee dated May 20, 2026, and pursuant to ORS 536.740,
     I find that and moderate and severe drought conditions in Harney, Gilliam, Lake
     and Malheur Counties and moderate, severe and extreme drought conditions in
     Douglas County as well as the conditions described below will cause natural and
     economic disaster conditionsin Douglas, Gilliam, NOLEN Lake and Malheur
     Counties (collectively “Counties”).


        1.   Above normal temperature conditions, below normal snowpack conditions,
             below normal precipitation conditions and long-term precipitation deficits,
             below normal streamflow conditions, and drier-than-normal soil moisture
             conditions in Douglas County.

         Seasonal climate and drought outlooks indicate that drought conditions in the
         Counties are likely to persist. Drought is likely to have a significant economic
         impact on the agricultural, livestock, natural resource and recreational tourism
         sectors in the Counties.


         Conditions continue to be monitored by the State’s natural resource and public
         safety agencies, including the Oregon Water Resources Department, the Oregon
         Department of Emergency Management, the Oregon Office of the State Fire
         Marshal, and the Oregon Department of Forestry’s Fire Protection Division.


         Preparation and resiliency to drought are vital to the health and safety of persons,
         property, and the economic security of the citizens and businesses of these
         Counties. I, therefore, declare that a severe, continuing drought emergency exists
         and is likely to continue to exist in Douglas, Gilliam, Harney, Lake and Malheur
         Counties.


         NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY DIRECTED AND ORDERED:


            Il,      The Oregon Department of Agriculture is directed to coordinate and
                     provide assistance in seeking federal resources to mitigate drought
                     conditions and assist in agricultural recovery in the Counties.


            Il.      The Oregon Water Resources Department and the Water Resources
                     Commission are directed to coordinate and provide assistance to water
                     users in the Counties as the Department and Commission determine
                     necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700 to 536.780.

            Ill.   | The Oregon Water Resources Department is directed to seek
                     information from the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife to help
                     understand the impacts of water availability on Oregon’s fish and
                     wildlife, as necessary and appropriate in accordance with ORS 536.700
                     to 536.780.


            IV.      The Oregon Department of Emergency Management is directed to
|                    coordinate and assist as needed with assessment and mitigation
                     activities to address current and projected conditions in the Counties.
