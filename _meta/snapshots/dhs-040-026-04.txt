 Guidelines

 Title:             ODHS-040-026-04 Donated Funds Reporting


 Related to:        ODHS-040-026 Donated Funds, Goods, and Services


 Effective date:    02/06/2023


Purpose

This document outlines the appropriate process for reporting donated funds, goods, and services.
Donated funds are cash or in-kind goods and services donated to ODHS and used to benefit the
agency’s mission of assisting individuals to become independent, healthy, and safe.

Guidelines

1. The donated funds coordinator, manager or designee maintains three distinct recording logs in
   each office, one each for:
   A. Money donations.
   B. Stored value cards and ticket donations.
   C. In-kind donations and services.
2. Staff authorized to accept or disburse donations immediately record all transactions in the correct
   log in accordance with policy ODHS-040-026.
3. OFS Grant Reporting unit maintains a list of individuals responsible for overseeing donated funds
   and provides a summary donated funds report to each designated manager, donated funds
   coordinator or both, on a quarterly basis. The summary of donated funds report is broken out by
   district and includes:
   A. Expenditures.
   B. Receipts.
   C. Interest income.
4. Designated managers monitor disbursements to ensure disbursements do not exceed revenue for
   any district or program area.
5. Donated fund managers audit and reconcile donated funds, goods, and services on a quarterly
   basis.
6. If donated funds are intermingled between areas, the designated manager or donated funds
   coordinator requests a correction by contacting OFS Grant Reporting.
