 Oregon Health Authority 
 Public Health Division - Chapter 333 
 
 
 
 
 Division 7 MARIJUANA AND HEMP TESTING 
 333-007-0390 
 Standards for Microbiological Contaminants Compliance Testing
 
 
 (1) A marijuana item or industrial hemp-derived vapor item required to be tested for microbiological contaminants under OAR 333-007-0320 to 333-007-0342 must be sampled using appropriate aseptic technique and tested by a laboratory for Shiga toxin-producing Escherichia coli and Salmonella species. 

 (2) A batch fails microbiological contaminants testing if, during an initial test where no reanalysis is requested or upon reanalysis as described in OAR 333-007-0450(1) the laboratory detects: 

 (a) Shiga toxin-producing Escherichia coli in one gram; or 

 (b) Salmonella spp. in one gram. 

 (3) Marijuana items and industrial hemp-derived vapor items tested for Aspergillus on or before August 25, 2023, in accordance with the rules that were in effect at the time, that have a laboratory test report indicating the samples contained pathogenic Aspergillus flavus, A. fumigatus, A. niger or A. terreus that were remediated or were attempted to be remediated must be resampled and retested in accordance with OAR 333-007-0450(6)(c) but are not required to be tested for pathogenic Aspergillus flavus, A. fumigatus, A. niger or A. terreus. 

 (4) A marijuana item or industrial hemp-derived vapor item tested for Aspergillus on or before August 25, 2023, in accordance with the rules that were in effect at the time, that have a laboratory test report indicating the samples contained pathogenic Aspergillus flavus, A. fumigatus, A. niger or A. terreus, is not considered to have failed a compliance test for the purposes of OAR 333-007-0450. 

 
 
 
 
 Statutory/Other Authority:  ORS 475C.544 
 
 
 Statutes/Other Implemented:  ORS 475C.544
