PURPOSE

This procedure outlines the timeline of tasks Executive Branch agencies must accomplish to comply with
Statewide Policy 107-004-155 (Covered Products and Vendors).

APPLICABILITY

This policy applies to any board, commission, department, division, office, or other entity of state government,
as defined in ORS 174.111, except the Secretary of State or State Treasurer.

ATTACHMENT

1) Sample law enforcement need email
2) Sample Section 2 compliance email
3) Sample Section 4 compliance email

DEFINITIONS

Please refer to policy 107-004-155 for definitions.

PROCEDURE

 RESPONSIBILITY           STEP     ACTION
 State CIO                   1     Determine changes to the covered vendor list.

 EIS - Cyber Security        2     Update the covered vendor list.
 Services (CSS)
                             3     Communicate covered vendor list changes to agencies and partners, and
                                   post the list on the Enterprise Information Services Covered Vendors page
                                   Enterprise Information Services: Covered Vendors.

 Agencies                    4     Determine whether covered vendor(s)/product(s) are used, accessed,
                                   downloaded, or installed by the agency’s information technology assets,
                                   using methods such as asset inventory review and querying in
                                   collaboration with CSS, and proceed with either Step 4a or 4b.

                            4a     Absent a law enforcement/investigatory need, agency:

                                   Within 30 days, removes all covered vendor products and implements
                                   measures to prevent their use as prescribed in Policy 107-004-155.

                                   Go to Step 5.

 Agency IT Leadership       4b     With an identified law enforcement and/or investigatory need, agency:

                                   Within 30 days, discloses justification to CSS via email to
                                   das_dl_oscio_css_risk_management_team@das.oregon.gov.

                                   (CSS will track the justification in the enterprise risk register for current and
                                   future assessments.)

                                   Go to Step 6

 Agency IT Leadership        5     Agency confirms compliance with Policy 107-004-155 Section 2 with CSS
                                   assessment team via email to
                                   das_dl_oscio_css_risk_management_team@das.oregon.gov.

                                   (CSS will track the compliance in the enterprise risk register for current and
                                   future assessments.)

 Agencies                    6     Within 120 days, agency incorporates Policy 107-004-155 into the agency’s
                                   Information Technology (IT) security plans and operations.
 Agency IT Leadership        7     Agency confirms compliance with Policy 107-004-155 Section 4 with CSS
                                   assessment team via email to
                                   das_dl_oscio_css_risk_management_team@das.oregon.gov.

                                   (CSS will track the compliance in the enterprise risk register for current and
                                   future assessments.)

 CSS and Agencies            8     CSS works with agencies to validate compliance with requirements and
                                   includes the results in its assessment report and executive summary for
                                   the agency director, policy area Assistant State CIO and CSS leadership.

 RESPONSIBILITY           STEP     ACTION
                             9     If agency is not in compliance, CSS creates a finding in the enterprise risk
                                   register; the agency is notified of non-compliant status; and the State CIO
                                   decides on remediation and/or consequence. Return to Step 4.

                                   If agency is in compliance, CSS documents status in the enterprise risk
                                   register.

 CSS                        10     CSS monitors for compliance via the enterprise risk register.
