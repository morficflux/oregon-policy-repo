PURPOSE

This policy identifies the minimum internal controls for managing the use of an agency’s Mobile
Communications Devices (MCDs).

APPLICABILITY

All Executive Branch agencies under direct authority of the Governor. Other agencies may follow this policy at
their option.

DEFINITIONS

Access Account: The account of each access number for voice/data transmission provided by a mobile
communications provider.

Authorized User: The person or entity authorized to use the access number on the Access Account.

Mobile Communication Device (MCD): A wireless or cellular, two-way communication device designed to
receive and transmit voice or text communication including mobile Global Positioning System (GPS) device
and/or application data. This policy applies to any Mobile Communication Device that requires the purchase of
mobile communication services by the state. MCD Excludes devices or any equipment that while capable of
connecting to a cellular network is not designed for direct human communication or interaction (e.g. roadside
temperature sensor, or controller for emergency exit lighting).

Mobile Device Management: Software that allows IT administrators to control, secure, and enforce policies on
smartphones, tablets, and other endpoints.

Plan Charge: The charge assessed by a data or voice provider for each Access Account.

Text: A Short Messaging Service (SMS), Multimedia Messaging Services (MMS) iMessage, or any other written
form of message using an MCD such as a cell phone, smart phone, tablet, or GPS device.

Short Messaging Services (SMS): Commonly referred to as texting or text messaging. This is a technology for
sending short messages between MCDs.

GENERAL INFORMATION

General use at work:
   • MCDs are assigned to authorized users in order to carry out their work while conducting state business.
   • MCDs assigned to authorized users only for business use except in case of an emergency when a
      personal device is not available. When assigned MCDs are utilized beyond emergency use, the agency
      may seek reimbursement through payroll deduction.
   • MCDs are to be protected by the authorized user, used in an appropriate manner, and returned when
      employment ends.
   • Plan charges for MCDs issued to authorized users will be monitored, allowing an employee’s manager
      to determine excessive or irregular use and resulting consequences.
   • Use of the camera function on camera-enabled MCDs is prohibited in spaces where there is reasonable
      expectation of privacy such as restrooms, locker rooms, and lactation rooms.
   • Use of the camera function on camera-enabled MCDs of Limited, Restricted, and Critical Information is
      prohibited. (Limited, Restricted and Critical as defined in Policy 107-004-050, Information Asset
      Classification.)
   • Use of the camera function on camera-enabled MCDs of locations considered to be Protected Critical
      Infrastructure Information (PCII) is limited to those certified to handle PCII.
   • State-owned MCDs will utilize Mobile Device Management software approved by Enterprise Information
      Services.
