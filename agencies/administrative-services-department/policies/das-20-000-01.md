POLICY STATEMENT
It is the goal of Oregon state government to achieve an equitable relationship between the comparability of the
value of work performed by state employees and the compensation and classification structure of state
government’s system.

APPLICABILITY
Classified and management service employees.

ATTACHMENTS
None

DEFINITIONS
Refer to State HR Policy 10.000.01, Definitions.

POLICY
The CHRO shall adopt and maintain a statewide classification plan. Accordingly:

    (1) The CHRO and agencies shall be jointly responsible to group jobs into broad, occupationally based
        statewide classes within the plan whenever possible.

    (2) The CHRO and agencies shall work to reduce the total number of classes consistent with good
        management practices and ORS 240.190 and ORS 243.650 to 243.782.

    (3) Classes of work shall be discrete and internally consistent.

    (4) All class specifications shall conform to CHRO content and format requirements, be supported by
        proper documentation and be approved by the CHRO.

    (5) An agency may request a classification plan change from the CHRO. The request shall include a
        statement of the origin of the problem, a general assessment of its scope, and recommend a solution
        including how the proposed action will resolve the problem.

   (6) The CHRO and agencies shall be jointly responsible for maintaining comparability and consistency in
       classification matters when establishing new classes or modifying the existing class plan.

   (7) Subject to final review and approval, the CHRO may delegate the revision of an existing class or series
       or the development of a new class or series to an agency or group of agencies. As guided by the CHRO,
       the agency or group of agencies will complete the necessary classification work.
