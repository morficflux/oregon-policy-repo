 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) is committed to providing patient-centered care in a
      safe, humane environment where a patient’s rights are protected. This policy
      outlines if and when rights may need to be restricted, and responsibilities of staff
      in these situations.
   B. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
   A. A patient’s rights may only be restricted for safety, security, or other clinically
      necessary reasons. A patient’s rights cannot be restricted as punitive measures,
      nor may their rights be considered privileges to be earned. Restrictions to their
      rights may only be implemented as delineated in this policy and within
      boundaries established by federal or state regulations for good cause, meaning
      sufficient reason to take action. In this policy, good cause for restricting a
      patient’s rights includes determining:
      1. the exercise of the specific right would be injurious to the patient or others;
      2. there is evidence that the specific right, if exercised, would seriously infringe
         on rights of others;
      3. the patient is engaging in illegal behaviors; or
      4. a restriction is considered necessary for the safety and security of OSH.

  B. Staff will use the least restrictive means of addressing the behavior that led to
     considering a restriction. Before implementing a restriction to a patient’s right,
     staff must offer or try alternative interventions.
  C. Any restriction to a patient’s right must be:
        1. clinically justified for good cause,
        2. ordered by a psychiatrist or psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner
           (PMHNP),
        3. explained to the patient within 24 hours of the restriction, and
        4. documented in progress notes.
  D. The reason for a restriction must be related to the right being restricted. A
     restriction must be lifted as soon as possible and may not be in effect longer than
     necessary for clinical or safety/security reasons.
  E. A member of the Interdisciplinary Treatment Team (IDT) must promptly notify a
     patient verbally and in writing whenever a right is restricted and when the IDT
     reviews and extends the restriction. Patient right restriction notifications identified
     in Attachment A must be documented on a “Patient Notification Rights
     Restriction” form 76126 MR-1. Exceptions to written notification include
     enhanced supervision and seclusion or restraint.
  F. When a restriction is established, the IDT must develop an individualized plan to
     reinstate the patient’s rights and document it in their treatment care plan (TCP).
  G. Documentation of any restriction must include:
        1. the clinical justification;
        2. assessment of the need for the restriction;
        3. alternative interventions attempted;
        4. the date and time the restriction was established;
        5. the date the restriction will be reviewed (no more than 30 days after the
           restriction was implemented);
        6. the reason for continuing the restriction, if applicable;
        7. a plan for reinstating the patient’s right;
        8. when indicated, a completed, approved “Restriction of Patient Rights
           Approval and Notification” form 76125 MR-1 ; and
        9. when the patient was notified verbally and in writing of the restriction.
  H. For a restriction to be established or extended, staff must promptly obtain
     appropriate approvals from the OSH Ombuds and Family Services Office (OFS),
     Program Director, and other appropriate persons per the “Restriction of Patient
     Rights Approval and Notification” form.

        NOTE: Exceptions to this process include enhanced supervision and seclusion or
        restraint.
        1. The IDT must review and reconsider the restriction at least monthly while it is
           in effect. If the IDT extends the restriction, they must promptly email
           restriction extension notifications at 30 days and 60 days after the restriction
           started. Each notification must include the justification for the restriction or
           restriction extension and a revised restriction plan.
        2. If the IDT determines that the restriction should continue past 90 days, they
           must obtain approval from the Program Executive Team and OFS before
           continuing the restriction. When the “Restriction of Patient Rights Approval
           and Notification” form is required, the IDT must submit an updated form, the
           justification for the restriction, and a revised restriction plan at 90 days
           afterwards and again at every 30-day extension request.
  I. In an emergency where there is significant risk of harm to the patient or others, a
     registered nurse (RN) may establish an emergency temporary restriction if it is:
        1. clinically justified for good cause;
        2. ordered by a psychiatrist/PMHNP;
        3. promptly explained to the patient verbally and in writing; and
        4. documented, including:
           a. the clinical justification,
           b. assessment of the need for the restriction,
           c. alternative interventions attempted,
           d. the date and time the restriction was established, and
           e. the date the IDT will review the restriction (the next business day).
  J. An emergency temporary restriction must be lifted as soon as possible and may
     not be in effect longer than clinically necessary.
        1. The emergency temporary restriction must be promptly reviewed by the
           patient’s IDT the next business day. In no situation may it be in effect beyond
           the IDT’s review.
        2. The IDT must complete requirements listed in this policy to extend the
           restriction. Before an emergency temporary restriction listed on the
           “Restriction of Patient Rights Approval and Notification” form can continue,
           the IDT must promptly submit a request to OFS and the Program Director as
           directed by this policy to develop a plan and obtain their approval.
        3. See applicable policies for enhanced supervision and seclusion or restraint
           processes.

   K. In certain circumstances the Superintendent, Administrator on Duty, or Chief
      Medical Officer may establish a temporary blanket restriction for all patients for
      medical or other safety reasons with good cause.
         1. Unit staff must verbally inform patients of the reason for the blanket restriction
            and when the restriction will end. They must post the written information in an
            area where patients may view it (e.g., “It is over 90 degrees outside, so we
            cannot go to the quads until it cools off”).
         2. In circumstances where a blanket restriction is implemented for medical or
            safety reasons, a psychiatrist/PMHNP order is not required for each patient.
            When in effect, IDTs are not required to document individual plans to
            reinstate patient rights in TCPs or submit “Restriction of Patient Rights
            Approval and Notification” forms.
   L. Staff, including OSH leadership, will involve or consult with OFS on all patient
      rights issues, including clarification and interpretation of patient rights, and before
      implementing related process or policy changes.
   M. If a patient right authorized by federal or state law is restricted, OFS will inform
      the Superintendent as needed.
   N. This policy does not apply to situations where something which is not a federal or
      state patient right is restricted, such as restricting a patient from going to the
      dining room.
   O. Only the Superintendent or their designee may make an exception to this policy
      for unusual or emergent circumstances.
   P. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   Q. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
      may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Interdisciplinary treatment team (IDT)” includes the attending
      psychiatrist/psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner (PMHNP), registered
      nurse (RN), psychologist, social worker, mental health therapist MHT or case
      monitor, treatment care plan specialist (TCPS), and any other clinical staff
      member who provides active treatment to the patient. The IDT is responsible to
      coordinate in planning and overseeing a patient’s care and treatment.
   B. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).

      needs.
