# Privacy Policy (ODHS|OHA Policy 100-001)

## Full text

                                    OFFICE OF INFORMATION SERVICES
                                   Information Security and Privacy Office

 Operational Policy
 Policy title:        Privacy Policy

 Policy number:       ODHS|OHA 100-001

 Original date:       09/20/2013 (OHA only)              Last update:      (Joint ODHS|OHA) 01/10/2022
                      Kris Kautz, Deputy Director Oregon Health Authority
 Approved:            Don Erickson, Chief Administrative Officer Oregon Department of Human
                      Services

Purpose
This policy is one of a series that outlines Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and
Oregon Health Authority (OHA) expectations for the necessary collection, use, and disclosure of
protected health information about individuals in order to provide services and benefits while
maintaining reasonable safeguards to protect the privacy of their information.

Description
This policy establishes a framework for compliance and responsibility regarding privacy protections of
ODHS|OHA.

Applicability
This policy applies to all OHA staff (see OAR 943-014-0015) and ODHS staff working in Business
Associate programs (see ODHS 100-013) including employees, volunteers, interns and agency
contractors. This definition of staff aligns with the HIPAA definition of workforce.

As keepers of the public trust, ODHS|OHA workforce has a responsibility to comply with state and
agency policies, administrative rule, and state and federal law. ODHS|OHA takes this responsibility
seriously and failure to fulfill this responsibility is not treated lightly. Individuals who fail to comply with
state or agency policy, administrative rule, or state and federal law may face progressive discipline,
up to and including dismissal from state service.

Policy
1. ODHS|OHA shall implement controls and processes to limit the collection, use, disclosure and
   storage of protected information to the minimum necessary information to accomplish the required
   business goals.
2. ODHS|OHA staff shall safeguard protected information consistent with federal and state statutes,
   rules, and regulations.
3. ODHS|OHA staff shall follow all agency policies and procedures regarding the confidentiality and
   security of protected information.

4. ODHS|OHA staff shall make reasonable efforts to use or disclose only the minimum amount of
   information necessary to provide services and benefits and only to the extent provided for in
   federal and state statutes, rules and regulations, and Agency policies and procedures.
5. ODHS|OHA staff shall complete security awareness training on an annual basis. This training
   outlines individual roles and responsibilities in protecting the privacy and security of the Agency’s
   protected information.
6. ODHS|OHA departments and programs shall implement this privacy policy by:
   a. Assigning and delegating responsible parties for adherence and implementation to this policy.
   b. Providing the policy or making the policy available wherever possible.
   c. Distribution of the policy to each new employee, contractor or to anyone who will access
       confidential and protected information.
7. If a conflict between policies or governing regulations arises, ODHS|OHA staff will consult with the
   Privacy Compliance Officer or a representative of the Information Security and Privacy Office to
   resolve the conflict.
