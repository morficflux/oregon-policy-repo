# Information Security Risk Assessment Policy (ODHS|OHA Policy 090-006)

Operational Policy
Policy title: Information Security Risk Assessment Policy

Policy number: ODHSOHA 090-006

Original policy date: 03/11/2015

Last update: 07/01/2026

Approved by: Kris Kautz, OHA and Seth Lyon, ODHS

Purpose
The Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and Oregon Health
Authority (OHA) are committed to ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and
availability of information assets and systems by safeguarding their physical
security and protecting those assets from unauthorized access, modification,
destruction, or disclosure. The purpose of an information security risk
assessment (ISRA) is to identify and assess the threats and vulnerabilities that
pose a risk to ODHS and OHA information technology assets.

Description
This policy ensures that ODHS and OHA information assets and systems are
assessed for potential risks and vulnerabilities so the agencies can maintain the
confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all protected information.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff, including full-time, part-time,
contingent, temporary, and limited duration employees, volunteers, trainees,
and interns. The requirements of this policy also apply to contractors, grantees,
and partners in their delivery of goods or services to or on behalf of either
agency, as required under the applicable contract, grant, or other form of
agreement.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA shall develop, implement, and maintain an information
   security risk assessment program that identifies threats and vulnerabilities to
   information assets and systems including all forms of physical and digital
   media.
    a. Information assets include any knowledge that can be communicated or
       documentary material, regardless of its physical form or characteristics,
       that has value to the organization.1
    b. Information systems are a discrete set of information resources organized
       for the collection, processing, maintenance, use, sharing, dissemination,
       or disposition of information.2
    c. Information systems include computers, hardware, software, storage
       media, networks, operational procedures, and processes used in the
       collection, processing, storage, sharing or distribution of information
       within the state’s shared computing and network infrastructure, beyond
       ordinary public access.
    d. Digital media is any form of electronic media designed to store data in a
       digital format.
2. The Information Security and Privacy Office (ISPO) shall manage and
   coordinate information security risk assessments of agency assets to
   evaluate how controls protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of

  these assets, as required by law, the ODHS and OHA Agency Information
  Security Plan, and based on the information assets contained in each
  system.
3. ISPO shall use information security risk assessments to advise the agency on
   appropriate measures to safeguard against identified risks, in accordance
   with federal and state statutes and rules as well as program requirements.
4. An information security risk assessment (ISRA) identifies and assesses
   threats and vulnerabilities that pose a risk to ODHS and OHA information
   assets, supports the management of risk, and guides selection of cost-
   effective controls. ISRAs shall include an evaluation of the following:
  a. Managerial controls.
  b. Operational controls.
  c. Technical controls.
  d. Compliance requirements.
  e. Effects on privacy and related safeguards, as necessary.
5. Information security risk assessments shall be performed:
  a. As directed based on the statutory and regulatory requirements.
  b. When initiated by the Chief Information Officer (CIO) or designee, or Chief
     Information Risk Officer (CIRO).
  c. At the request of the agency program director or designee.
  d. At the request of the information system owner who is accountable for
     the information created, transmitted, received, or processed by a specific
     system.
  e. When there are significant changes to the information system, the
     environment of operation, or other conditions that may impact the
     security of the system.
  f. To assess the agency’s security posture following a security incident, as
     directed.

6. ISPO shall communicate the results of information security risk assessments
   to the following staff for inclusion in the agencies’ risk evaluation and
   management plans:
  a. CIO.
  b. CIRO.
  c. Privacy Compliance Officer.
  d. Agency program director.
  e. Information system owner.
  f. Chief Audit Executive for ODHS and OHA.
  g. Local Agency Security Officer if assessment is related to criminal justice
     information.
  h. Other internal regulatory liaisons if applicable.
  i.   All ODHS and OHA staff identified as supporting the risk management
       program shall cooperate with the information security risk assessment
       process.
