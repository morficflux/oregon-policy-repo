 YOUR TRACK CHANGES, CONTACT OSH.POLICIESANDPROCEDURES@DHSOHA.STATE.OR.US.

I. SCHOOL ENROLLMENT FOR EDUCATIONAL SERVICE DISTRICT (ESD)
   A. Within five school days, the education specialist shall offer educational services and
      enroll to new patients meeting eligibility criteria.
   B. Classes shall be scheduled if a patient would like to attend school unless the
      patient’s interdisciplinary team (IDT) or the education specialist assesses the
      patient to be unable to participate in education services for clinical or security
      reasons.
      1. The educational specialist must document the temporary pause of services for
         clinical or security reasons in the patient’s medical record and review the reasons
         for the temporary pause at least monthly.
      2. When the clinical or security reasons for the temporary pause cease to be a
         concern to the IDT:
          a. A member of the IDT must promptly inform the education specialist and
             update the patient’s medical record.
          b. Classes will be scheduled for the patient at the next earliest opportunity.

II. PATIENT SCHOOL PARTICIPATION - VOLUNTARY DECLINATION
   A. If the patient voluntarily declines to enroll in school, the patient must be asked to
      sign an education waiver as documentation that the patient was offered services
      required by federal and state law.
      1. The signed waiver must be kept on file with the ESD registrar.
      2. ESD must document the patient’s declination in the patient’s medical record.

PROCEDURES A: School Enrollment                                 POLICY NUMBER: 6.053

    3. The patient may withdraw the waiver at any time.
    4. If the patient does not voluntarily sign the waiver, staff must complete the waiver
       on the patient’s behalf, indicate that the patient refused to sign the waiver, and
       obtain a second staff signature as a witness.
