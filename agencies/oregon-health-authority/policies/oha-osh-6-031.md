 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) follows established guidelines for routine screening
      of the signs and symptoms of dysphagia in order to identify patients who have a
      pre-existing condition or develop signs and symptoms related to dysphagia. This
      policy outlines the requirements for dysphagia screening, management, and
      treatment at OSH.
   B. This policy applies to clinical staff.

II. POLICY
   A. Physicians and nurse practitioners are ultimately responsible for oversight of
      dysphagia screening, management, and care.
   B. All patients newly admitted to OSH must be screened for the risk of dysphagia.
   C. Risk of dysphagia must be assessed in all patients as part of the initial and
      annual medical history and physical examination.
   D. Any patient with a significant choking incident requiring assistance from staff
      must be assessed by a physician or nurse practitioner for a formal swallowing
      evaluation by a speech-language pathologist.
   E. Physicians, nurse practitioners, nurses, dietitians, and other clinicians must
      follow procedures attached to this policy when managing dysphagia risk.
   F. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
