I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy describes Oregon State Hospital’s (OSH) system of reporting staff
         injury or illness sustained on the job.
      B. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH staff must report every injury or illness sustained on the job in a timely
         manner per Procedures A and other relevant requirements.
      B. If staff is requesting light duty, work restrictions must be clearly listed by the
         medical provider for OSH to consider temporary accommodation.
      C. All work status notes must be provided to the OSH Occupational Health and
         Safety department within 24 hours after the staff’s medical appointment
         addressing the work-related injury or illness or immediately upon return to the
         worksite, whichever occurs first.
         1. Staff returning to work following an injury must adhere to OSH policy 5.011,
            “Return to Work of Staff Injured on the Job.”
      D. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
         rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
         and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
         standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
         policy is more restrictive.

         E. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
            may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III.     DEFINITIONS
         A. “Illness” in this policy means poor health resulting from an acute or chronic
            disease or condition such as, but not limited to, a skin disease, respiratory
            disorder, or poisoning.
         B. “Injury” in this policy means a case of hurt, damage, or loss such as, but not
            limited to, a cut, fracture, sprain, or amputation.
         C. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
            and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
