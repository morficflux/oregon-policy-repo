# Medical Care of Patients (OSH Policy 6.070)

Subject:                                                      Policy Number: 6.070
           Provision of Medical Care of Patients
Date:      July 8, 2025                                                  P AGE 2 OF 4

    1. At OSH-Salem and OSH-Junction City, from 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday
       through Friday, the Medical Clinic physicians and nurse practitioners are
       available to assist in the treatment of patients.
    2. Medical Providers who are assigned to specific units are responsible for
       rounding on their assigned units each day and attending at least one morning
       report each working day: they will apportion their week so that morning report
       attendance is divided as equally as possible between their assigned units.
    3. Medical Providers who are assigned to cross cover units will physically round
       for an absent colleague on 3 or fewer units. If assigned four or more units,
       cross coverage is provided by phone triage to the clinic.
    4. On weekends or holidays the MOD will be onsite for a minimum of 4 hours
       per each 24-hour period. MOD will contact each unit to triage patient needs
       with unit RN and will round on Butterfly units and others as needed.
    5. During weekday daily rounds the Medical Provider will consult with Nursing
       Staff to determine which patients are to be seen and will document as per
       Medical Clinic Provider Documentation Standards protocol 1.017.
    6. Urgent interventions requested by the Medical Provider will be relayed to
       Nursing Staff in person or by phone and then documented in the progress
       note.
    7. Unit Staff will notify the Medical Provider assigned to that unit as a primary or
       cross-covering provider of any medical problems during normal business
       hours (Monday through Friday from 0800 to 1630). Outside of these times, or
       if the Medical Provider is unable to respond in a timely manner, the unit
       Psychiatrist or Psychiatric Nurse Practitioner will be notified, who will then
       coordinate with the Medical Provider or the Medical Clinic for appropriate
       follow-up care.
    8. As outlined in the Medical Department Protocol 1.011, “On-Duty Physician”,
       staff should contact the POD for medical emergencies when the Medical
       Clinic is closed. The POD shall assess the situation and may refer the call to
       the MOD. The MOD shall also be available for telephone consultation with
       OSH-Junction City POD.
    9. If medical services are required during an Admission, an overhead page will
       call for the Physician on Duty: the POD and the MOD for that day will respond
       to the Admissions area and will request additional medical assistance as
       indicated.

     10. Medical care is provided on the unit whenever possible. Specialty clinics,
         procedures and some special or sensitive exams (such as pelvic exams) are
         done in the medical clinic as ordered by the medical provider.

  B. Outside Medical Care
     1. Non-emergent movement to medical hospitals must be accompanied by the
        following information:
        a. a photocopy or printout of the current face sheet;
        b. the most recent medical review;
        c. recent pertinent laboratory work;
        d. completed hospital transfer forms filled out by the physician and nurse;
        e. completed billing authorization form;
        f. a case summary updated to include the most recent problems and
           treatment or recent progress notes;
        g. copies of medical and psychiatric Advance Directives and the Physician
           Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST) form.

     2. All referrals of OSH patients to an outside medical hospital must be
        accompanied by a fax to a triage nurse for Salem Hospital (specific form from
        Salem Hospital) a phone call to River Bend from the OSH physician,
        psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner, medical clinic physician or POD.
        Communication must include pertinent medical and psychiatric information,
        the name and phone number of the unit physician, and the patient’s unit.
     3. Telephone contact between the treating provider from the medical hospital
        and the receiving OSH clinic provider needs to occur prior to the patient’s
        return to OSH.
     4. Use of restraints during must follow OSH Policy 8.039, “Use of Restraints for
        Emergency Medical Transfers and Medical Appointments.”

  C. Continuity of Care
     1. All patients returning from an outside hospital or Emergency Department must
        be seen face-to-face by their attending psychiatrist or psychiatric mental
        health nurse practitioner (or POD) or medical provider within eight hours after
        return to OSH.
     2. Nursing must notify the psychiatrist or psychiatric mental health nurse
        practitioner (or POD) and medical provider (MOD) of the patient’s return.

III. DEFINITIONS
          A. “Psychiatrist On Duty (POD)” means the psychiatrist assigned to provide
             campus-wide coverage outside of normal business hours.
          B. “Psychiatrist On Duty (POD)” means the psychiatrist assigned to provide
             campus-wide coverage outside of normal business hours.
