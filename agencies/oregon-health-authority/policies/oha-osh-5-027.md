I.   POLICY
     A. As a division of the Oregon Health Authority, Oregon State Hospital (OSH)
        follows the performance management process delineated in Department of
        Administrative Services (DAS) policy 50.035.01, “Performance Management
        Process,” and in this policy’s provisions.
     B. An employee’s performance must be annually evaluated per standards
        delineated in DAS policy 50.035.01 and per established DAS’ Workday system
        processes.
        1. The performance evaluation must be developed and communicated to the
           employee before its due date. Each step of the process should be completed
           promptly.
        2. An employee who is on Family Medical Leave (FMLA), Oregon Family
           Medical Leave (OFLA), or other approved extended leave must have a
           performance appraisal completed within established performance appraisal
           timeframes.
     C. An employee may submit performance evaluation comments, rebuttals, or
        appeals as delineated in DAS policy 50.035.01. and per established Workday
        system processes.
     D. Supervisory managers will be regularly evaluated per DAS guidelines established
        in “The Oregon Management Project” (TOMP) and according to processes
        delineated in Workday.
     E. Contract administrators must monitor contractors according to their contract
        provisions. Clinical contractors, meaning individuals and professional companies
        who provide clinical care or services to patients, must be regularly evaluated
        according to contract provisions and processes attached to this policy.
     F. This policy applies to all staff, including employees, volunteers, trainees, interns,
        contractors, vendors, and other state employees assigned to work at OSH.

PROCEDURES A    Clinical Contractor Administration and Evaluation   POLICY NUMBER 5.027
DATE            March 23, 2021                                              PAGE 1 of 1

RESPONSIBLE
                                              PROCEDURES
PERSON/GROUP
Manager           1. Designate an employee as a Contract Administrator (CA) to
                     administer contracts within the manager’s department.
                  2. Review the list of assigned CAs semiannually to verify all CAs are
                     still assigned to relevant contracts.
                  3. Assign another CA if a designated CA is no longer appropriate
                     (e.g., no longer employed by OSH, transferred to another role).
                  4. Verify CA training has been completed and documented.
Contract          1. Complete required Oregon Health Authority Office of Contracts
Administrator        and Procurement contract administration training (unless the
(CA)                 training has already been completed) before assignment.
                  2. Complete the OHA Roles and Responsibilities Matrix to delegate
                     any contract administrator duties.
                  3. Monitor assigned contracts on an ongoing basis, including weekly
                     updates of contract expenditures for accuracy and appropriate
                     billing, and monitoring total expenditures against the “do not
                     exceed” level, or the dollar amount listed in each contract that
                     cannot be exceeded for services provided.
                  4. Coordinate all needed contract adjustments to the “do-not-exceed”
                     limit.
                  5. Monitor contract expiration dates and verify all contracts are
                     reviewed for renewal within the allocated timeframe.
Manager           Evaluation of clinical contractor:
                  1. Perform official contract–contractor performance evaluations for
                      all assigned clinical care contracts.
                  2. Conduct contract performance evaluations at least annually, 30
                      days before the annual contract expiration anniversary date.
                      Conduct evaluations more frequently if the contractor’s
                      performance is not at the expected level of performance or as
                      specified in the contract.
                  3. Use the “OSH Contract Evaluation: Determining Which Form To
                      Use” flowchart to determine which form is appropriate to use for
                      the contractor performance evaluation.
                  4. Complete the appropriate evaluation form and review with the
                      contractor. Maintain completed evaluations on file with each CA
                      and with the Business Analysts’ office.
