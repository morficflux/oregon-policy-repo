                 Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
     A. As delineated in Oregon Revised Statute (ORS) chapter 125, Oregon State
        Hospital (OSH) must provide guardians with the same rights as patients in
        the area(s) of authority designated by the court unless another authority’s
        power overrides the court order (e.g., a civil commitment order can override
        a guardian’s authority to remove a patient from OSH). In all OSH policies,
        references to patient decisions are understood to include the patient and/or
        their guardian, to the extent their decisions are authorized by court
        documents.
     B. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
    A. A patient who receives a guardian maintains all legal and civil rights provided
       by law (such as the right to contact an attorney, the right to vote, and to have
       access to their own records) except those that have been specifically granted
       to the guardian by the court or limited by a court order. Any OSH consent
       forms or releases should be sent to the guardian for review and
       signature/approval.
    B. OSH recognizes that guardianship may be appropriate for a patient who is
       determined by a court to be incapacitated, unable to give informed
       consent, in danger to themselves or others, and for whom other less
       restrictive alternatives have been determined to be insufficient to meet their
       needs.
    C. In the cases where OSH pursues guardianship, OSH will only pursue

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Court visitor” means an independent investigator appointed by the court to
      meet with the patient and talk with doctors, caregivers, family members, and
      others who may have relevant information about whether a guardian is
      needed.
   B. “Emergency or temporary guardianship” means a temporary guardian who may
      be appointed for a limited period of time by the court if the patient is found to
      meet all three of the following criteria: 1) the patient is considered incapacitated;
      2) there is immediate and serious danger to the patient’s life/health; and 3) the
      patient’s welfare requires immediate action.
   C. “Family" in this policy means a person or persons who play a significant role in
      an individual's life. A family is a group of two or more persons united by blood
      or adoptive, marital, domestic partnership, or other legal ties. The family may
      also be a person or persons not legally related to the individual (such as a
      significant other, friend, or caregiver) whom the individual personally considers
      to be family. A family member may be the surrogate decision-maker if

   D. Guardianship Determination Team (GDT) means a person or persons who
      consult with IDT members, review referrals, and make the determination on
      whether OSH will pursue guardianship, including whether or not OSH will pay
      for initial court costs for guardianship. “Guardian” in this policy means an adult

   E. “Incapacitated” in accordance with ORS 125.005 means the patient has a
      condition in which his or her ability to receive and evaluate information
      effectively, or to communicate decisions, is impaired to such an extent that the
      patient presently lacks the capacity to meet the essential requirements for the
      person’s physical health or safety. “Meeting the essential requirements for
      physical health and safety” means those actions necessary to provide the
      health care, food, shelter, clothing, personal hygiene, and other care without
      which serious physical injury or illness is likely to occur.
   F. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
