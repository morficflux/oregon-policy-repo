  E. A department or discipline director may designate certain training as essential for
     a staff to acquire specific skills to perform assigned duties. These essential
     trainings are categorized as “manager directed” or “manager requested”. The
     department or discipline director is responsible for appropriate communication
     and follow-up regarding these trainings and related competencies.
  F. OSH employees may be eligible to request and attend elective training, meaning
     training that is available, but not required. Elective trainings are categorized as
     “employee requested – related” or “employee requested – unrelated.” OSH does
     not pay for elective training for other staff who are not employees.
     1. Employees must follow the “OSH Employee Training and Conference
        Payment Guidelines” (Attachment B) and complete the Employee Training
        Request form (Attachment C) when requesting offsite training.
     2. Elective training may only be attended with supervisor approval.
     3. An approving supervisor or manager must have appropriate delegated
        financial expenditure decision authority granted per OSH policy 1.016, “OSH
        Delegation of Authority.”
  G. This policy does not apply to training required by any board or commission for
     initial or continued licensure or certification required by a staff member’s position.
     Such training falls under the provisions of OSH policy 4.013, “Professional
     Credential and Membership Fee.”
  H. Training directed by this policy must be documented in the OSH-designated
     learning management system, Human Resources, supervisory, or
     Complementary Personnel Database (CPD) files.
  I. Training instructors must send copies of all lesson plans, supplemental materials,
     and attendance records to the OSH Learning and Development Department. The
     OSH Learning and Development Department must maintain records of all class
     lesson plans and attendance records used in training provided by OSH.
  J. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) follows all applicable regulations, including federal
     and state statutes and rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services
     (DAS), Shared Services, and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and
     relevant accreditation standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of
     this policy unless this policy is more restrictive.

III.     DEFINITIONS
         A. “Career development” means the process of engaging in activities to acquire
            knowledge or skills which will advance the employee’s career.
         B. “Employee” in this policy means a person hired by OSH. Employees in this policy
            do not include volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, or other state employees
            assigned to work at OSH.
         C. “Employee-requested training – related” means training requested by the
            employee that is not required for the position and is not directly linked to their
            specific job duties but is related to their job or field and is intended for employee
            growth or development.
         D. “Employee requested training – unrelated” means training requested by the
            employee that is not required for the position, has no direct benefit to OSH, and
            is not related to their job or field.
         E. “Manager-requested training” means training that is not required for the
            employee’s position, but it would directly benefit patients or OSH. The training is
            requested by the manager and is intended to increase skill and expertise and
            directly relates to the employee’s job duties (e.g., “train the trainer” where the
            employee trains other employees in that material).
         F. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
            and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
         G. “Technical qualification” means a specific learned ability, such as knowledge of
            software, processes, or other knowledge that applies to specific work-related
            tasks.
