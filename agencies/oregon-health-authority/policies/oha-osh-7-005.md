I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. This policy concerns the protection and promotion of each patient’s rights at the
      Oregon State Hospital (OSH), and provides guidance to staff.
   B. This policy applies to all staff and patients.

II. POLICY
   A. Per Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) and state regulations, Oregon State
      Hospital (OSH) must protect and promote each patient’s rights. A patient
      receiving treatment, their health care representative (HCR), or legal guardian has
      the right to exercise patient rights provided by federal and state regulation
      including, but not limited to:
      1. 42 CFR § 482.13;
      2. 45 CFR § 164;
      3. Oregon Revised Statutes (ORS) § 127;
      4. ORS § 426.385;
      5. ORS § 430.210;
      6. Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR) § 309-035-0175; and
      7. OAR §§ 309-102 – 309-118.
   B. OSH must inform each patient, or when appropriate their HCR or guardian, of the
      patient's rights.

        1. Admitting staff must read and discuss patient rights with each patient, their
           HCR, or legal guardian as part of the admission process before providing
           patient services. This patient rights discussion must be documented on form
           OSH STK 03653 MR and filed in the patient's medical record.
        2. If a patient has limited English proficiency, language services will be arranged
           so the patient’s rights information is provided in a manner in which the patient
           understands, as delineated in OSH Policy 6.020, “Interpreter, Language
           Services for Patients”.
        3. OSH will provide a patient who is a Medicare beneficiary the “An Important
           Message from Medicare” (IM) notice in accordance with provisions of 42 CFR
           § 482.13(a)1 and 42 CFR § 489.27(a).
        4. A summary of patient rights delineated in CFR, ORS, and OAR are posted on
           every unit where they are freely available to all patients. Complete copies of
           patient rights regulations are available upon request from Consumer and
           Family Services.
  C. A patient may exercise the rights specified in federal and state regulations and in
     related OSH policies on patient rights without any form of reprisal or punishment.
        1. A patient may exercise all civil rights in the same manner and with the same
           effect as one not admitted to the facility, including, but not limited, to the right
           to dispose of property, execute instruments, make purchases, enter
           contractual relationships, and vote, unless the patient has been adjudicated
           incompetent and has not been restored to legal capacity.
        2. A patient may have phone and mail access to the Office of Training,
           Investigations, and Safety; public defenders or attorneys of the patient’s own
           selection who are independent of the hospital structure; and organizations
           identified by the federal government which provide pertinent rights and
           advocacy services.
  D. With the exception of an emergency situation of serious risk of harm to the
     patient or others, a patient’s right may only be restricted after alternative
     interventions have been offered or attempted. A patient rights restriction must
     comply with processes established in relevant regulations and policies.
  E. OSH may not restrict a patient’s right which a federal or state regulation specifies
     cannot be restricted.
  F. A patient may submit a grievance regarding their rights in accordance with the
     process in OSH Policy 7.006, “Patient Grievances”.
  G. Any person may report a violation of patient rights to the following organization:

    H. A person must report allegations of abuse in accordance with OSH Policy 7.008,
       “Patient Abuse or Mistreatment Allegation Reporting” and related regulations.
    I. Staff must know and protect the rights of patients.
    J. The OSH Patient Rights Committee will monitor patient rights.
    K. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
       rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
       and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
       standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
       policy is more restrictive.
    L. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
       may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
    A. “Health care representative” in this policy has the same meaning as the definition
       in ORS 127.505.
    B. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
       and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
