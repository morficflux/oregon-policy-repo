Purpose
Passwords or passphrases are the primary form of user authentication for granting
access to Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and the Oregon Health
Authority (OHA) information systems. Compromised passwords or passphrases remain
the top cause of data breaches. The purpose of this policy is to establish a standard for
the creation of strong passwords or passphrases, the protection of those passwords or
passphrases, and the frequency of change. Enterprise Information Services provides
regulatory guidance for state agencies. This policy aligns ODHS and OHA with
Statewide Information Technology (IT) Control Standards.

Description
This policy outlines the requirements for creating, storing, and using passwords or
passphrases to enhance the security of user accounts and agency data.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff including employees, volunteers,
trainees, interns, as well as contractors and partners.

As keepers of the public trust, all agency employees have a responsibility to comply
with state and agency policies, administrative rule, and state and federal law. The
agency takes this responsibility seriously and failure to fulfill this responsibility is not
treated lightly. Employees who fail to comply with state or agency policy, administrative
rule, or state and federal law may face progressive discipline, up to and including
dismissal from state service.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA shall implement the identification and authentication related
    controls1 mandated by the DAS 107-004-052 Cyber and Information Security
    Policy.
2. ODHS and OHA information system users shall use a combination of a unique
    username and password or passphrase to access all systems and applications. A
    passphrase is a sequence of words or other text and consists of a mixture
    uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
3. Passwords or passphrases shall be constructed with these composition and
    complexity requirements:2
    a. Minimum password or passphrase length of fifteen (15) characters. Long
        passwords and passphrases are allowed.
    b. Minimum password or passphrase complexity to contain at least:
        A. One (1) numeric: zero-9.
        B. One (1) symbol such as, @, #, $, %, ^, &, etc.
        C. One (1) English uppercase letter: A-Z.
        D. One (1) English lowercase letter: a-z.
    c. No single dictionary words or common names unless used in passphrase
        context.
    d. No portions of the associated account name or identifier such as User I.D. or
        login name may be used.
    e. Enforce at least one (1) character change when new passwords or
        passphrases are selected for use.
4. Staff shall:
    a. Use different passwords or passphrases for each network, system, and
        application-based account they access.
    b. Keep passwords or passphrases confidential and shall not share passwords or
        passphrases to their accounts with anyone.
    c. Store agency passwords or passphrases using secure and approved methods,
        such as agency approved password managers.
5. Temporary or default passwords or passphrases shall be changed at the first
    successful login.
6. If an account or password or passphrase is suspected to have been compromised,
    staff or the Office of Information Services (OIS) shall immediately:
    a. Change the password or passphrase and reset Multifactor Authentication
        (MFA).
    b. Notify the OIS Service Desk who then notifies ISPO.

7.  Staff shall not use organization-provided identifiers such as email addresses, and
    authentication secrets such as passwords or passphrases, for creating accounts
    on social media, social networking sites, external sites, and applications.3
8. Password or passphrase consecutive timeouts have been set by the agency
    administrator. When the limit of consecutive failed log-ins has been reached, staff
    will need to contact the Service Desk to have their password or passphrase
    unlocked or reset.4
9. ODHS and OHA shall implement MFA for staff when local, network, or remote
    access to state assets are required for both privileged and non-privileged
    accounts.5 MFA requires a minimum of two of the following:
    a. Something they know (personal identification number, password or
         passphrase).
    b. Something they have (token, phone, card, key).
    c. Something that identifies them individually (fingerprints, facial recognition,
         retinal patterns.
10. ODHS and OHA systems or applications shall provide a single sign-on capability
    where available.6
11. Password or passphrase lifetime restrictions and expirations for password or
    passphrase reset shall be one day minimum and 60 days maximum.7
12. Passwords or passphrases shall not be set to one of the last 24 previously used
    passwords.
13. ODHS and OHA system administrators shall:
    a. Maintain or subscribe to a list of commonly used, expected, or compromised
         passwords or passphrases that are banned from use, and shall update the list
         quarterly.8
    b. Verify, when users create or update passwords or passphrases, that the
         passwords or passphrases are not found on the list of commonly used,
         expected, or compromised passwords or passphrases.9
14. When a password or passphrase is issued or changed, or communicated for other
    reasons, the password or passphrase:
    a. Shall be sent securely by email or other electronic communication.
    b. Shall not be sent with other identifying information.
15. Agency information systems and applications shall not display passwords or
    passphrases in plain text and shall only transmit encrypted passwords or
    passphrases.
