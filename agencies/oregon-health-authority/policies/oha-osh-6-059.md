  SELECT ONE:

I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy defines requirements and procedures for animals and animal handlers
         visiting Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
      B. This policy applies to:
         1. Visiting animals including animal-assisted therapy (AAT)/animal-assisted
            activities (AAA) animals, visitor-handled animals, Americans with Disabilities
            Act (ADA) service animals, and service animals in training.
         2. Handlers bringing animals into OSH.
         3. All OSH staff and patients.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH recognizes the benefits of human-animal interactions as therapeutic
         interventions in structured therapist-led groups, individual sessions, and informal
         encounters.
      B. An animal must be approved per Procedures A before it may enter an OSH
         building.
      C. Approval for an animal may be revoked at any time per Procedures A for
         situations including, but not limited to:
         1. If the animal does not meet the identified patient or hospital needs,
         2. If there are infection control issues, and/or

     3. If there is a complaint or incident involving health or safety of the animal or

  D. There must be a business need for an animal to be in the hospital. For the

     1. AAT/AAA
     2. Patient visitation
     3. An approved Americans with Disabilities Act of 1991 (ADA) accommodation.

           the presence of the animal poses a direct threat to other persons or
           fundamentally disrupts the nature of hospital services.
        b. Staff must have an approved ADA accommodation through HR per

           building.
     4. Vocational service dog training
  E. All animals and animal handlers on an OSH campus are subject to the following
     guidelines:
     1. Animals must be an approved domesticated species to visit OSH. Approved
        domesticated species include dogs and cats only.
        a. A wild or exotic animal is not allowed in an OSH building.
     2. Any animal outside of the approved domesticated species list requires Chief
        Medical Officer (CMO) approval to visit OSH.
        a. Exception reviews must be requested using the Handler/Animal
           Exceptions Request Form (Attachment E) through the approving manager,
           or designee.
     3. All animals must continue to adhere to requirements described in Procedures
        A.
     4. A handler who has to step away from the animal temporarily must have a
        designated staff with the animal at all times who is responsible for meeting
        the animal’s needs.
     5. An animal may not be left unattended in a vehicle on an OSH campus (see
        OSH Policy and Procedure 8.023, “Parking and Parking Registration”).

     6. Staff must follow OSH policy 8.006, “Vehicle Usage,” and Oregon

     7. Animals are not permitted in the following locations;

III.     DEFINITIONS
         A. “Animal-assisted activities (AAA)” are activities designed to provide opportunities
            for motivational, educational, recreational, or therapeutic benefits to enhance
            quality of life. AAA are delivered by specially trained professionals,
            paraprofessionals, or volunteers in association with animals that have, at
            minimum, an American Kennel Club Canine Good Citizen (CGC) certification.
            Animal-Assisted Activities do not have specific treatment goals planned for each
            visit.
         B. “Animal-assisted therapy (AAT)” is a goal-oriented, planned, structured, and
            documented therapeutic intervention directed by a qualified professional or
            therapist in conjunction with a therapy animal as part of that person’s profession.
         C. “Code Blue” is as defined in OSH policy 8.038, “Code Blue Medical Emergency.”
            Code Blue means a request for immediate response to any apparent emergency
            medical condition that could be either potentially life threatening or life altering.
         D. “Handler” means a person responsible for the animal during the time they are on
            an Oregon State Hospital (OSH) campus.
            1. “Visiting animal handler” refers to the owner or individual who takes
               responsibility for a visiting animal while on OSH property.
            2. “Volunteer animal handler” means approved OSH volunteers’ handling
               animals
            3. “Therapy handler” means a person handling a therapy animal.

        E. “Respite” means time for the animal away from the animal-assisted activity.
        F. “Service animal (Americans with Disabilities Act [ADA] animal)” means any guide
           dog, signal dog, or other animal individually trained to do work or perform tasks
           for the benefit of an individual with a disability, including, but not limited to guiding
           an individual with impaired vision, alerting an individual with impaired hearing to
           intruders or sounds, providing minimal protection or rescue work, pulling a
           wheelchair, or fetching dropped items. When an animal meets this definition, the
           animal is considered a service animal under the ADA, regardless of whether the
           animal has been licensed or certified by a state or local government.
        G. “Service animal in training” means an animal that is in the developmental process
           of structured Vocational Services training program that provides a service for an
           individual with specific identified needs.
        H. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
           and other state employees assigned to work at OSH.
        I. “Therapy animal” means an animal who is, at minimum, certified (e.g., American
           Kennel Club CGC), but may also be registered by one of the national or
           international animal therapy programs (e.g., Pet Partners) and which is
           accompanied by a handler.
        J. "Visitor-handled animal,” means an animal accompanied by an approved
           visiting animal handler attending visitation(s) with OSH patients.
