# Access Control Policy (ODHS|OHA Policy 090-003)

## At a glance

ODHS|OHA Policy 090-003 — Access Control Policy. Oregon Health Authority (Information Security & Privacy Office). Effective 1/29/2006.

## Full text

                                             SHARED SERVICES
                                        Office of Information Services

 Operational Policy

 Policy title:           Access Control Policy

 Policy number:          ODHSOHA 090-003

 Original date:          1/29/2006                     Last update: 02/01/2026

 Approved:               Kris Kautz, OHA Deputy Director and Seth Lyon, ODHS COO

Purpose
The Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) are
committed to controlling, securing, and protecting information created and maintained by the
agencies by enacting requirements for accessing ODHS and OHA information assets and systems.
This policy ensures users have the appropriate access levels for the information specifically
authorized in information systems and applications they can access; ensures individuals understand
the responsibility their level of data security access requires; and supports the agencies’ equity goals
by simplifying collaboration.

Description
This policy establishes the requirements to protect ODHS and OHA information assets and systems
against improper or unauthorized access that could result in the compromise of confidentiality,
integrity, or availability of ODHS and OHA information, information technology (IT) assets, or
technology-enabled capabilities.

It describes the requirements for the use of resources designed for collaboration and file sharing.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff including full-time, part-time, contingent, temporary,
and limited duration employees, volunteers, trainees, and interns.

As keepers of the public trust, all ODHS and OHA staff play a vital role in upholding state and agency
policies, administrative rules, and state and federal laws. ODHS and OHA are committed to
exceptional public and customer service. The agencies are dedicated to maintaining standards of
exceptional service and trust staff to do the same. Failure to follow policies, rules and laws may result
in beginning a personnel process to consider formal action as appropriate, including progressive
discipline up to and including dismissal from state service.

If this policy is intended to apply to contractors or grantees the necessary language must incorporated
into the contract.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA access controls shall use and comply with the Statewide Information Technology
   (IT) Control Standards, the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), and the Center
   for Internet Security (CIS) controls.
2. Identity access means the process for authorizing an identity such as an identifying number or
   email address that allows access to ODHS and OHA assets and systems by ODHS and OHA
   staff, partners, vendors, contractors, and visitors.
       a. A visitor is any individual who is authorized to access and use agency filesharing and
          collaboration resources using a personal or professional email account not issued by a state
          agency.
       b. A guest is an individual from a state agency external to ODHS and OHA who is authorized
          to access and use agency filesharing and collaboration resources. Guests undergo an
          identity access process within their own agency.
3. ODHS and OHA shall comply with statewide security requirements around identity access and
   auditing as agreed in the Enterprise Information Services Risk Disclosure Communication
   regarding external collaboration with non-state visitors, including maintaining:
     a. Security and privacy policies.
     b. Risk and incident response processes and documentation.
     c. Training and documentation requirements for staff approved to request access for visitors to
          agency collaboration and filesharing resources, including an attestation of understanding
          and responsibility (Owner Agreement for External Collaboration).
     d. A terms of use document (Visitor Agreement) for visitors granted access to agency
          collaboration and filesharing resources for attestation of understanding and responsibility.
     e. Processes and controls for verification of users, routine auditing, and ongoing system
          administration.
     f. Data loss prevention strategies.
4. As appropriate to the system or application, the Office of Information Services (OIS), the
   Enterprise Communication, Collaboration and Productivity Strategy Team (ECCP), and other
   applicable business groups shall clearly define and establish processes to:
     a. Create, document, approve, modify, and terminate an individual’s right of access to ODHS
          and OHA systems and applications.
     b. Access collaboration and filesharing resources approved by OIS.
     c. Establish and maintain a centralized inventory of accounts to include privileged accounts,
          service accounts, and user accounts.1
     d. Automate the disablement of dormant accounts after a period of 45 days of inactivity.2
5. The ODHS and OHA Human Resources information system (HRIS) shall serve as the authority to
   automate onboarding and offboarding identities and predefined default access.
6. Only properly trained and authorized agency staff shall approve or provision access to network,
   systems, data, and collaboration and filesharing resources. OIS shall designate specific
   individuals to create visitor accounts.
7. Staff shall complete approved training before providing access to collaboration and filesharing
   resources for visitors. Visitors may not invite other visitors.

8. ODHS and OHA resource owners, managers, or delegates evaluate and approve staff requests
    for:
      a. Access to resources.
      b. Access for internal users of ODHS and OHA information systems, including remote access
           based on job duties and the user’s role.
      c. Identity changes for staff and third-party entities, including onboarding, re-enabling,
           updating identity attributes, and offboarding. An identity update is when any part of a user's
           identity, including name, email, or account status, changes.
9. Third-party entities shall follow the Third-Party Entity Approval for System Access Process-Agency
    Contractors and Partners (ODHSOHA 090-003-08 and ODHSOHA 090-003-10).
10. Privileged access requests shall follow the Privileged Access and Management Process
    (ODHSOHA 090-003-01).
11. Requests for access to collaboration and filesharing resources shall follow the Visitor
    Collaboration and Filesharing Process (ODHSOHA 090-003-18).
      a. ODHS and OHA shall provide educational information to visitors about sensitivity labels and
           their required use.
      b. Prior to being admitted to an agency collaboration or filesharing resource, visitors shall
           complete and return a Visitor Agreement.
12. User accounts shall not be used as a service account or a shared login. A service account is a
    type of account that an application, system, workstation, laptop, or server uses to interact with the
    operating system and other systems on the network.
13. Secure production wireless networks in ODHS and OHA locations may only be accessed by
    agency-owned and managed devices.
14. Federal Tax Information (FTI) shall only be accessed or used by ODHS and OHA staff in
    accordance with the Internal Revenue Services (IRS) Publication 1075 and Oregon Administrative
    Rules (OAR) 407-007-0020(1).
15. Criminal Justice Information (CJI) shall only be accessed or used by ODHS and OHA staff
    including contractors and partners in accordance with the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
    Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) Security Policy and OAR 407-007-0020(1).
16. ODHS and OHA contractors and partners who access or use CJI shall follow the CJIS Security
    and Management Control Outsourcing Standard for Non-Channelers for Non-Criminal Justice
    units of ODHS and OHA, or the CJIS Security Addendum for Criminal Justice units of ODHS and
    OHA.
17. Social Security Administration (SSA) data shall only be accessed or used by ODHS and OHA staff
    in accordance with the SSA’s information security safeguard requirements. For additional
    information related to SSA safeguard requirements, staff shall contact the Information Security
    and Privacy Office (ISPO).
18. Background checks shall be completed as appropriate based on agency, state and federal
    requirements, and contractual obligations.
19. ODHS and OHA managers shall ensure that assigned staff access entitlements are reviewed to
    validate the need for such entitlements:
      a. Initially upon hire.
      b. Any time assigned job duties change.
      c. Any time there is a change in job position such as promotion, demotion, separation, or
           transfer to another division or section within the agency.
      d. Annually.

20. Information system owners, as identified in the Configuration Management Application List or the
    System Security Plan, shall review who has access to their system at least annually. Information
    system owners are ODHS and OHA staff who are responsible for the overall procurement,
    development, integration, modification, or operation and maintenance of an information system.1
21. Information system owners shall approve shared or group accounts.
22. Shared or group account credentials shall be changed when ODHS and OHA staff leave duties
    related to a shared or group account. When shared accounts or authenticators are employed,
    users are required to be individually authenticated before granting access to the shared accounts
    or resources.
23. Agreements that require third-party access to ODHS and OHA information technology systems or
    assets, shall include:
      a. ISPO-approved secure language. The need shall be identified on Third-Party System
          Access form (MSC785).
      b. Oregon State Police (OSP)-approved CJIS Outsourcing Agreement for Noncriminal Justice
          Administrative Functions language where logical access to CJIS data and systems, or
          physical access to logical systems, shall be granted to an external party. Logical access is a
          technical term that means to read, create, modify, delete a file, execute a program, or use
          an external connection, for an individual or other computer system to utilize CJI or CJIS
          applications.
      c. Oregon Department of Justice (DOJ)-approved Business Associate Agreements when the
          contractor accesses protected health information (PHI) from an agency HIPAA covered
          entity or business associate program. (Refer to Business Associate Relationship Policy
          ODHSOHAODHSOHA 100-013).
24. The Chief Information Risk Officer (CIRO) may require specific access controls, as circumstances
    require.
