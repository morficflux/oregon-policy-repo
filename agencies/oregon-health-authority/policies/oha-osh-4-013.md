I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy establishes Oregon State Hospital (OSH) requirements related to
         professional credentials and criteria for OSH payment of professional credential
         or membership fees in professional organizations.
      B. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) staff are required to maintain their own professional
         credentials while working at OSH, including paying necessary fees to maintain
         professional credentials, unless an exception in this policy applies.
      B. Staff are also encouraged to voluntarily become members of professional
         organizations related to their job and discipline.
         1. Individual membership fees are generally the responsibility of staff.
         2. These membership fees include fees for formal association in a fraternal or
            professional organization for the purpose of exchange of information,
            scholarship, certification, and ideas.
      C. At the discretion of the Chief Financial Officer or the Superintendent, OSH may
         pay a professional credential fee or membership fee per Procedures A if the
         credential or membership directly benefits OSH and is for compliance purposes
         or is a specialized license or certificate required by the job duty.

          D. OSH will not pay for membership fees for organizations which discriminate in
             membership on the basis of race, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability,
             color, marital status, or sexual orientation.
          E. This policy does not apply to:
             1. Membership fees or dues paid primarily for the purchase of goods (e.g., a
                subscription) or services (e.g., a cooperative association for the disposal of
                hazardous waste), or
             2. Memberships in the name of OSH benefiting all staff and patient treatment.
          F. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) follows all applicable regulations, including federal
             and state statutes and rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services
             (DAS), Shared Services, and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and
             relevant accreditation standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of
             this policy unless this policy is more restrictive.
          G. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
             may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

 III.     DEFINITIONS
          A. “Professional credentials” in this policy means licenses, certifications,
             registrations, or other credentials required for employment.
          B. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
             and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
