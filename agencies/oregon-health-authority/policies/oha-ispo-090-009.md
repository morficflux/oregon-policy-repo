# Administrative, Technical and Physical Safeguards of Information Policy (ODHS|OHA Policy 090-009)

                                Office of Information Services
                           Information Security and Privacy Office

Operational Policy
Policy title: Administrative, Technical and Physical Safeguards of
Information Policy

Policy number: ODHSOHA 090-009

Original policy date: 11/08/2004

Last update: 07/01/2026

Approved by: Kris Kautz, OHA and Seth Lyon, ODHS

Purpose
The Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and Oregon Health
Authority (OHA) are committed to protecting the information assets and systems
of the agencies. This policy establishes criteria to safeguard protected
information and minimize the risk of unauthorized access, use, or disclosure

Description
This policy describes the responsibilities of ODHS and OHA staff to safeguard
information assets and systems and to manage associated risks during daily
work, whether in the office or remotely. Staff are expected to exercise discretion
regarding information shared in documents or conversations, maintain the
security of workplace surroundings, and ensure the protection of information
transported outside of the worksite.

Applicability
This policy applies to all ODHS and OHA staff, including full-time, part-time,
contingent, temporary, and limited duration employees, volunteers, trainees,
and interns. The requirements of this policy also apply to contractors, grantees,
and partners in their delivery of goods or services to or on behalf of either
agency, as required under the applicable contract, grant, or other form of
agreement.

As keepers of the public trust, all ODHS and OHA staff play a vital role in
upholding state and agency policies, administrative rules, and state and federal
laws. ODHS and OHA are committed to exceptional public and customer
service. The agencies are dedicated to maintaining standards of exceptional
service and trust staff to do the same. Failure to follow policies, rules and laws
may result in beginning a personnel process to consider formal action as
appropriate, including progressive discipline up to and including dismissal from
state service.

Policy
1. ODHS and OHA shall implement administrative, technical, and physical
   safeguards to protect information assets from unauthorized access, use or
   disclosure. Examples of information assets include:
      a. Data and information.
      b. Information technology (IT) systems.
      c. IT networks.
      d. Software.
      e. Hardware.
      f. Network devices.
      g. Digital and physical media.
2. ODHS and OHA staff shall protect information assets, including but not
   limited to, personally identifiable information (PII), protected health
   information (PHI), federal tax information (FTI), criminal justice Information
   (CJI), and Social Security Administration (SSA) information.
3. Geofencing controls shall be implemented to restrict access to sensitive
   systems and data from unauthorized or high-risk geographic locations
   according to the Statewide Information Technology (IT) Control Standards.1
4. ODHS and OHA shall monitor agencies’ information asset use in accordance
   with agency policies and procedures including but not limited to Department
   of Administrative Services (DAS), federal and state requirements.

5. Information system owners shall ensure systems managed by ODHS and
   OHA, Department of Administrative Services (DAS) Enterprise Information
   Services (EIS), DAS EIS Data Center Services (DCS), and hosted service
   providers, have secure installations, secure configurations, and
   management for all information assets.
6. Information systems owners are ODHS and OHA staff who are responsible
   for the overall procurement, development, integration, modification, or
   operation and maintenance of an information system.2
7. The agency is responsible for maintaining current inventory of information
   assets and the accuracy of its environment in collaboration with the Office of
   Information Services (OIS) teams and leadership oversight.
8. All ODHS and OHA staff are responsible for safeguarding information assets
   and systems to prevent unauthorized access and disclosure. To fulfill this
   responsibility, staff shall:
      a. Use only agency-approved software and systems for handling agency
         data. (Refer to IT Standards and Approved Products List available in the
         Reference section of this policy).
      b. Use only agency-approved portable devices or other storage media that
         are encrypted with agency-approved encryption technologies with the
         following minimum standards:
           A. A. For data at rest at a minimum shall be Advanced Encryption
              Standard (AES-XTS)-XTS-256.
           B. B. For data in transit at a minimum shall be Transport Layer Security
              (TLS) 1.2.
      c. Secure their agency-issued devices at all times and report any loss or
         theft immediately to their supervisor, the OIS Service Desk, Information
         Security and Privacy Office (ISPO), and their local police department.
         (Refer to ODHSOHA 090-009-03 Lost or Stolen Computer Process and
         ODHSOHA 070-001-02 Lost or Stolen Mobile Communication Device).

      d. Comply with all agency remote work policies and procedures, and shall
         use only agency-approved equipment and remote access systems.3
      e. Safeguard approved peripheral equipment such as printers, copiers, fax
         machines, smart boards, cameras, and other devices, from inadvertent or
         unauthorized access, and comply with DAS 107-004-110 Acceptable Use
         of State Information Assets Policy.
      f. Accurately prepare and protect information sent via fax and send securely
         to authorized recipients only, except FTI which shall not be transmitted via
         fax. [Refer to Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR) 943-014-0000(33)]
      g. Ensure that any transmission of agency information is sent to an
         authorized recipient.
      h. Securely store and transport documents.
           A. Hard copies of protected documents shall be securely stored in
              locked rooms or containers.
           B. Centralized storage areas for protected materials shall be
              appropriately labeled and locked.
           C. ODHS and OHA staff authorized to remove documents from the
              worksite shall maintain physical custody and control, and secure the
              documents.
      i.   Take reasonable steps to protect the confidentiality of verbal exchanges
           of protected information regardless of where the discussion occurs.
           A. Verbal exchanges of protected information shall occur only when
              members of the workforce have taken appropriate steps to secure the
              privacy of the exchange, and with authorized recipients only.
           B. Each ODHS and OHA worksite shall ensure staff are aware that
              protected information can be unintentionally disclosed during
              conversations.

  j.   Not use publicly accessible electronic devices to access, process, store,
       or transmit agency information assets, such as a library or hotel
       computer.
  k. Not share information assets through publicly available cloud storage
     solutions or applications (anything not approved and purchased through
     agency processes).
  l. Not intentionally introduce any computer code designed to self-replicate,
     damage or otherwise hinder the performance of information assets or
     systems.
  m. Not connect any devices unless they are owned or approved by a state
     agency.
