  E. Video use for purposes not identified in this policy must be approved by the
     Superintendent or their designee on a case-by-case basis as permitted by
     federal and state privacy and confidentiality laws.
  F. Unless granted an exception per this policy, video may only be viewed for
     purposes related to:
      1. Protecting OSH property;
      2. Train staff with footage not used as evidence in investigations;
      3. Verify policies and protocols are being followed;
      4. Reportable incident investigations;
      5. Stored and retained video related directly to a criminal investigation,
          subpoena, or arrest may be disclosed as authorized by applicable laws;
      6. Patient safety events, including sentinel events;
      7. Seclusion or restraint events;
      8. High-liability incidents, including, but not limited to, code events;
      9. Fire and life safety investigations occurring during an episode of care;
      10. Safety accident investigations related to staff, patients, or visitors;
      11. Clinical safety;
      12. Security-related concerns;
      13. Workplace violence response; or
      14. HR investigations.

  G. Only assigned Security staff, Nursing Management, Program Directors, and Unit
     Administrators may monitor live video while performing their duties and while on
     their assigned work shift. Other access to view live video must be approved by
     the Superintendent or their designee on a case-by-case basis as permitted by
     federal and state privacy and confidentiality laws. Access to video surveillance
     areas is restricted to those persons authorized to view live video during the
     authorized period of time.

  H. Unless otherwise exempt per this policy, staff must follow Procedures A, “Video
     Surveillance” to request approval to view video.
  I. The following are exempt from the Superintendent’s written approval process to
     view video (Procedures A):

            h. The Assault Mitigation Workgroup.
        2. OHA Human Resources;
        3. An entity with a valid court order;
        4. Law enforcement; and
        5. Office of Training, Investigations, and Safety (OTIS).
  L. Any discussion relating to viewing live, stored, or retained video is restricted to
     sanctioned and confidential processes and forums to be discussed only on a
     need-to-know basis for business purposes.
  M. Video viewing requests must be made within 28 days of the incident or event
     taking place.
  N. All viewing of stored or retained video must be documented in video surveillance
     logs that contain the contents of Attachment A. Staff are not required to use this
     document, but only incorporate the elements within their department’s log.
  O. Union officers may request copies of the video surveillance logs and receive
     them in a timely manner, not to exceed 15 days after their request.
  P. OSH must keep retained video as required by applicable laws and policies and in
     accordance with OSH policy 2.006, “Public Records Retention and Destruction.”
     After applicable retention periods expire, OSH may destroy retained videos as
     directed in the Secretary of State Retention Schedule.
        1. Unless an exception is permitted by law or the Superintendent, stored video
           shall be kept to the full extent of the video surveillance system allowance (28
           days).
        2. Staff may retain recordings regarding authorized OSH investigations until
           the final resolution of the investigation.
  Q. Logs and requests pertaining to video surveillance must be stored for 3 years.
     This includes: accepted video request forms, denied video request forms,
     maintenance logs, repair request documentation, departmental video logs, and
     security video logs.
  R. In the event of video system equipment failure, follow Procedures B.
  S. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
     rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
     and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
     standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
     policy is more restrictive.
