                                  Information Security and Privacy Office

 Operational Policy
 Policy title:        HIPAA: Notice of Privacy Practices

 Policy number:       ODHS|OHA 100-002

 Original date:       09/20/2013 (OHA only)              Last update:      (Joint ODHS|OHA) 01/10/2022
                      Kris Kautz, Deputy Director Oregon Health Authority
 Approved:            Don Erickson, Chief Administrative Officer Oregon Department of Human
                      Services

Purpose
This Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) policy is one of a series that outlines
Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS) and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) expectations for
the necessary collection, use, and disclosure of protected health information (PHI) about individuals
in order to provide services and benefits while maintaining reasonable safeguards to protect the
privacy of their information.

ODHS|OHA workforce should review all ODHS|OHA privacy policies to be sure they understand how
these policies work together to protect individual privacy.

Description
This policy describes an individual’s right to receive a Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP).

Applicability
This policy applies to all OHA staff (see OAR 943-014-0015) and ODHS staff working in Business
Associate programs (see ODHS 100-013) including employees, volunteers, interns and contractors.
This definition of staff aligns with the HIPAA definition of workforce.

As keepers of the public trust, ODHS|OHA workforce has a responsibility to comply with state and
ODHS|OHA policies, administrative rule, and state and federal law. ODHS|OHA take this
responsibility seriously and failure to fulfill this responsibility is not treated lightly. Workforce who fail
to comply with state or agency policy, administrative rule, or state and federal law may face
progressive discipline, up to and including dismissal from state service.

Policy
1. ODHS|OHA shall make available to every individual applicant for service a Notice of Privacy
   Practices (NPP) describing the responsibility of ODHS|OHA to maintain the privacy of protected
   health information (PHI).
2. The NPP shall include clear information to inform the individual about the types of uses and
   disclosures of PHI ODHS|OHA is permitted or required to make.

   a. ODHS|OHA shall provide all individuals an NPP in direct care settings when they first receive
        care and request the individual’s signature on form MSC 2092.
   b. Any change or revision to the NPP shall be in collaboration with the Information Security and
        Privacy Office.
   c. Any changes to the NPP apply to information already collected as well as any information
        ODHS|OHA receives in the future.
   d. If the notice is revised, all former notices must be kept for six (6) years.
   e. ODHS|OHA shall prominently post a copy of the NPP for public viewing at each ODHS|OHA
        worksite and on the individual ODHS|OHA websites.
   f. Upon request, ODHS|OHA shall provide a copy of the NPP to any person in the format
        requested.
3. If the ODHS|OHA policy conflicts with federal or state statute or rule, that statute or rule
   supersedes unless the ODHS|OHA policy provides more protection.
