I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) acknowledges the inherent, constitutionally-
         protected rights retained by patients to believe, express, and exercise the
         spiritual/religious system of the patient’s choice. OSH also acknowledges that a
         patient’s spiritual/religious system is an important part of recovery. Therefore,
         OSH extends those opportunities as reasonably appropriate for patients to
         practice spiritual/religious system freedom in a manner consistent with facility
         requirements for security, safety, health, orderliness, and stewardship of
         resources.
      B. This policy establishes aspects of Spiritual Care department operations, including
         procurement and handling of spiritual/religious items, and describes patient
         supports related to spiritual/religious systems.
      C. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH acknowledges that a patient’s access to culturally, spiritually, and
         linguistically appropriate services assists with positive treatment outcomes for
         patients. A patient does not need to be a member of a specific spiritual/religious
         system to participate in ceremonies, traditions, or practices offered by the
         Spiritual Care department.
      B. Chaplains, staff, spiritual/religious system representatives, members of the
         community, and patients may not attempt to persuade another individual to

     convert from one belief system to another belief system or criticize the
     spiritual/religious beliefs and/or practices of others at OSH.
  C. Nothing in this provision prohibits the sharing of information regarding a
     spiritual/religious system at the request of a patient.
  D. Only staff in Spiritual Care may provide religious/spiritual items and services to a
     patient. Patients and staff not in Spiritual Care should not be providing
     spiritual/religious items or services to others.
  E. OSH encourages all patients to engage in ceremonies, traditions, and practices
     in the manner that a patient identifies, which may include the use of specific
     items or property.
     1. Some ceremonies, traditions, and practices may include the use of Native
        artifacts and sacred items. Such items must be handled in accordance with
        OSH policy 7.017, “Native Artifacts and Property.”
     2. Religious and/or spiritual item categories for the purposes of this policy are
        defined in Attachment A. Requests for any spiritual/religious items that may
        not be included under a category in Attachment A shall be evaluated by the
        Spiritual Care department and the Product Risk Assessment process per
        OSH policy 4.018, “OSH Purchase and Maintenance: Supplies, Assets, and
        Equipment.”
     3. Spiritual/religious items that may not be included under a category in
        Attachment A and are not approved in advance by the Spiritual Care
        department may be treated in accordance with OSH policy 8.044,
        “Contraband.”
  F. Within the limitations of resources and the need for facility, security, safety,
     health, orderliness, and stewardship of resources, OSH:
     1. offers patients the opportunity to practice their spiritual/religious system of
        choice;
     2. provides for the orderly management of spiritual/religious system
        opportunities for each patient;
     3. seeks methods to promote patient and staff respect for all spiritual/religious
        system objects and practices; and
     4. consults and collaborates with authorized spiritual/religious system
        representatives and the OSH Spiritual Care department to purchase artifacts
        and items for patients to use during ceremonies and other spiritual/religious
        practices.

         G. Authorized belief system representatives and Spiritual Care department staff
            attend to the spiritual needs of all patients by performing any or all of the
            following functions related to spiritual beliefs and/or religion:
            1. Conduct spiritual/religious system services for patients,
            2. Provide spiritual, religious, and moral instruction to patients affiliated with a
               spiritual/religious system;
            3. Interview and provide requested spiritual care assistance to patients;
            4. Provide consultation and education to staff and patients (either as an
               individual or in a group gathering) regarding spiritual/religious systems and
               proper handling/intended use of any items associated with a spiritual/religious
               system or practice;
            5. Provide assistance to patients and staff with purchasing, receiving, and
               sending spiritual/religious items per Procedures A;
            6. Provide consultation to staff when a patient requires an individualized plan for
               handling, storing, and/or using spiritual/religious items;
            7. Provide assistance to patients and staff if spiritual/religious items need to be
               inspected or moved, such as during unit searches;
            8. Provide assistance to patients and staff if access to an approved spiritual
               and/or religious item is limited or restricted in accordance with OSH policy
               7.015, “Patient Rights Restrictions,” and this policy’s procedures.
            9. Consult with staff about the spiritual, moral, ethical, or social concerns of
               patients affiliated with cultural and/or a spiritual/religious system; and
            10. Work under the guidance and supervision of the Director of Spiritual Care.
         H. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) follows all applicable regulations, including federal
            and state statutes and rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services
            (DAS), Shared Services, and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and
            relevant accreditation standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of
            this policy unless this policy is more restrictive.
         I. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
            may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III.     DEFINITIONS

         A. “Authorized belief system representative(s)” include but are not limited to Native
            Services department, Chaplains, and other OSH staff authorized by the Spiritual
            Care department.
         B. “Belief system” in this policy includes spiritual or religious beliefs, practices, and
            traditions.
         C. “Belief system representative” is a recognized leader of a belief system such as a
            priest, pastor, medicine man or woman, imam, or rabbi who has arranged with
            Spiritual Care to provide belief system services to patients.
         D. “Native artifacts” and “sacred items” are as defined in OSH policy 7.017, “Native
            Artifacts and Property.”
         E. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
            and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
