 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
    A. Medication reconciliation is an interdisciplinary process designed to maintain an
       accurate list of an Oregon State Hospital (OSH) patient’s medications and
       decrease adverse drug events, thereby reducing the risk of medication-related
       injury to patients. This policy and its attachments establish guidelines and steps
       that must occur for medication reconciliation.
    B. This policy applies to all staff who are responsible for the ordering, dispensing, or
       administration of patient medication.

II. POLICY

    A. Every patient at Oregon State Hospital (OSH) must have all medications
       reconciled upon admission, transfer, and discharge according to the appropriate
       procedures in Procedures A.
    B. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
       rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
       and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
       standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
       policy is more restrictive.
    C. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
       may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS

   A. “High-risk, high-alert medications” include those medications involved in a high
      percentage of errors or sentinel events, or carry a higher risk for abuse, error, or
      other adverse outcomes. OSH identifies the following medications as High Risk,
      High Alert – insulin, anticoagulants, clozapine, lithium, oxcarbazepine, and
      metformin.
   B. “Medication reconciliation” means the formal process of creating a complete and
      accurate list of each patient’s current medication at the time of admission,
      transfer, or discharge.
   C. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
