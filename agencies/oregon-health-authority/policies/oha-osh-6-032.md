# Marriage Requests (OSH Policy 6.032)

## At a glance

OSH Policy 6.032 — Marriage Requests. Oregon State Hospital (Oregon Health Authority). Effective JULY 31, 2024.

## Full text

                     OREGON STATE HOSPITAL
                                          POLICY
SECTION 6:       Patient Care                                               POLICY: 6.032

SUBJECT:         Marriage Requests

POINT PERSON:    Director of Spiritual Care

APPROVED:        Sara Walker, MD                                     DATE: JULY 31, 2024

                 Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
 A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) recognizes that a patient may request to be
    married while residing at OSH. OSH also recognizes that, before an OSH patient
    gets married, OSH must verify that the patient has the capacity to make an
    informed decision about getting married and that the unusual nature of the
    environment does not unduly influence the patient’s choice. OSH will comply with
    Oregon laws regarding marriage.

 B. This policy applies to all staff who work with clients seeking to be married while at
    OSH.

II. POLICY
 A. The interdisciplinary treatment team (IDT) is responsible to determine whether
    the patient has the capacity to make an informed decision to be married.
    This may include clinical recommendations, but those should be
    distinguished clearly from the assessment regarding capacity to make an
    informed decision about marriage.
 B. Staff should refrain from giving their personal opinions to the patient separate
    from the IDT’s official position
 C. The applicant, whether indigent or not, accepts responsibility for all fees incurred
    related to the marriage process, such as marriage license fees rings, and
    ceremony expenses. No government funds may be used for private parties.
 D. Spiritual Care Department Staff, including contractors, may not conduct marriage

     ceremonies at OSH.

 E. If one or both patients does/do not have off-ground privileges or if the couple
    chooses to get married at OSH, the ceremony must be performed by an
    authorized marriage official selected by the patient(s) that has completed the OSH
    Adult Visitor Application and screening process per OSH policy 8.026 “Visitors to
    Patients.”

         NOTE: The location of the ceremony determines the maximum number of
           guests. The guest number includes two witnesses and does not include the
           authorized marriage official who performs the ceremony. All guests and the
           authorized marriage official performing the ceremony must follow the visitor
           clearance process as indicated in OSH Policy and Procedure 8.026, “Visitors
           to Patients.

 F. OSH Spiritual care staff are not able to obtain a marriage license on behalf of
    patients, see procedures A for requirements.
 G. A patient request to be married must adhere to Procedures A.

 H. Any celebratory food must be paid for with patient or visitor funds only. Celebratory
    food and beverages must be purchased through OSH Food and Nutrition Services.

 I. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
    rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services, and
    Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation standards.
    Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this policy is more
    restrictive.
 J. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
    may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.
III. DEFINITIONS
 A. “Authorized marriage official” for the purposes of this policy means a person who
    is a marriage official who has a license to officiate a marriage at the Oregon
    State Hospital, and who has been cleared by Security. In this policy, the term
    “authorized marriage official” does not include Spiritual Department STAFF or
    contractors.
 B. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors, and
    other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
