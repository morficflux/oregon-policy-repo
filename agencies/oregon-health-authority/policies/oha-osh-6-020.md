III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Authorized bilingual employee” means an OSH employee proficient in one or
      more languages in addition to English, including signing for the deaf and hard of
      hearing, whose position description requires the employee to use their bilingual
      skills, and who has been approved by the OSH Human Resources Office to
      receive compensation for providing translation or interpretation assistance to
      patients.
   B. “Authorized translator” means a contractor approved through the DHS/OHA
      contracting process to provide translation services for DHS/OHA written
      materials.
   C. "Bilingual skills" refers to translation to and from English, interpretation of another
      language, or the use of sign language.
   D. “Contracted interpreter" means a person certified or qualified in the State of
      Oregon as a conversational, court, or health care interpreter, or a person certified
      by the National Registry of the Interpreters of the Deaf who is under contract with
      OSH to provide interpreter services.
   E. “Interpretation” as used in this policy means the transfer of an oral
      communication from one language to another, including sign language for the
      deaf and hard of hearing.
