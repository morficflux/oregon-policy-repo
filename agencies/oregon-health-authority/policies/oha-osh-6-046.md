 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. This Policy and Procedures establishes expected response to patient falls and
      subsequent care and preventative measures to reduce risk of falls at Oregon
      State Hospital (OSH).
   B. This policy applies to all staff excluding students, volunteers or vendors unless
      properly supervised or trained.

II. POLICY
  A. Every patient shall be assessed for risk of falling via a Fall Risk Assessment per
     Nursing protocol 2.200 “Fall Risk Assessment” as described in Procedures A.
  B. A patient who falls must be appropriately addressed, evaluated, and monitored.
     For response to a fall and fall risk reassessment, see Procedures A.
  C. If any member of the IDT or direct care staff observes a potential change in
     patient status that may impact their fall risk, they should communicate this to the
     RN. The RN must determine if a reassessment of the fall risk is needed,
     justification for reassessment or not conducting a reassessment must be
     documented in avatar.
  D. Staff must monitor the patient care environment and will reasonably address
     potential fall hazards.
  E. The Falls Committee is an interdisciplinary group of fall prevention subject-matter
     experts whose primary goals are to promote patient safety, understand and

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Fall” means a sudden, independent movement to the ground or lower surface
      that is unintentional, uncontrolled, or involuntary. It may be witnessed or not
      witnessed, with or without injury. A fall resulting from an aggressive interaction
      with another individual is not included in this definition. (NOTE: although fall
      monitoring processes would apply, such falls are reported separately.)
   B. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
