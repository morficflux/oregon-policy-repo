# Communication System Testing (OSH Policy 8.015)

## At a glance

OSH Policy 8.015 — Communication System Testing. Oregon State Hospital (Oregon Health Authority). Effective NOVEMBER 15, 2024.

## Full text

                          OREGON STATE HOSPITAL
                                              POLICY
SECTION 8:       Safety Department                                                   POLICY: 8.015

SUBJECT:         Communication System Testing

POINT
                 Director of Safety
PERSON:

APPROVED:        Sara Walker, MD                                       DATE: NOVEMBER 15, 2024

                 Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

    I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. Oregon State Hospital will maintain a safe environment for patients, staff, and
         others by conducting regular testing of communication systems, including but not
         limited to the duress alarm system and Everbridge system in accordance with
         applicable regulations, this policy and procedures, and department protocols.
      B. This policy applies to all staff at OSH.

   II. POLICY
      A. Communication systems must be regularly tested by Security,
         Safety, and designated unit staff.
      B. PMTs must be tested in accordance with OSH Policy and
         Procedure 8.035, “Duress Alarm System”.
              a. PMT testing locations are located at Junction City Sallyport 1 and
                 Salem Sallyports 2, 5, and 7.
              b. Staff may contact Junction City Access Control at (541) 465-3000 or
                 Salem Access Control at (503) 947-9984 for assistance with testing.
           C. If any device within the duress alarm system fails to meet its self-
              monitoring requirements, an error message must be sent to the
              monitoring stations in Access Control.
           D. Upon receipt of a system reporting error, Access Control staff must
              report the error to the Duress Coordinator or designee. The Duress
              Coordinator must verify and ensure appropriate corrective actions are

         E. Radios assigned to an individual staff or unit must be tested prior to

            a. For radio issues or broken equipment, contact Oregon State Hospital
               Security Office at (503) 945-2800 (Salem) or (541) 465-3000
               (Junction City).
         F. The public-address system must be tested campus-wide on a monthly
            cycle. Confirmation of adequate volume and clarity in each area must be
            recorded on the checklist.
         G. Blue emergency phones on the OSH Junction City property must be
            tested monthly by Security staff and results documented. System
            failures must be immediately reported to Facilities for repair or
            servicing.
         H. Safety staff will test satellite phones on a weekly basis and before staff
            assigned to patient outings to locations with poor or no cellular
            service.
         I. The Security Director, Safety Director, Duress Coordinator, the
            Environmental Safety & Emergency Preparedness Manager or
            designee is responsible for testing completion and must submit any
            work requests necessary to repair communication system issues
            discovered during testing.

         J. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes
            and rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared
            Services, and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant
            accreditation standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this
            policy unless this policy is more restrictive.
         K. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or
            protocols may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III.     DEFINITIONS
       A. “Duress alarm system” means a non-medical emergency alarm
          system consisting of personal mobile transmitters, fixed position
          transmitters, annunciator panels, and a camera monitoring system.
       B. “Everbridge system” refers to a mass communication system used to
          notify individuals or groups in the event of an emergency.
       C. “Fixed position transmitter (FPT)” means a stationary “push button” or
          equivalent device which transmits an emergency signal and location to

    D. "Personal mobile transmitter (PMT)" means a device that, upon activation
       for a non-medical emergency, transmits a signal to hospital annunciators
       and the Security Department.
    E. “Public address system” means an overhead paging system controlled by
       the Access Control operator.
    F. “Two-way radio” means a multi-channel handheld device that transmits
       verbal communications using radio frequencies.
    G. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors,
       vendors, and state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital
       (OSH)
