                     OREGON STATE HOSPITAL
                                          POLICY
SECTION 6:       Patient Care                                              POLICY: 6.060

SUBJECT:         Photographs for Patient
                 Identification

POINT PERSON:    Director of Security

APPROVED:        Sara Walker, MD                              DATE: NOVEMBER 15, 2024

                 Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Patient photographs taken by Oregon State Hospital (OSH) security staff are
      used for patient identification by staff for the accurate and safe delivery of
      treatment and services. Patient photographs are subject to applicable
      confidentiality laws.
   B. This policy applies to staff authorized to take photos of patients for patient
      identification purposes

II. POLICY
   A. A photograph must be taken of a patient:
      1. within 24 hours of admission;
      2. annually, if necessary;
      3. within 24 hours of an inter-campus transfer between the Salem and Junction
         City campuses, with exception of a temporary transfer to the medical unit.
         The receiving campus will complete the patient photograph process upon
         arrival of the patient;
      4. if an identification badge, or Avatar has poor photograph quality; and
      5. whenever a patient’s appearance changes to the extent staff may be unable
         to identify the patient easily or accurately by the photograph, including, but
         not limited to, significant changes in hair color or style, facial hair, facial
         tattoos, weight, eyeglasses, dentition, or general facial appearance.

   B. A photograph does not need to be repeated if taken within the previous month of
      one of the situations noted above and there is no significant change in the
      patient’s appearance.
   C. The camera and photograph settings must align to the following guidelines:
      1. minimum resolution dimensions must be at least 600 x 600 pixels;
      2. aspect ratio must be 1:1;
      3. the camera must be positioned more than four feet from the patient;
      4. the patient’s upper body and space above the patient’s head must be
         included in the viewfinder;
      5. the background must be well-lit or off-white and free of patterns, objects,
         textures, etc.;
         6. shadows caused by overhead lighting must be eliminated or minimized;
         7. distance between subject and background must be minimized.
   D. Admissions, Nursing Services and Security must follow Procedures A, “Patient
      Photograph Process” during the admission process or when requesting a new
      patient photograph.
   E. This policy applies to all staff, including employees, volunteers, trainees, interns,
      contractors, vendors, and other state employees assigned to work at OSH. Staff
      who fail to comply with this policy or related procedures may be subject to
      disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.
   F. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   G. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
      may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
“Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors, and
other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
