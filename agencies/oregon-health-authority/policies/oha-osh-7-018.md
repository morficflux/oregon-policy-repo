                       OREGON STATE HOSPITAL
                                            POLICY
SECTION 7:         Patient Rights                                            POLICY: 7.018

SUBJECT:           Meaningful Involvement

POINT PERSON:      Treatment Services Director

APPROVED:          Sara Walker, MD                                    DATE: April 1, 2025

                   Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
 A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) is committed to providing a means for residents to
    share their experiences and ideas to improve the quality of care, treatment, safety,
    and services provided at the hospital.
 B. This policy and procedures support and encourage meaningful involvement (MI) of
    residents in decisions which impact their eight dimensions of wellness (social,
    physical, emotional, intellectual, financial, environmental, occupational, or spiritual
    wellness).
 C. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
  A. The Treatment Services Director delegates authority to the Access and Care
     Coordinator who oversees the MI process.
  B. Event Consideration
    1. Events to consider for MI may include committees, quality improvement efforts,
       policy, procedure, protocol, and communication development.
             a. Committees require ongoing work and participation, if a resident leaves
                OSH, the position must be filled as soon as possible to maintain resident
                involvement.

  2. Events to not consider for MI may include those in which the following conditions
     are present:
        a. Patient Health Information (PHI) is shared
        b. Subject to attorney client privilege
        c. Staff performance issue related, issues related to staff, discipline,
            separation of service
        d. Participants are needed in less than 30 days
        e. It is prohibitive to operational flexibility to incorporate resident feedback
            (for example: when it compromises security, when participation would
            have no impact on the final decision, or when there is a narrow deadline).
  3. All staff must attempt to include residents in hospital decision making when the
     event qualifies for MI and or the work would have direct impact on the eight
     dimensions of wellness as outlined above.
 C. Events for which residents have been chosen to participate must have an
    appropriate ratio of resident representation. At OSH, this means that at least two
    members of the event must be a resident, with the goal of 20% of the event
    membership being comprised of residents.
 D. If only one resident is selected for an event, the Access and Care Coordinator
    must review the appropriateness of the resident to workgroup ratio.
 E. Staff shall follow processes outlined in Procedures A. this includes but is not
    limited to:
      1. Preparing for MI
      2. Announcing and recruiting residents for MI
      3. Resident selection
      4. Coordinating and supporting MI during the event
      5. Closing the MI process at either the conclusion of the event or when the
         resident chooses to withdraw their participation.
 F. Residents who participate in MI may not receive cash payment for their
    participation. Any honorarium or stipend payments for participation in OSH
    committees or workgroups outside of OSH must follow all applicable guidelines
    outlined in OSH policy 4.010 “Patient Funds” for funds received.
 G. Refer to the MI OWL page for the MI Resource Packet.
 H. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
    rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
    and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation standards.
    Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this policy is more
    restrictive.

III. DEFINITIONS
    A. “Event” refers to any hospital activity that impacts the eight dimensions of
       wellness. An event can include community events held outside of the hospital
       including but not limited to Oregon State Hospital Advisory Board meetings and
       Oregon Consumer Advisory Council meetings.
    B. “Meaningful involvement” (MI), for the purposes of this policy, means that
       opportunities are created for residents to participate in decision making that is
       equitable and that honors and recognizes the importance of their feedback/input.
    C. “Requestor” refers to the person responsible for coordinating resident
       participation. This includes the chair, co-chair, lead or designee of an event who
       makes the request and follows the MI process.
    D. For purposes of this policy, “Resident” means anyone who is receiving services
       at OSH on a hospital or residential unit.
    E. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
       and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
