                     OREGON STATE HOSPITAL
                                           POLICY
SECTION 6:        Patient Care                                               POLICY: 6.063

SUBJECT:          Hepatitis C Treatment

POINT PERSON:     Chief of Medicine

APPROVED:         Sara Walker, MD                                     DATE: JULY 25, 2024

                  Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) is committed to providing or facilitating medical
      treatment for patients diagnosed with hepatitis C. OSH will designate treatment
      options in keeping with the patient’s legal commitment status, their projected
      length of stay, and their anticipated ability to comply with treatment.
   B. This policy applies to all staff who may treat patients with hepatitis C.

II. POLICY
   A. OSH will not treat a patient with hepatitis C who is committed under Oregon
      Revised Statute 161.370.
   B. OSH will consider treating any other patient with hepatitis C if:
      1. The patient is expected to be at OSH for the duration of treatment; and,
      2. If the Interdisciplinary Treatment Team (IDT) concludes the patient is likely to
         comply entirely with the treatment.
   C. When a patient with hepatitis C will be treated while at OSH, the physician must
      order and evaluate appropriate labs or other studies; consult with clinical
      disciplines, Pharmacy, and the Chief of Medicine as needed; and monitor follow-
      up care as listed in the attached algorithm guidelines.
   D. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
