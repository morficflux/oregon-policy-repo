                                           POLICY
SECTION 7:        Patient Rights                                             POLICY: 7.016

SUBJECT:          Outdoors and Fresh Air Patient
                  Rights

POINT PERSON:     Director of Ombuds and Family
                  Services

APPROVED:         Sara Walker, MD                                     DATE: JULY 25, 2024

                  Interim Superintendent

SELECT ONE:

 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) supports a patient’s right to daily access the
      outdoors and fresh air and will provide opportunities for them to exercise these
      rights.
   A. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
   B. Patients will have the right to access the outdoors for at least 45 minutes each
      day, excluding gathering and transportation time. Patients also have the right to
      access fresh air in areas such as the air courts. An air court meets the
      requirement for accessing fresh air but does not meet the requirement for
      providing access to the outdoors.
      1. If a patient declines to go outside during the unit’s scheduled time but later
         asks to go outside, the unit is not required to accommodate the request.
      2. A patient who has been placed on “restrict to unit (RTU)” status maintains
         their right to daily access the outdoors and fresh air.
      3. For safety, a patient who is in seclusion or restraint is not entitled to access
         the outdoors or fresh air for the duration of the intervention. Staff are not
         required to inform them of this.

  C. OSH may temporarily restrict a patient’s right to access the outdoors when it
     would create a risk of significant harm to the patient or others. Only a registered
     nurse or physician/nurse practitioner may initiate the temporary restriction.
  D. When a patient is restricted from going outside, staff must immediately inform the
     patient and follow patient rights restrictions processes delineated in the patient
     rights restrictions policy, including obtaining a psychiatrist/psychiatric mental
     health nurse practitioner (PMHNP) order (see OSH Policy 7.015, “Patient Rights
     Restrictions Requirements”).
  E. Per the patient rights restrictions policy, the Superintendent, Administrator on
     Duty, or Chief Medical Officer may establish a blanket restriction preventing all
     patients from going outside or accessing air courts when the outdoor temperature
     is more than 90 degrees or for other medical or safety reasons.
        1. As directed by the patient rights restrictions policy, unit staff must verbally
           inform patients of the reason for the blanket restriction and when the
           restriction will end. They must also post written information in an area where
           patients may view it (e.g., “It is over 90 degrees outside, so we cannot go to
           the quads until it cools off”).
        2. Per OSH’s patient rights restrictions policy, during such blanket restrictions a
           psychiatrist/PMHNP order is not required for each patient. While in effect,
           interdisciplinary treatment teams (IDTs) are not required to document
           individual restriction plans in treatment care plans (TCPs) or submit the forms
           required by that same policy (see OSH Policy 7.015, “Patient Rights
           Restrictions Requirements”).
        3. OSH will designate outdoor areas for patients to use while a blanket
           restriction is in effect, unless the outdoor air quality is too unhealthy to safely
           be outside or in air courts. As needed, OSH will designate alternate times for
           patients to go outside.
  F. If the unit's staffing numbers are such that staffing both the unit and outdoors
     would create unreasonable risk of harm to the patient or others, the unit’s nurse
     manager may request the program nurse manager approve canceling that day’s
     scheduled time to go outside. The PNM may only approve the request one day at
     a time. They must promptly report the decision to the OSH Ombuds, Program
     Director, Director of Nursing, and Chief Nursing Officer.
  G. Staff are responsible to consider patient medical and physical needs when they
     are outside and to provide for their safety as needed (e.g., providing water,
     sunscreen, rain gear, gloves).
  H. Staff, including OSH leadership, will involve or consult with the OSH Ombuds on
     all patient rights issues, including clarification and interpretation of patient rights,
     and before implementing related process or policy changes.

   I. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   J. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
      may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
      and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
