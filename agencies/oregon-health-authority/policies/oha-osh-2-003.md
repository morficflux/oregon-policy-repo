                                POLICY ATTACHMENT

  RESPONSIBLE                                    PROCEDURES
PERSON/GROUP
Staff              1. Contact a primary care provider when exposure to,
                      transmission of, or evidence of an infectious disease
                      necessitates medical care.
                   2. Report signs and symptoms of an infectious disease to the
                      supervisor at the onset of symptoms.
                   3. Follow Oregon State Hospital (OSH) Policy and Procedure
                      5.012, “Injury or Illness Reporting”.
                   4. In the case of accidental (occupational) exposure to blood-
                      borne pathogens (BBP) or other potentially infectious material,
                      follow the “Occupational and Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure
                      Plan” as directed by Infection Prevention.
Staff Supervisor   1. Notify Infection Prevention, the Physician Advisor (the appointed
                      physician chairperson of the Infection Control Committee), or
                      the medical on-call doctor on weekends or after hours whenever
                      a staff reports signs and symptoms of an infectious disease.
                   2. Follow OSH Policy and Procedure 5.012, “Injury or Illness
                      Reporting.”
                   3. In the event of BBP exposure, complete an Incident Report
                   4. Provide a copy of the BBP Exposure Risk Level Assessment to
                      the staff member and Infection Prevention.
                   5. If needed, refer the staff member to their own health care
                      provider, urgent care, or emergency care facility.

PROCEDURES A: Staff Responsibilities                        POLICY NUMBER : 2.003

Infection        1. If needed, refer the staff member to their own health care
Prevention          provider, urgent care, or emergency care facility.
Department       2. In the event of investigation of a reportable disease, disease
                    outbreak or epidemic, report staff demographical and medical
                    information related to the case to the Oregon Public Health
                    Administrator in accordance with Oregon Administrative Rule
                    (OAR) 333-018-0000 and OAR 333-019-0002.
                 3. Follow the “Occupational and Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure
                    Control Plan” in the case of BBP exposure.
Human            Collaborate with Infection Prevention to consider and implement
Resources (HR)   any necessary work restrictions.
