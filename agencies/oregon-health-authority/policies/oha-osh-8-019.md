 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. All Oregon State Hospital (OSH) employees are responsible for protecting
      patients and staff by reporting and responding to alleged criminal acts and
      observations of contraband being introduced into the organization. This policy
      applies to criminal acts or alleged criminal acts which occur at OSH or while a
      patient is committed to OSH.
   B. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
   A. All staff who observe or are notified of an alleged criminal act must report via the
   processes described in Procedures A and B of this policy and other policies as listed
   below.
      1. OSH recognizes that many critical incidents may not rise to the level of a
         criminal act. Therefore, this policy should be read in conjunction with OSH
         Policy and Procedure 7.008, “Patient Abuse or Mistreatment Allegation
         Reporting.”
      2. Refer to OSH Policy and Procedure 6.016 “Sexual Activity involving Patients”
         and Procedures C for response to allegations of sexual abuse.
      3. Refer to OSH Policy and Procedure 8.033, “Workplace Violence Prevention”
         for incidents of criminal acts in which the victim is a staff member.

       4. Refer to OSH Policy and Procedure 1.003 “Incident reporting” for additional

   C. OSH Security follows Protocol 5.005 “OSP Criminal Investigation Referrals” to
      report alleged criminal acts and coordinate investigations with Oregon State
      Police or relevant law enforcement agency.
   D. Incident Reporting Systems Investigation (IRSI) Department and Standards and
      Compliance may initiate systems investigations related to events that may qualify
      as an alleged criminal act as determined by Incident Review, Superintendent, or
      designee.
   E. OSH Security will coordinate with Law Enforcement Agencies to establish
      interview timelines. ISRI and Standards and Compliance must collaborate with
      OSH Security to schedule interviews. OSH Security shall collaborate with OTIS to
      establish interview timelines.
   F. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   G. Failure to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols may
      be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Acute care hospital” for the purposes of this policy refers to Salem Health
      hospital in Salem or Sacred Heart hospital in Riverbend.
   B. “Contraband” means any controlled substance, drugs not prescribed to the
      patient, drugs not prescribed or authorized by OSH, drug paraphernalia,
      weapons, unauthorized currency or prohibited items.
   C. “Criminal act” or “alleged criminal act” means an act or crime actually or allegedly
      committed by a person which violates a law and is punishable by the state or
      federal government. Such acts may include, but are not limited to, assault,
      vandalism, theft, threat to staff, supplying contraband into OSH, or suspicious
      activity on an OSH campus.
       1. An "alleged criminal act" should be considered to have occurred if:
          a. a staff member has reasonable cause to believe a crime has been
             committed; or

         b. information has been reported to a staff member which, if true, would
            constitute a crime
  D. “Sexual abuse” is as defined by ORS 430.735(13). The definition of sexual abuse
     includes any incident which meets the definition of sexual assault, as well as
     incidents of non-consensual sexual behavior without sexual contact. An example
     of this includes but is not limited to exposing one’s genitals to someone else
     without their consent. (If any sexual behavior occurs in which one party is a staff
     and the other is a patient, it is always considered assault or abuse, even if the
     patient asserts that they consented.)
  E. “Sexual assault” means an incident of sexual contact between patients, patient to
     staff, staff to patient or non-consensual staff to staff where criminal activity is
     alleged to have occurred as defined by Oregon Criminal Code, including, but not
     limited to, non-consensual sexual intercourse or penetration, and those acts
     involving an alleged victim who lacks capacity to consent to sexual contact. (If
     any sexual behavior occurs in which one party is a staff and the other is a patient,
     it is always considered assault or abuse, even if the patient asserts that they
     consented.)
  F. “Sexual contact” is defined by ORS 163.305 and means any touching of the
     sexual or other intimate parts of a person or causing such person to touch the
     sexual or other intimate parts of the actor for the purpose of arousing or gratifying
     the sexual desire of either party.
  G. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
     and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
  H. Weapon means any item that can be used or modified to be used to harm the
     patient or others, including but not limited to guns, knives, improvised pointed or
     bladed instrument, and pepper spray.
