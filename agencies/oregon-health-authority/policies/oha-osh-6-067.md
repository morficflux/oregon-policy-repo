SELECT ONE:

I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy outlines the responsibility and expectation for Oregon State Hospital
         (OSH) staff in the use of opioids in treating chronic pain, to provide and monitor
         treatment for opioid use disorder with medication assisted treatment (MAT), and
         establishes expectations for staff to perform emergency reversal of suspected
         opioid-related overdose. The policy supports OSH’s capability to deliver effective
         health care to its patients by:
        1. offering patient-centered care, evidence-based care, harm reduction, linkage
           to treatment, and discharge with naloxone or prescription for naloxone when
           indicated;
        2. reducing harm of substance use at OSH;
        3. establishing a standing order for naloxone distribution and administration; and
        4. maximizing the safety of the patients, staff, volunteers, and visitors.
      B. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH practices a patient-centered treatment approach for substance use
         disorders (SUD) with the overall goal of reducing harm.
      B. OSH is committed to screening and identifying patients with SUD. The admitting
         psychiatric practitioner must screen for SUD and note their findings in the
         psychiatric admission assessment. The medical provider completing the initial
         medical history and physical examination must also screen for SUD and note
         their findings in the medical history and physical.

  C. Patients who screen positive for opioid use disorder may be offered evidence-
     based medications. MAT must be provided as required by the Medication-
     Assisted Treatment for Opioid Use Disorder Clinical Practice Guideline.
  D. Patients receiving long-term opioids for any reason (including, but not limited to,
     opioid use disorder and chronic pain management) are required to sign the
     Informed Consent and Management Agreement for Long Term Opioid Use.
  E. In cases of suspected opioid overdose of any individual on OSH property, the
     responder must first call a code blue. The responder does not need a verbal or
     written order from a prescriber to administer naloxone to a person suspected of
     experiencing an overdose. Attachment A: Standing Order for the Possession,
     Distribution, and Administration of Naloxone provides authorization for non-
     prescribers to administer naloxone in the event of a suspected overdose without
     an order specific to the person receiving the naloxone. Attachment A also
     contains the procedures for responding to opioid overdoses inside the secure
     perimeter and the procedures for responding to opioid overdoses outside the
     secure perimeter.
     1. All staff are required to complete training and policy attestation annually via
         online video and quiz on the recognition of opioid overdose and the
         administration of naloxone.
     2. Staff are not permitted to carry naloxone on off-grounds outings. If any staff
         or patient on an off-grounds outing appears to be experiencing an opioid
         overdose, staff must call 911.
     3. Staff must only use hospital-issued naloxone.
  F. OSH must provide and maintain on-site access to opioid overdose reversal
     medication naloxone (Narcan®) in various locations throughout the campus
     grounds, both within the secure perimeter and non-secure perimeter.
      1. Stock naloxone (Narcan®) must be clearly labeled, stored in original
          packaging, and stored in the following areas:

      2. Inside the secure perimeter, any Registered Nurse (RN), physician, or nurse
          practitioner (NP) may administer an opioid overdose reversal medication to
          treat any patient, staff, or member of the public suspected of having an
          opioid-related drug overdose, whether or not there is a known previous
          history of opioid use disorder.
          a. A Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) may administer an opioid overdose
              reversal medication at the direction of an RN.
      3. In areas that are inside an OSH building, but outside the secure perimeter,
          any Registered Nurse (RN), physician, or nurse practitioner (NP) may
          administer an opioid overdose reversal medication to treat any patient, staff,
          or member of the public suspected of having an opioid-related drug
          overdose, whether or not there is a known previous history of opioid use
          disorder.
            a. A Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) may administer an opioid overdose
                reversal medication at the direction of an RN.
            b. On the Salem Campus these areas include the Kirkbride building,
                Facilities area, and Main Lobby. On the Junction City Campus these
                areas include the Administrative Area and Main Lobby.
      4. Outside the secure perimeter, any staff member or layperson may
          administer an opioid overdose reversal medication to treat any patient, staff,
          or member of the public suspected of having an opioid-related drug
          overdose, whether or not there is a known previous history of opioid use
          disorder.
  G. The Pharmacy Department must conduct periodic audits of the inventory of
     naloxone for upcoming expirations, and proactively remove and replace expired
     products according to Pharmacy protocol 33.6 Medication Storage, Security, and
     Management.
  H. All patients with current orders for opioid medications, including buprenorphine,
     or who are being treated for opioid use disorder shall receive naloxone, or a
     prescription for naloxone, upon discharge from OSH, per ORS 441.052.
  I. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
     rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
     and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
     standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
     policy is more restrictive.
  J. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
     may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

 III.     DEFINITIONS
    "Secure perimeter” means restricted high-security buildings, areas, and quads within the
    sallyport exits that are operated to manage movement of persons within the OSH
    campus. The secure perimeter areas include all quads, the ball field, and Medical Clinic.
    “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors, and
    other state employees assigned to work at OSH.
    “Opioid-related overdose” occurs when a person takes too much of an opioid, or
    combination of opioids and other drugs, at a level that is toxic to the body. During an
    opioid related overdose, the part of the brain that regulates breathing is negatively
    affected, resulting in ineffective or complete cessation of breathing.
    “Standing order” means an order that becomes active without the use of a Pharmacy
    and Therapeutics Committee-approved protocol or without prior review of ordering
    practitioner.
