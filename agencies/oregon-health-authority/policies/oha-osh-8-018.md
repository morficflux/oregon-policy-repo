  SELECT ONE:

I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy establishes definitions and procedures for patient unauthorized leave
         events for Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
      B. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH staff are responsible to take reasonable and lawful actions as directed by
         this policy to attempt to return a patient who leaves the hospital's care and
         custody without authorization. This includes all admitted OSH patients, including
         those placed in OSH-operated on-campus residential treatment facilities.
      B. If a patient leaves OSH without authorization, staff must follow procedures A for
         response and reporting.
      C. External assistance for unauthorized leave may be requested as directed by the
         Director of Security.
        1. OSH must contact law enforcement for assistance with any unauthorized leave
           of a patient under forensic or civil commitment.
        2. For a voluntarily admitted patient, including a patient voluntarily admitted by
           guardian, OSH may contact law enforcement for assistance as directed by the
           Director of Security. The responding law enforcement officer determines
           whether to take the patient into custody.

    3. For a patient under forensic “aid and assist” or “guilty except for insanity” (GEI)
       commitment, the Superintendent or their designee may issue an order that
       creates a warrant for the patient’s arrest and detention according to Oregon
       Revised Statute (ORS) 161.375.
 D. Protected information and other information disclosure:
    1. OSH may disclose information about a patient on unauthorized leave or
       attempting unauthorized leave if:
         a. It is considered necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent
            threat to the health or safety of a person or the public, and
         b. The disclosure is made to a person or entity who is reasonably able to
            prevent or lessen the threat (e.g., law enforcement or the target of the
            threat) per 45 CFR 164.512 (j)(i).
    2. OSH may only notify victims of a patient on unauthorized leave in cases where
       there is a direct threat to that victim under the above provision or when OSH
       has been ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction to provide such
       notification.
   3. If a patient under the jurisdiction of the Psychiatric Security Review Board
      (PSRB), Oregon Youth Authority, or Department of Corrections is on
      unauthorized leave, OSH must immediately report their status to the applicable
      agency.
   4. If a patient was committed to OSH for arson or arsonist activity, OSH must
      immediately report their status to the State Fire Marshal and the Department of
      State Police.
 E. Incidents must be reported internally per OSH policy 1.003, “Incident Reporting.”
    1. OSH Incident Report System Investigators (IRSI) reviews reported incidents
       and may reclassify an incident based on the circumstances of the event.
    2. Staff must also review OSH policy 2.012, “Sentinel Events,” for incidents
       meeting the definition of unauthorized leave and significant attempt of
       unauthorized leave.
 F. In the case of unattended/wandering, staff must follow this policy and OSH policy
    6.024, “Transportation and Supervision Ratios.”
 G. The medical record for a patient on unauthorized leave remains subject to
    confidentiality laws and hospital policy.

III.    DEFINITIONS
        A. “Civil commitment," or “civil patient” for the purposes of this policy means a
           patient who is civilly committed per Oregon Revised Statute (ORS) 426.130 by
           order of a judge. A civil patient is not the same as a patient civilly committed under
           the forensic jurisdiction of the Psychiatric Security Review Board (PSRB) per ORS
           426.701.
        B. “Forensic commitment,” for the purposes of this policy means a patient
           committed to OSH under the following forensic jurisdictions:
           1. Psychiatric Security Review Board (PSRB), which includes Guilty Except for
              Insanity (GEI) under Oregon Revised Statute (ORS) 161.327 and civil
              commitment under ORS 426.701;
               a. “Forensic commitment” or “forensic patient” does not include civil
                  commitment under ORS 426.130.
           2. GEI-misdemeanor under ORS 161.328;
           3. Dual Department of Corrections (DOC) jurisdiction (see ORS 179.473); or
           4. Aid and assist (“.370”) order under ORS 161.370.
        C. “Non-significant attempt of unauthorized leave” means a person tried an
           unauthorized leave but did not have the means, capability, or opportunity to be
           successful. Examples include, but are not limited to:
           1. A patient evades observation of staff while inside the secure perimeter but did
              not make it inside of a sally port.

    2. A patient walks away from a group on an on-grounds outing and staff redirect
       the patient without physical intervention and never leave OSH property,
    3. A patient is physically restrained while attempting to leave OSH property
       boundary when inside the secure perimeter. (NOTE: A patient physically
       restrained while attempting to leave OSH property boundary that has made it
       inside of a sally port qualifies as a significant attempt of unauthorized leave).
 D. “Off-grounds” is as defined in OSH policy 6.006. Off-grounds means any area
    outside the boundaries of the Salem and Junction City campus grounds.
 E. “On-grounds” is as defined in OSH policy 6.006. On-grounds means any area
    outside the secure perimeter (e.g., outside a sallyport), while still on the OSH
    grounds (Salem or Junction City).
    1. On-grounds areas on the Salem campus include the Empowerment Center;
       Kirkbride building floors 2, 3, and 4; the OSH Museum; Cottages; tennis court
       parking lots and roadways; and the OSH disc golf courses.
    2. On-grounds areas on the Junction City campus include the Education and
       Development Department classrooms, National Alliance on Mental Illness
       (NAMI) office, administration second-floor areas, community gardens, NAMI
       memorial garden, challenge course (outside obstacle course), disc golf
       course, fruit orchard, parking lots and roadways, and the access road.
 F. “Secure perimeter” is as defined in OSH policy 6.006, “On-grounds and Off-
    grounds Movement. Secure perimeter means restricted high-security buildings,
    areas, and quads within the sallyport exits operated to manage movement of
    persons within the OSH campus. The secure perimeter areas include all quads,
    the ball field, and Medical Clinic.
    1. The secure perimeter for Salem is per OSH policy 6.006, Attachment B, and
       includes the Kirkbride café, chart rooms on every unit, and the cubicle areas
       other than in Kirkbride building (floors 2-4).
    2. The secure perimeter for Junction City is per OSH policy 6.006, Attachment
       C, and includes the Valley café, chart rooms on every unit, and the first floor
       cubicle area.
 G. “Significant attempt of unauthorized leave” means a patient tried an unauthorized
    leave and had the means and capability to be successful but was not successful.
    Examples of a significant attempt of unauthorized leave include, but are not
    limited to:
    1. A patient attempts to depart the secure perimeter and makes it inside a sally
       port but does not depart OSH secure perimeter.

          2. A patient is physically restrained while attempting to leave OSH property
             boundary when outside the secure perimeter.
          3. A patient attempts to scale a secure perimeter wall but does not exit the
             secure perimeter.
       H. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
          and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
       I. “Unattended/wandering” means a patient was found by staff in an area where
          they are not supposed to or expected to be in (on or off unit), the patient is
          without appropriate supervision within the secure perimeter and the event does
          not rise to any level of attempt of unauthorized leave.
          1. Examples of unattended/wandering include, but are not limited to:
              a. A patient gets left out of a staff count and unintentionally left behind.
              b. A patient is found unescorted on a treatment mall during non-treatment
                 mall hours.
              c. A patient is found in a staff only area.
              d. A patient is found in a treatment mall library without staff supervision.
       J. “Unauthorized leave” means a patient:
              a. Successfully leaves OSH boundaries without reasonable ability for staff to
                 pursue the patient, or
              b. Leaves the supervision of staff without authorization and is out of the line
                 of sight while outside the secure perimeter (this includes a patient
                 overstaying an off-grounds pass).
