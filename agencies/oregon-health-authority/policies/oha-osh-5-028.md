 I.      PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
         A. This policy establishes standardized procedures for tracking work hours,
            requesting leave, and managing overtime at Oregon State Hospital (OSH). This
            includes, but is not limited to, the use of the Electronic Timekeeping Management
            System (ETMS).
         B. This policy applies to all OSH staff, regardless of classification or work location.

II.      POLICY
         A. Timekeeping Requirements
            1. All staff (except Supervising Physicians, Physicians, and Nurse Practitioners)
               must use ETMS to record work time.
            2. The Superintendent reserves the authority to modify this list and may require
               additional staff to use ETMS at their discretion at any time.
            3. OSH staff exempt from using the ETMS must use the State of Oregon's
               official Human Resources/payroll system and follow all applicable state and
               agency policies.
         B. ETMS User Responsibilities
            ETMS users must:
            1. Accurately report all time worked (see Procedures A),

        2. Maintain an up-to-date work schedule, including approved flexible work
           schedules (see Procedures B),
        3. Submit leave requests, including protected leave (see Procedures C).
  C. ETMS Access and Management
        1. Access is granted based on job functions and the operational needs of the
           organization.
        2. Administrative privileges may only be used for authorized scheduling or
           delegated managerial functions specific to the ETMS users' work area.
        3. Managers must monitor ETMS usage to ensure appropriate access and
           compliance.

  D. Clocking Thresholds
        Clocking outside of defined thresholds (see Attachment A) will be rounded
        accordingly.
  E. Rest and Meal Breaks
  •     ETMS users must take two paid 15-minute rest breaks (one in each half of the
        shift) and at least one unpaid 30-minute meal break per full shift, following labor
        laws and agency policy. Meal break length may vary with manager approval.
  •     Rest Breaks: Two (2) paid 15-minute breaks should be taken during the first half
        of the shift, and the second during the second half. Rest breaks are considered
        paid time.
  •     Meal Breaks: At least one unpaid 30-minute meal break per shift, generally near
        the midpoint. Duration may vary with prior manager approval. The timing and
        duration of the meal break may vary based on operational needs and manager
        approval.
  •     Supervisors must ensure staff are scheduled and able to take all required breaks.
  •     Staff may not legally waive their rights to receive required rest and meal periods.
        Staff are not permitted to skip, shorten, lengthen, or combine breaks without prior
        approval from management.
  •     ETMS users with irregular or missed breaks should follow Procedures A.

  F. Work Outside Scheduled Hours
        Any work outside scheduled shifts or during unpaid meal breaks requires prior
        manager approval.

  G. Managers Responsibilities
        Managers must clearly communicate time management expectations and the
        appropriate use of the timekeeping systems. If staff are not meeting these
        expectations, Human Resources should be contacted for further guidance.
  H. Overtime
        1. Unless otherwise permitted under the applicable collective bargaining
           agreement(s), staff must follow Procedures D for submitting overtime (OT)
           requests.
           o   Use Attachment B (Overtime Request Form) as required.
           o   Unexpected OT over 30 minutes must be submitted within two business
               days
        2. Regularly scheduled on-call work is not considered overtime for this policy.
        3. Mandatory overtime shall be administered in accordance with applicable
           union collective bargaining agreements, relevant staffing laws, and
           established mandatory overtime procedures.

        4. Compensation for hours worked beyond an employee's agreed-
           upon daily schedule or full-time equivalent (FTE) status shall be
           provided in accordance with applicable collective bargaining
           agreements, agency compensation policies, and relevant local,
           state, and federal laws or regulations.

        5. Flexing of Work Schedules must follow:
           o   Weekly flexing: Procedures B
           o   Monthly Flex: Procedures E.
  I.    Time Sheet Corrections
        1. Timesheet corrections must be approved by individuals with signature
           authority as designated in OSH Policy 1.016 - OSH Delegation of
           Authority.
        2. Approved timesheet corrections may need to be processed outside of the
           ETMS system. Contact the Centralized Timekeeping Office to initiate or
           inquire about timesheet correction procedures.
        3. Timesheet corrections resulting from failure to meet established OSH ETMS
           user or Manager deadlines may result in coaching and may lead to
           disciplinary action.

 J. Training and Support
           o   All new users will receive standardized training on the ETMS system.
           o   Remedial training may be provided as required based on usage concerns.
           o   Additional educational resources and user support materials related to
               ETMS are available through the Centralized Timekeeping Office.
  K. Unauthorized Leave Without Pay (LU)
        1. Leave that has not been approved by the staffs immediate supervisor or
           designee will be designated as unauthorized leave without pay (LU).
           Unauthorized leave without pay may be a result of:
               i.   Exhausted leave accruals,
               ii. Failure to work the full shift or agreed-upon scheduled hours,
               iii. Failure to submit a timely request for the use of leave accruals.
        2. Each instance of unauthorized leave without pay will be reviewed by
           management and may involve consultation with Human Resources to
           determine appropriate follow-up or corrective action.
  L. Prohibited ETMS Use
        Inappropriate use of the ETMS is strictly prohibited and may result in corrective
        action in accordance with OSH policy and applicable collective bargaining
        agreements. Examples of inappropriate use include, but are not limited to:
        1. Tampering with ETMS hardware/software;
        2. Entering or altering ETMS data for another ETMS user without explicit
           authorization (e.g., adding missed clockings or submitting leave requests);
        3. Reporting work time that was not actually worked;
        4. Interfering with another ETMS user's ability to access or use the ETMS;
        5. Using another ETMS user's badge to badge in for that ETMS user;
        6. Using the ETMS for purposes unrelated to assigned job duties or business
           needs.
  M. Deadline Compliance
        Failure lo meet OSH ETMS user or manager deadlines may result in corrective
        action.
