# Visitors to Patients (OSH Policy 8.026)

  POINT PERSON:      Directory of Security

  APPROVED:          Sara Walker, MD                                 DATE: OCTOBER 4, 2024

                     Interim Superintendent

  SELECT ONE:

I.    PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
      A. This policy and related procedures are established to facilitate safety and
         security for patients, staff, and visitors during in person contact, non-contact, and
         video visits at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
      B. This policy applies to all staff.

II.   POLICY
      A. OSH supports patient contact with family and community members in
         accordance with a patient’s right to visitors per 42 C.F.R. § 482.13 and Oregon
         Administrative Rule (OAR) 309-106-0010.
      B. In person contact visitation may be permitted for patients residing on Secure
         Residential Treatment Facility (SRTF) units only as outlined in Procedures A “In
         Person Visitation Process.” All other patients may utilize video visitation
         (Procedures B) or non-contact visitation (Procedures C) as available.
      C. All prospective visitors, unless exempt by this policy, must apply to visit OSH
         using the visitor application (attachment A) and must be reviewed and approved
         or denied per Procedures D.
      D. The following persons are exempt from the visitor application process, including
         the criminal records check:
         1. A patient’s attorney recognized by the court as representing the patient, or an
            agent of the attorney (e.g., investigator, paralegal), given each person

     2. A court-appointed, attorney-appointed, or otherwise contracted licensed
        psychologist or psychiatrist whose purpose of conducting an independent
        evaluation of the patient and where legal authority to conduct the evaluation
        has been provided to OSH, such as but not limited to a court order or letter of
        engagement signed by the patient, guardian, judge, or patient’s attorney; or
     3. A patient’s legal guardian who has supplied proof of guardianship.
  E. Although a patient’s attorney or clergy is not restricted to established visiting
     hours or location per OAR 309-106-0010, OSH will encourage attorneys and
     clergy to use established visitation hours and locations, so patients are not
     unduly disrupted.
     1. Attorneys and clergy must call 2 hours in advance to set up an appointment
        outside of established visiting hours.
  F. An annual criminal records check must be completed for each prospective visitor
     aged 18 years and older unless an exception in this policy applies.
      1. A current, approved visitor who meets criteria listed in the Annual Law
         Enforcement Data System (LEDS) must have an annual review of criminal
         records check.
      2. Any visitor with criminal history must have the information processed
         according to the standard process listed in this policy.
  G. The Director of Security may place limitations on visitation by notifying the visitor
     and patient in writing because they do not meet visitation criteria or based on a
     safety concern, security risk, or disruption to hospital operations.
  H. Security shall review patient/visitor behavioral events during visitation and
     determine whether a visit should be terminated, and whether there should be any
     restrictions to future visits.
  I. The Director of Security must review any visits requests between a patient and
     former staff, deny or approve the visitation based on criteria in this policy and
     based on considerations of safety and security to the patient, staff, and hospital.
     1. The Director of Security must consult with the Superintendent to review staff
        history.

  J. Current staff may not visit a patient unless reviewed by the program director, the
     interdisciplinary treatment team (IDT), and approved by the director of security or
     designee.
         a. All staff who visit a patient on non-working time must follow the outlined
            visitation process.
  K. When two patients living on two separate units request to visit each other, the
     patients must first obtain the approval of both IDTs by completing the OSH visitor
     application. If both patients are housed on SRTF units, in-person visitation is
     permitted, non-contact visitation or video visitation may also be utilized. Security
     must approve both patient’s applications for a visit between patients to occur.
  L. Three-way visits (i.e., visitor and two patients from different areas) must be
     reviewed by the IDTs, Program Director(s) and approved by Security upon
     completion and submission of a visitor application for each person seeking to
     participate in the visit. If all patients are housed on a SRTF unit, in person
     visitation is an option; otherwise, the patients must utilize video visitation.
  M. In addition to being approved by Security, a visit with a minor must be reviewed
     and supported by the patient’s IDT and determined to be therapeutic and safe for
     all involved.
    1. All minors must be accompanied by a pre-approved adult visitor who is a
       parent, legal guardian, or court-ordered escort to enter a visitation area.
       Visitors must provide documentation confirming that each child visiting is their
       child, in their legal custody or court-ordered physical custody at the time of the
       application.
    2. A minor may only visit through video or during contact visits unless an
       exception is approved by the program director and security leadership.
  N. Staffing exceptions are coordinated by the unit nurse manager, visitation
     committee, and/or IDT. Staffing expectations are outlined in Nursing protocol
     2.260 and Security Protocol 5.019.
     1. Visitation staffing ratios must allow for maintenance of required unit staffing
        throughout the hospital.
  O. Video visitation is available to all patients and their approved visitors.
     1. Refer to Procedures B for scheduling video visitation and OSH Patient
        Visitation page on OWL for additional video visitation resources.
     2. The Visitation Committee establishes visitation hours, visitation length, and
        staffing requirements for video visitation.

     3. Video visits will be held in a designated location on each unit determined by
        unit staff and applicable staffing requirements of each unit. An exception may
        be requested for visits that may require increased privacy for confidentiality
        purposes.
  P. Visitation may be supervised, limited to a specific location, or denied as set out in
     this policy where there is reason to believe such measures are necessary or
     reasonable. Limitations or denials of visitation must be documented as set out in
     this policy. Denials of visitation may be appealed as set out in OAR 309-106-
     0035.
  Q. Visitation may be limited where the unit or hospital is under a medical restriction
     because of a communicable disease, or where the visitor may have a
     communicable disease or refuses to comply with required preventative protocols.
     The Infection Prevention Department may recommend or require other options
     for visitation while the hospital is in a communicable disease situation. A visitor
     may be required to wear personal protective equipment, including a mask, and/or
     undergo health screening (i.e., temperature check and health questions) as
     recommended by IPEH while a medical restriction is in effect.
  R. A visitor may only bring a pet or service animal to in-person visitation if the
     animal has been approved in accordance with OSH policy 6.059, “Animals at
     OSH.”
  S. Staff must ensure visitors do not bring in any property, including food items, and
     must comply with OSH policy 7.001, “Mail and Packages for Patients,” 8.037
     “Patient Property,” and 4.010 “Patient Funds.”
  T. If an unauthorized item is discovered in visitation:
     1. If the item is illegal or criminal in nature, staff must follow OAR 309-108,
        applicable security protocols and actions as follows:
        a. Notify security as necessary;
        b. Create a chain of custody and hold the item in a secure area;
        c. Contact law enforcement and turn over the contraband, if law enforcement
              is willing to accept it, and document the action taken;
        d. If law enforcement declines to investigate further or accept the
              contraband, then dispose of the contraband in the presence of at least two
              OSH staff, and document the action taken;
        e. Complete an incident report as outlined in OSH policy 1.003 “Incident
              Reporting.”
     2. If it is an item that is not illegal but is not permitted inside the secure
        perimeter, staff must escort the visitor to the secure lobby and require the

  U. A visitor must follow visitor guidelines during in-person and video visits or while
     on OSH property. For clinical and safety reasons, refusal to follow visitor
     guidelines will result in visits being terminated, and may result in restrictions,
     denial of visitations, or the loss of visiting privileges.
    1. Visitors must not bring items for patients to use during their visit or to take
       back to their unit.
    2. Visitors are permitted to bring only these items into the secure perimeter:
         a. OSH locker keys;

         c. Pre-disclosed and approved prescription medication;

                ii. A single baby blanket;

                iv. One undamaged plastic toy.
    3. Strollers, baby carriers, car seats, and stuffed animals are not allowed in the
        secure perimeter.
    4. Any additional items must be approved on a case-by-case basis by the
        security shift supervisor.
  V. An OSH patient who receives care at another healthcare facility is not allowed
     visitors at that facility unless:
     1. The visit is consistent with that facility’s visitation policies,
     2. There are extenuating circumstances to support the necessity of the visit,
     3. The Program Director, Chief Medical Officer, Chief of Medicine, or Social
        Work has communicated with that facility about OSH concerns or
        requirements for the visit, and
     4. The Director of Security or designee approves the request.
  W. The OSH Security Department must provide training to all OSH Security
     Department personnel at the time of hire on the provisions of this policy.
  X. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
     rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
     and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation

III.     DEFINITIONS
         A. “Attorney” means any lawyer actively licensed by the Oregon State Bar.
         B. “Clergy” means any member of the community who is recognized by a religious
            authority and who has been accepted, oriented, trained, and approved by the
            Oregon State Hospital Spiritual Care Department. “Clinical staff” means any
            person employed or contracted by OSH to provide clinical services to patients.
         C. “Contraband” means any controlled substance, drugs not prescribed to the
            patient, drugs not prescribed or authorized by OSH, drug paraphernalia,
            weapons, unauthorized currency, or prohibited items.
         D. “Denied” in this policy means prohibited.
         E. “Direct Care Staff” means any health care workers, either employed directly or
            contracted with OSH, who work directly with patients in the hospital, including but
            not limited to physicians, nurse practitioner, registered nurse, nursing assistants,
            therapists, and technicians
         F. “Forensic commitment” or “forensic patient” is as defined in OSH policy 6.029,
            “Risk Review and Privileges.” Forensic commitment or forensic patient means a
            patient committed to OSH under the following forensic jurisdictions:
            1. Psychiatric Security Review Board (PSRB), which includes Guilty Except for
               Insanity (GEI) under Oregon Revised Statute (ORS) 161.327 and civil
               commitment under ORS 426.701 and 426.702.
            2. GEI-misdemeanor under ORS 161.328; or
            3. Dual Department of Corrections (DOC) jurisdiction (see ORS 179.473).
            4. “Forensic commitment” or “forensic patient” does not include civil commitment
               under ORS 426.130.
         G. “Legal guardian” (“guardian”) for the purpose of this policy means an individual
            appointed by a court of law to act as guardian of an adult under ORS Chapter
            125.
         H. “Minor” means any person less than 18 years of age.

  I. “OSH Property” means OSH buildings, surrounding grounds, and parking lots.
     Personally owned vehicles parked on OSH property are not considered part of
     OSH property.
  J. “Prohibited Item” means:
      1. Alcohol;
      2. Possession of drugs in the patient’s room without authorization by OSH;
      3. Any item that reasonably could be used to escape or leave the hospital
         without authorization;
      4. Food stored in the patient room or in the patient’s storage in the patient
         property room;
      5. Any item in the possession of a patient who is prohibited from possessing or
         using that item in their treatment care plan because it is considered
         detrimental to their treatment;
      6. Excess personal property;
      7. Any limited access item in the possession of a patient that exceeds the
         scope of the authorization or is not being supervised by OSH staff as
         required; or
      8. Any item that could pose a safety or security risk for the hospital.
  K. “Restricted” in this policy means approved with limitations.
  L. “Secure Perimeter” is defined in 309-106-0005 as a restricted high-security
     buildings, areas, and quads within the sally port entrances and exits at the state
     hospital.
  M. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
     and other state employees assigned to work at OSH.
  N. “Visitation Committee” is a group of OSH management staff working to centralize
     the patient visitation process and to support/encourage patient visitation while
     meeting the hospital’s need to maintain patient safety and security.
  O. “Visitor” means an individual who is not an OSH staff member or another patient
     and who OSH has authorized to enter a designated visiting area in the hospital to
     meet with a patient.
  P. “Visit” or “visitation” means a meeting at the hospital between patient and an
     approved visitor who are permitted to see and talk with each other on a
     scheduled basis for a reasonable period of time.
  Q. “Weapon” means any item that can be used or modified to be used to harm the
     patient or others, including but not limited to guns, knives, improvised pointed or
     bladed instrument, and pepper spray.
