 I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. This policy concerns the reasonable assistance that staff must offer to a person
      experiencing an apparent emergency medical condition.
   B. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
   A. In accordance with Oregon State Hospital (OSH) licensing requirements, OSH
      does not provide emergency medical, surgical, or psychiatric care to the public or
      staff. However, staff must offer reasonable assistance to any person
      experiencing an apparent emergency medical condition within the scope of their
      training and according to the provisions of this policy and procedures.
   B. If a person appears to experience an emergency medical condition, including
      when a member of the public comes to OSH seeking treatment, a physician,
      nurse practitioner (NP), or registered nurse (RN) must assess whether the
      person is experiencing an emergency medical condition and determine whether
      the person needs additional emergency care.
      1. If needed, staff must initiate Code Blue per OSH Policy and Procedure 8.038,
         “Code Blue Medical Emergency”.
      2. When a member of the public comes to OSH seeking treatment and a
         physician, NP, or RN determines the person is experiencing a psychiatric
         emergency medical condition, staff may call 911 when needed.

         3. Staff may refer the person to the person’s primary medical or psychiatric
            provider or assist them with contacting the psychiatric acute crisis system in
            their county of origin.
         4. Staff may not transport the person seeking care or treatment.
   C. If a person experiences an emergency medical condition during an off-grounds
      outing, staff must call 911.
         1. Until EMS response arrives, staff should assess and treat the person as
            appropriate within their professional scope of practice and training.
         2. Once EMS arrives, staff should request the person experiencing an
            emergency medical condition be transported to the nearest appropriate care
            facility.
   D. When a staff member experiences an emergency medical condition and a
      physician, NP, or RN assesses they need further medical assessment or care,
      staff must follow the OSH policy on injured staff transportation.
   E. In the event a person exercises the “Safe Place for Newborns Act” and wishes to
      leave a newborn at OSH, staff must follow related procedures for receiving a
      newborn at OSH.
   F. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
      rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
      and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
      standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
      policy is more restrictive.
   G. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
      may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
   A. “Emergency medical condition” has the same meaning as the term in 42 Code of
      Federal Regulations § 489.24 and means:
         1. a medical condition manifesting itself by acute symptoms of sufficient severity
            (including severe pain, psychiatric disturbances and/or symptoms of
            substance abuse) such that the absence of immediate medical attention could
            reasonably be expected to result in:
            a. placing the health of the individual (or, with respect to a pregnant woman,
               the health of the woman or her unborn child) in serious jeopardy;
            b. serious impairment to bodily functions; or
