I. PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
   A. Oregon State Hospital (OSH) staff will be given reasonable and timely access to
      the hospital areas for which they need to perform their job safely and effectively.
      This policy establishes requirements for issuing keys and responsibilities
      concerning possession and issuance of keys.
   B. This policy applies to all staff.

II. POLICY
   A. Only an OSH locksmith is permitted to reproduce, cut, or stamp keys based on
      the Schlage Everest-B master key system. Locks and keys based on non-
      Schlage Everest-B systems may only be used with the approval of the OSH
      Salem Lead Locksmith and as allowed in this policy.
   B. All keys issued by OSH are considered to be loaned keys and are not the
      property of the recipient. Once issued, keys may not be altered, taped, or
      otherwise marked.
   C. Keys may only be issued on an as-needed basis.
      1. A recipient may not be issued or permitted to carry two or more of the same
         key.
      2. A recipient may not be issued or permitted to carry a lower-level key if a
         higher-level master key was already issued to the recipient.
      3. Lower-level keys must be turned in before master-level keys will be issued.
   D. Only the final recipient may sign key issuance receipts.

  E. A key recipient may not transfer or loan their issued key to any other person.
  F. If a key is no longer needed or obsolete, it must be immediately returned to the
     original issuing location’s Reception or lock shop. If a staff member transfers to a
     different department, the supervisor from the former department must direct the
     staff member to return the keys that are no longer needed.
  G. Keys may be assigned to program or departmental key cabinets subject to the
     Salem Lead Locksmith’s approval.
  H. With the exception of padlocks on employee lockers, personally-owned locking
     devices are not allowed in OSH.
  I. Requests to change a door lock function or to have a key cylinder core keyed
     differently than the area’s normal key system requires the written approval from
     the program director, department director, or Security Director.
  J. The locksmiths may deny a key request for security reasons.
  K. Disputes related to the issuing of keys, access to departmental key cabinets, or
     use of loaner keys must be presented to the Security Director for resolution.
  L. Reports on key status (e.g., issued, overdue, lost, stolen, broken or destroyed)
     and reports on metal key accessed doors must be made available upon request.
  M. The Office of Human Resources must notify the lock shops and motor pools of all
     staff name changes, lateral transfers, demotions, promotions, and separations.
  N. Master keys and sally port keys may be maintained in a locked key cabinet for
     Security manager’s use. These keys may be temporarily loaned to non-
     supervisory staff in emergency situations only.
  O. Departmental key cabinets must be audited.
        1. Department management or supervisory staff must perform an audit on keys
           issued from their departmental key cabinet once per shift.
        2. Discrepancies must be reported immediately to the Director of Security. The
           lost key process in this policy must be followed.
        3. The local lock shop must perform an annual audit of each departmental key
           cabinet.
  P. Keys must be returned to the original issuing location’s Reception or lock shop,
     including keys recovered after an employee’s separation.
        1. Separating staff must pay any replacement charge fees for all lost keys or
           keys not returned before receiving their final paycheck.
        2. The Reception or lock shop receiving the key must record the key number,
           sequence number, and returned date on a “Key Return Receipt” form (OSH-
           PLO0086 1-7/90).

         3. A copy of the key return receipt must be offered to the person returning the
            key.
         4. If the key was returned to Reception, the key must be immediately returned to
            the lock shop that issued it.
    Q. OSH follows all applicable regulations, including federal and state statutes and
       rules; Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS), Shared Services,
       and Oregon Health Authority (OHA) policies; and relevant accreditation
       standards. Such regulations supersede the provisions of this policy unless this
       policy is more restrictive.
    R. Staff who fail to comply with this policy or related policy attachments or protocols
       may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

III. DEFINITIONS
    A. “Departmental key cabinet” means any storage area used for organizing OSH
       keys or rings of keys which are loaned to staff.
    B. “Key issuer” for the purposes of this policy means staff authorized to give or
       exchange keys with the final key recipient. In this policy, key issuer includes
       locksmiths and Reception personnel.
    C. “Staff” includes employees, volunteers, trainees, interns, contractors, vendors,
       and other state employees assigned to work at Oregon State Hospital (OSH).
