PURPOSE

Establish guidelines to promote energy and resource conservation and safety during celebration seasons.

APPLICABILITY

State agencies that own, operate or lease buildings as defined in this policy.

DEFINITIONS

Agency:                      Any state agency, board, commission, department or division.
Agency Coordinator:          Person appointed by an agency director to facilitate and coordinate the energy and
                             resource conservation efforts within an agency.
Facility Manager:            Single point of contact for tenants in a state-owned building.

Site Coordinator:            Person located at a branch or other agency location who works with the Agency
                             Coordinator on energy and resource conservation.

GUIDELINES

1.   Celebration Decorations

     a. Natural Decorations
        Remove decorations made of natural materials, including trees, at first sign of dryness, such as
        brittleness, loss of color, or fallen needles. Trees and other natural cut decorations must be removed if
        the building is unoccupied for more than three (3) days.

         A. Do not apply fire retardant, flocking or other coatings to natural trees.
         B. Saw off trunk at an angle, at least one inch above the original cut.
         C. Mount securely in a tree stand, pail, tub or other water holder or reservoir.
         D. Add fresh water daily to maintain the water level above the cut.

     b. Artificial Decorations
        Use electrical devices in artificial decorations in the manner and capacity designed by the
        manufacturer. Exception: Do not use artificial decorations made with metal surfaces together with any
        type of electrical devices, decorations or appliances.

     c. All Decorations
        A. Choose decorations with natural fire retardant or items treated with a flame retardant that
           complies with the current Oregon Fire Code, Sections 806 and 807 Oregon State Fire Marshal :
           Oregon Fire Code : Fire Service Partners : State of Oregon. Note: Fire-retardant coating is not
           required or recommended on natural cut trees and will not extend the length of time a tree should
           be permitted indoors.
        B. Outside Celebration Seasons, use only non-electrical decorations.
        C. Inflatable, air-blown decorations requiring electricity or emitting sound and decorations intended for
           outdoor use are not allowed indoors or outdoors.
        D. Access to exits and exit paths must not be obstructed by decorative materials.
        E. Refer to the State Fire Marshal’s website for further information: Oregon State Fire Marshal.
        F. Refer to additional holiday décor information in the Oregon Fire Marshals Association Technical
           Advisory 22-09

2. Celebration Lights

     a. Use celebration lights within the following restrictions:
        • Use only in common areas, such as reception areas, lobbies or break rooms.
        • Operate from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. only.
        • Use only UL-listed miniature or Energy Star certified LED Lights.
        • Use only heavy-duty, UL-listed extension, grounded cords (14-gauge minimum, 15-amp minimum).
        • Remove electrical lights and extension cords that show physical damage.
        • When building is unoccupied, disconnect the power supply to all electrically energized decorations.

3. State Policy 107-011-010, Energy and Resource Conservation Applies

     All statewide energy conservation measures and waste prevention guidelines apply to celebration
     decorations as outlined in State Policy 107-011-010, Energy and Resource Conservation.

4. Safety Considerations

   a. Follow the safety guidelines below when adding or removing decorations:
      • Use only wooden or fiberglass ladders around electrical lines. Metal ladders conduct electricity,
          which causes shocks.
      • Never work alone on a ladder; a second person should hold the ladder.
      • Ensure the correct ladder height for the project.
      • Never set a ladder on an unstable surface.
      • Never exceed a ladder’s weight limit and never stand on its bucket shelf.
      • Never carry equipment while climbing.
      • Face the ladder when climbing up or down, keeping the body centered between the rails.
      • Keep cords out of walking aisles, but not under rugs where they could cause a fire or tripping
          hazard.
      • Never block doorways or foot paths.
      • Place decorations away from heat sources.
