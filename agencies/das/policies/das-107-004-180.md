PURPOSE

This policy establishes requirements for agencies to request funding for information technology (IT) budgets
and IT projects, including replacements, modernizations, upgrades and expansions of IT systems. This policy
supports an approach for prioritizing IT funding requests for the Oregon Executive Branch.

APPLICABILITY

This policy applies to all state agencies as defined in ORS 174.112, and includes any board, commission,
department, division, office or other entity within the Oregon executive department. The following agencies and
boards are excluded:
   • Secretary of State
   • State Treasurer
   • Oregon State Lottery
   • Public university listed in ORS 352.002
   • Semi-independent agencies listed in ORS 182.454
   • Oregon Film and Television Office (ORS 284.365)
   • State Fair Council (ORS 565.555)
   • Travel Information Council (ORS 377.840)

DEFINITIONS

Information technology (IT): all present and future forms of hardware, software and services for data
processing, office automation and telecommunications. Services relate to Enterprise Information Services
service lines including geographic information systems (GIS) and data inventory related activities.

IT project: a temporary technology endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.
Typically, projects have project managers assigned, are approved by agency governance and are officially
chartered. A project may be a component of an IT program.

GENERAL INFORMATION

Each state agency that intends to request funding (regardless of fund type) for an IT budget or IT project shall
submit the request in the format and timeframe detailed in Statewide Procedure 107-004-180_PR Information
Technology Readiness. IT projects exceeding the cost of $1,000,000 require a prioritization business case and
project prioritization worksheet. The project prioritization worksheet is a component of the IT Readiness
Workbook.

IT budget requests may include the agency’s current service level budget amount for positions, services and
supplies, capital outlay as well as additional needs above the current service level. IT budget requests may
also include needs for IT projects that fall below the $1,000,000 threshold.

Each request should clearly present alignment with the prioritization criteria (Attachment A). Detailed
questions to conduct prioritization analysis are available in the IT Readiness Workbook.

IT Requests Prioritization

The State Chief Information Officer (CIO), in collaboration with the Legislative Fiscal Office (LFO), shall
establish criteria for assigning priorities among the requested IT budgets and IT projects.

Enterprise Information Services (EIS), in collaboration with affected state agencies, shall evaluate submitted IT
budget and IT project requests, using the prioritization criteria (Attachment A).

EIS shall recommend priority rankings of the requests for the State CIO approval, in the following contexts:
    • requests within each state agency
    • requests within a legislative program area
    • requests for the executive department as a whole

IT Funding Reporting

The State CIO shall, not later than November 30 of each even-numbered year, submit to the Governor and the
Joint Legislative Committee on Information Management and Technology a report that includes:
    • priority ranking of requests within each state agency
    • priority ranking of requests within an Executive Branch program area
    • priority ranking of requests for the executive department as a whole
    • recommendations for design, delivery and management of identified IT services as enterprise or
        shared IT services
    • status of new enterprise or shared IT services
    • a description of critical IT system or services needs not otherwise identified within the priority rankings

The State CIO, not later than October 31 of each odd-number year, in collaboration with the LFO, shall submit to
the Governor and the Joint Legislative Committee on Information Management and Technology, a report that
lists all the IT budget and IT project requests that the Legislative Assembly approved in the prior concluding
legislative session.

Attachment A: Prioritization Criteria

Strategic Alignment

       (1)     Aligns with and supports implementation of the Enterprise Information Resources
               Management Strategy described in ORS 276A.203 (4)(a)(M) (...the Enterprise
               Information Resources Management Strategy must provide for integrating statewide
               technology initiatives, ensuring compliance with information technology rules,
               policies and standards, promoting coordination, consolidation and alignment of
               information resources and technologies and effectively managing the state’s and
               state agencies’ information technology portfolios.)
       (2)     Aligns with the agency’s IT and business strategic plans

Technology Best Practices

       (1)     Enhances the information security posture of the agency or the state
       (2)     Involves or promotes the use of shared systems, resources or data
       (3)     Aligns with the State CIO adopted technology standards
       (4)     Uses commercially available systems or software

People and Business -Centered Approach

       (1)     People-centered approach
       (2)     Business process transformation
       (3)     Investment risk (investment fulﬁlls a legislative mandate, enables compliance
               with current state or federal law, or addresses speciﬁc audit ﬁndings)

Agency Readiness and Solution Appropriateness

       (1)     IT governance and project management processes
       (2)     Organizational change management
       (3)     Solution scale and approach
       (4)     Capacity
