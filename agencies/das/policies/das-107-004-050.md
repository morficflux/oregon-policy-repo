# Information Asset Classification Policy (DAS Statewide Policy 107-004-050)

## At a glance

**[SUMMARY]** Defines how Oregon state government identifies, classifies, and protects
data and information assets across their lifecycle — the classification-level scheme the
IT Control Standards key off ("controls for accessing, processing, and storing
information at defined information asset classification levels"). Effective 7/12/2023
(supersedes the 2008 version); approved by Terrence Woods, State CIO. Note: the policy's
REFERENCE header still cites OAR 125-800-0005/-0020, which were renumbered to
128-030-0005/-0020 effective 5/1/2026.

## Key provisions

### Purpose

> **[VERBATIM]** "This policy defines Oregon state government's approach to identifying,
> classifying, and protecting state data and information assets throughout their
> lifecycles. Information, like other assets, must be properly managed from its creation
> to disposal. For the purpose of this policy, data is incorporated into the definition of
> “information.”"

### Applicability

**[SUMMARY]** All state agencies as defined in ORS 276A.230, including any Executive
Branch board, commission, department, division, or office; excludes Secretary of State,
State Treasurer, the Attorney General (as to ORS 276A.303 authority), and others listed
in the source. (Source: Applicability section.)
