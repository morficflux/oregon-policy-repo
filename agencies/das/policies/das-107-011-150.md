# Service Animals (DAS Statewide Policy 107-011-150)

## At a glance

Guides how "service animal" applies under Titles II and III of the ADA in
facilities owned, operated, or leased by DAS. Effective May 20, 2025 (supersedes the 2018
version); owned by Operations and Maintenance. Explicitly does not cover Title I (employee
workplace accommodation requests, which fall under HR policy 50-020-10).

PURPOSE

To provide guidance on the term “service animal” as it applies to Titles II and III of the Americans with
Disabilities Act (ADA) and where service animals are allowed in facilities owned, operated or leased by the
Department of Administrative Services (DAS). This policy does not apply to Title I of the ADA. Employee
requests to use a service animal in the workplace fall under the State HR Policy 50-020-10, ADA and
Reasonable Accommodation in Employment.

APPLICABILITY

Any tenant agencies, employees and visitors occupying facilities owned, operated or leased by DAS.

FORMS/EXHIBITS/INSTRUCTIONS

   •   125619 – Service Animal Request for Exception

   •   125602 – Project Authorization Request

   •   RiskWise – Animals in the Workplace

DEFINITIONS

Animal: a living organism of the animal kingdom that is not a human being or plant.

Assistance animal: has the same meaning as a service animal as authorized in ORS 659A.143.

Educational animal: has the same meaning as a service animal.

Facility: any enclosed building, parking lot or parking structure. Facility does not include: a building, lot, or
structure designed to house non-human animals, and facilities legally occupied by entities that are not state
agencies. Facility does not include Department of Corrections (DOC) institution as defined in ORS 421.005; a
state hospital as defined in ORS 162.135; a youth correction facility as defined in ORS 420.005; a local
correction facility or lockup as defined in ORS 169.005; or an institution or facility that is in its nature distinctly
private.

Service animal: As of March 15, 2011, the ADA Titles II and III only recognize dogs as service animals. A
service animal is a dog trained to do work or perform tasks for an individual with a disability as defined under
Titles II and III of the ADA. A service animal is not a pet. Dogs whose sole function is “the provision of
emotional support, well-being, comfort, or companionship” are not considered service animals under the ADA.
An animal individually trained to provide aggressive protection, such as an attack dog, is also not
appropriately considered a service animal. There is no definition of service animal under the employment
provision (Title I) of the ADA.

Tenant agency: any state agency residing in a DAS-owned, operated or leased facility.

Therapy animal: has the same meaning as a service animal.

EXCEPTIONS AND SPECIAL SITUATIONS FOR MINIATURE HORSES

1. Though not considered service animals, the ADA generally requires businesses to accommodate the use of
   miniature horses under when four assessment factors are met. Miniature horses generally range in height
   from 24-34 inches, measured to the shoulders, and generally weigh between 70-100 pounds. Four
   assessment factors set out in the ADA regulations must be met: (1) whether the miniature horse is
   housebroken; (2) whether the miniature horse is under the owner’s control; (3) whether the facility can
   accommodate the miniature horse’s type, size and weight; and (4) whether the miniature horse’s presence
   will not compromise legitimate safety requirements necessary for safe operation of the facility.

2. DAS considers the use of any animal that is not a service animal a special situation that requires approval.
   Tenant agencies may seek approval for special situations by submitting a Service Animal Request for
   Exception form to DAS Enterprise Asset Management (EAM) Operations and Maintenance at
   facilities.helpdesk@das.oregon.gov

3. Any modifications to the building as a result of an approved exception request will require project approval
   by submitting a Project Authorization Request form to DAS EAM Operations and Maintenance at
   facilities.helpdesk@das.oregon.gov

EXCLUSIONS, ACCESS AND OTHER SPECIFIC GUIDELINES RELATED TO ANIMALS

1. Employee requests to use a service animal in the workplace fall under the State HR Policy 50-020-10, ADA
   and Reasonable Accommodation in Employment.

2. Animals critical to fulfilling the mission of the agency such as police K9 units, fish or animals in hatcheries,
   or wild animals in a state park are exempt from this policy.

3. It may be appropriate to exclude a service animal from areas where the animal’s presence may
   compromise a specific environment. For example, a lab facility where presence of the animal may
   contaminate test results.

4. If necessary, the individual may be asked to remove the service animal from the facility for the following
   reasons:
       a. The service animal is out of control and the handler does not take effective action to control it; or
       b. The service animal is not housebroken.

5. When there is a legitimate reason to ask that a service animal be removed, staff must offer the person with
   a disability the opportunity to obtain goods or services without the service animal’s presence.

6. Damage caused by a guest, a service animal or any animal that is not a service animal is the responsibility
   of the handler.

7. Animals, whether service animals, or animals for emotional support, well-being, comfort or companionship
   (i.e., pets), may be kept in an employee’s or visitor’s personal vehicle within parking structures or parking
   lots under the following conditions:
        a. The environmental conditions within the structure or lot and the vehicle are within acceptable limits
            to ensure the well-being of the animal.
        b. The animal does not bark, cry or create an excessive disturbance in any way.
        c. If taken out of the vehicle, the animal is always kept in control by the employee or visitor. Damage
            to property or harm caused to others by the animal is the sole liability of the employee or visitor.
        d. Any waste generated by the animal is cleaned up immediately by the employee or visitor.
        e. Compliance with the above conditions is in the sole judgment of DAS EAM Operations and
            Maintenance or DAS EAM Parking Services. Failure to follow DAS directions to remove an animal
            from a parking structure or lot may result in recission of an employee’s parking assignment or
            disciplinary action.

GENERAL INFORMATION

1. Service animals are allowed in DAS-owned, operated or leased facilities where the public is normally
   allowed to go, if the animal:
       a. Is a service animal; trained and required to assist an individual with a disability.
       b. Is in training to become a service animal and is accompanied or directed by its trainer.
       c. Resides or is brought into a facility for educational, therapy or laboratory purposes.
       d. Is harnessed, leashed, or tethered except when performing work or tasks where such tethering
          would interfere with the animal’s ability to perform. In these cases, the animal must be controlled
          with voice commands, signals or other means.

2. Staff are not required to provide care, food or supervision for a service animal or a service animal trainee.

3. When it is not obvious what service the animal provides, staff may ask only the following two questions:
     a. Is the animal required because of a disability?
     b. What work or task has the animal been trained to perform?

4. Staff may not:
      a. Ask about the person’s disability.
      b. Require medical documentation, require a special identification card or training documentation for
          the service animal, or ask that the animal demonstrate its ability to perform the work or task.
      c. Charge an admission fee for a service animal or service animal trainee.

5. Animals in the workplace must be housebroken and meet the requirements of any other local laws
   imposed on animals.
