# Cyber and Information Security (DAS Statewide Policy 107-004-052)

## At a glance

**[SUMMARY]** Establishes Oregon's unified statewide cyber and information security
program under the State CISO. Agencies must meet the Statewide Information Technology
Control Standards and the Statewide Information Security Plan, run their own security
programs (asset inventory, risk assessment, incident reporting, training), and may not
offshore computing services without a State CIO exception. Effective February 17, 2026;
supersedes the 2020 version. (Source: Purpose, General Information, Offshore and Foreign
Services sections.)

## Scope & applicability

> **[VERBATIM]** "This policy applies to all state agencies as defined in ORS 276A.230,
> and includes any board, commission, department, division or office within the Oregon
> Executive Branch."

**[SUMMARY]** Excluded: Secretary of State; State Treasurer; the Attorney General (only as
to ORS 276A.303 authority over Department of Justice information systems security); Oregon
State Lottery; the State Board of Higher Education and public universities listed in ORS
352.002. (Source: Applicability section.)

## Key provisions

### Purpose

> **[VERBATIM]** "This policy establishes a unified and coherent statewide cyber and
> information security program to manage risks to state agency operations, information and
> information systems and supporting infrastructure and services, while aligning cyber and
> information security with agencies’ missions, goals and business operations."

### Roles and responsibilities

**[SUMMARY]** The State CISO manages the state's information security program, serves as
CSS Director, and is responsible for statewide rules/policies/standards, the risk-based
assessment and remediation program, enterprise incident response, security awareness
training, information sharing, and program metrics. Agency heads are ultimately
accountable for their agency's cybersecurity risk; agency programs must confirm
responsibilities with EIS, inventory and categorize assets (per Policy 107-004-010),
assess risks, implement the control standards, establish incident identification and
reporting, and train all users. All users must complete annual security training and
comply with applicable plans, policies, procedures, and standards. (Source: General
Information (1)(a)–(c).)

### Agency compliance obligation

> **[VERBATIM]** "Each agency must comply with the Statewide Information Security Plan,
> statewide policies and security standards."

**[SUMMARY]** Agencies may exceed CSS security requirements for business or legal reasons,
but must at minimum achieve the security objectives in those documents and may not
conflict with them. (Source: General Information (1)(b).)

### Enterprise security baseline (the referenced-external-standard edge)

> **[VERBATIM]** "In order to establish a common security baseline, EIS has developed the
> Statewide Information Technology Control Standards which define baseline security
> controls for all systems."

> **[VERBATIM]** "Agencies must meet the Statewide Information Technology Control
> Standards."

**[SUMMARY]** This is the policy's outward pointer to a non-policy document agencies must
uphold — see [../standards/](../standards/_index.md). CSS provides guidance and assistance
on meeting the controls. (Source: General Information (2).)

### CSS assessments

**[SUMMARY]** CSS assesses agency security posture using industry-standard metrics and
methodologies (such as those developed by the Center for Internet Security) on the
schedule posted on the CSS website; agencies must remediate findings per the assessment
recommendations. (Source: General Information (3).)

### Offshore and foreign services

> **[VERBATIM]** "Offshoring computing services, to include, but not limited to
> cloud/hosting, application development, systems/service maintenance, and support is not
> allowed."

**[SUMMARY]** Exceptions require State CIO review and approval before procuring, and are
limited to (a) no viable U.S.-based solicitation responses or contracts, or (b) a declared
agency emergency — in both cases with disclosure of risk-mitigation policies. Existing
contracts using offshore hosting or workers not authorized to work in the U.S. must be
submitted to the State CIO for approval of continued use. (Source: Offshore and Foreign
Services (4).)

### Non-compliance (special situations)

> **[VERBATIM]** "State agencies unable to comply with the standards or procedures
> supporting this policy or cyber and information security program requirements must
> document the non-compliance and compensating controls and risks assessment and transmit
> to the State Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) at eso.info@das.oregon.gov."

## Definitions

**[SUMMARY]** The policy defines: availability, confidentiality, cybersecurity,
information security incident, integrity, information security, information security
event, information system, offshoring, and users. Notably:

> **[VERBATIM]** "Information security: the protection of information and information
> systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification or
> destruction in order to provide confidentiality, integrity and availability."
