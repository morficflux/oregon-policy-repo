# Statewide Information Technology (IT) Control Standards (January 2024)

## At a glance

**[SUMMARY]** The baseline security control catalog every Oregon executive-branch agency
must meet. DAS Policy 107-004-052 requires agencies to meet these standards; they are
issued under ORS 276A.300 and align with the moderate control families of NIST SP 800-53
Rev. 5. 18 control families, ~180 base controls (321 entries with enhancements) —
inventoried per family under
[eis-css-it-control-standards/](eis-css-it-control-standards/_index.md).

## Authority

> **[VERBATIM]** "Oregon Enterprise Information Services (EIS) has the responsibility for
> developing and overseeing the implementation of statewide information and cyber security
> standards, and policies relating to information and cyber security, under the authority
> of Oregon Revised Statute 276A.300."

Approved 01/29/2024 by Ben Gherezgiher, State of Oregon Chief Information Security
Officer (per the source approval block).

## Scope & applicability

> **[VERBATIM]** "These Standards apply to systems, policies, and procedures within all
> Executive Branch agencies. Agencies are responsible for complying with these Standards
> and ensuring that third parties acting on behalf of agencies have formal agreements that
> guarantee compliance with these Standards."

## Key provisions

### Purpose and alignment

> **[VERBATIM]** "These Standards provide a catalog of IT controls for State of Oregon
> information systems to protect state operations, assets, and individuals from a diverse
> set of threats including malicious acts, natural disasters, structural failures,
> information system errors and human errors."

**[SUMMARY]** The standards establish a minimum control set by information asset
classification level; agencies may exceed them but the controls are interdependent and
intended to be implemented in their entirety. Reference sources: NIST, StateRAMP
baselines, and federal/state/local statutes and rules; terminology follows the NIST
glossary. Deviations that cannot be implemented must be documented. (Source:
introduction, pages 1–2.)

### Control families

**[SUMMARY]** AC, AT, AU, CA, CM, CP, IA, IR, MA, MP, PE, PL, PS, RA, SA, SC, SI, SR —
see the [per-family inventories](eis-css-it-control-standards/_index.md).
