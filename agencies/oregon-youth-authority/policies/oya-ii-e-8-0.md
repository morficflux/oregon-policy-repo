I.     PURPOSE:

       This policy establishes guidelines for staff to follow when ensuring youth follow
       prescribed dress standards in OYA facilities.

II.    POLICY DEFINITIONS: None.

III.   POLICY:

       OYA values equity, diversity, inclusion and the importance of individual
       expression. OYA strives to balance facility safety and security with allowing youth
       to have clothing options that are culturally sensitive and age appropriate for the
       settings in which they live, work, attend school and recreate.

       OYA will provide and maintain basic clothing for all youth in OYA facilities to
       allow them to present a neat and acceptable appearance at all times. The
       clothing style and color will not distinguish youth as incarcerated people but will
       reflect appropriate community dress. Staff may permit youth in transition
       facilities/camps and those achieving certain behavioral program levels to wear
       personal clothing within the standards of this policy.

       This policy is aligned with OYA’s Living Units Fundamental Practices 1 and 3,
       Clean, Safe, and Organized Living Units and Developmentally Appropriate Milieu
       and Services. Specifically, the key principles that environments matter and
       seeing people as resources.

       Staff must ensure youth do not dress in clothing that resembles the facility staff
       uniform.

IV.    GENERAL STANDARDS:

       Facility staff must ensure youth clothing meet these standards.

       A.     Clothing must be clean and neat.

       B.     Clothing must fit properly without being excessively small or large.

       C.     No portion of a youth’s undergarments (underwear or bra) may be visible.

       D.     Pants must be worn fully fastened and zipped.

       E.     Multiple sets of clothing may not be worn simultaneously.

       F.     Shoes or shower sandals must be worn at all times except during water-
              related recreational activities, or in bed.

       G.     Boots may only be worn for work, and are not to be worn on living units.

       H.     Footwear must be worn appropriately on the feet.

       I.     G-string or thong-type underwear are not permitted.

       J.     Strapless, low-cut, or underwire bras are not permitted.

       K.     Undergarments must be worn at all times during the day.

       L.     Socks are required when wearing shoes or boots.

       M.     Shirts must be worn at all times during the day. Unit leadership teams may
              make exceptions for summer outdoor recreation.

       N.     Issued nighttime attire must be worn in bed.

       O.     Sunglasses may only be worn outdoors.

V.     CLOTHING ISSUE AND REPLACEMENT:

       A.     Staff must ensure each youth has a set of clothing and shoes in good
              repair as follows:

              1.     Pants or shorts;

              2.     T-shirts;

              3.     Long sweatpants;

              4.     Long-sleeved sweatshirt;

              5.     Sweat shorts;

              6.     Individually-issued undergarments (underwear, and bras when
                     needed);

              7.     Individually-issued socks;

              8.     Nighttime attire;

              9.     Polo shirts;

              10.    Black slacks;

              11.    Shower sandals; and

              12.    Sneakers.

       B.     Staff must issue youth seasonal garments as needed by the facility
              climate, as follows:

              1.     Hat (ball cap or stocking hat);

              2.     Set of thermals; and

              3.     Jacket.

       C.     Staff must issue youth in certain programs protective or weather resistant
              clothing as required by the programs, such as aprons for the kitchen,
              raingear for landscaping, coveralls for maintenance, and boots for off-site
              work crews.

       D.     Staff must provide youth an opportunity to have exchanges of clean
              clothing at least three times per week and daily exchanges of
              undergarments and socks. This may be provided in several ways,
              including access to self-serve washer facilities, central clothing, or a
              combination of the two.

       E.     Staff must replace clothing when worn or damaged on a one-for-one
              basis. Staff must hold youth accountable for replacing intentionally-
              damaged issued clothing.

       F.     Staff must also allow youth clothing needs as outlined in OYA policy
              I-A-10.1 Meeting LGBTQ+ Youth Needs.

       G.     Staff must confiscate clothing items that have been misused, altered, or
              used in a harmful manner. See OYA policy II-A-2.0 Searches of Youth and
              Youth Property in OYA Facilities regarding confiscating items.

VI.     DRESS REQUIREMENTS:

       A.     School and Special Event Attire

              Staff must ensure youth school and special event attire is black slacks and
              polo shirts. Special event attire must be worn as directed by facility
              administrators to such events as graduations, court attendance, and
              religious services.

       B.     Leisure Attire

              Staff must ensure youth leisure attire is jeans and T-shirts. Staff may allow
              youth to wear sweats within the living unit and during recreational
              activities.

       C.     Nighttime Attire

              Staff must ensure youth wear shirts, and short or long pajama or
              sweatpants to bed.

       D.     Work Assignment Attire

              Staff may issue youth work-related gear, aprons, coveralls, or safety boots
              according to the requirements of the assigned jobs.

       E.     Intake Attire

              Staff may temporarily issue youth sweats or scrubs during intake and prior
              to being issued regular clothing from an intake facility.

       F.     Isolation Attire

              Staff may issue youth in isolation scrubs or a safety smock according to
              safety and security concerns. (See OYA policy II-B-1.2 Use of Time-out,
              Room-lock Other, Isolation, and Safety Programs in OYA Facilities.)

       G.     Facility Transfer Transport Attire

              Staff must ensure all escorted youth transferring between OYA facilities
              are dressed in coveralls, underwear, bra (when needed), socks, and
              sandals.

       H.     Exceptions

              Exceptions to youth dress requirements may be made due to medical
              issues, safety and security concerns, or special circumstances, at facility
              management’s discretion.

VII.   PERSONAL CLOTHING:

       A.     Staff may allow youth residing in transition facilities/camps and youth on
              certain higher levels of behavioral programs to wear personal clothing.

        B.    Staff must ensure youth personal clothing is worn according to the general
              dress standards of this policy and does not resemble the facility staff
              uniform.

        C.    Staff must ensure youth personal clothing is modest in style. See-through
              garments, short-shorts, and halter or tank tops are not permitted.

        D.    Staff must ensure youth personal clothing and hats do not contain any
              hate symbols; references to alcohol, substances, gang
              signs/symbols/colors, or any kind of sexual, offensive, or illegal activities.

        E.    Facility administration may impose further limitations as appropriate or
              needed for the facility’s safety and order.

        F.    Facility administration may allow youth personal shoes to be worn at all
              facilities. Limitations on color and style may be imposed.

        G.    Youth personal clothing is limited to five changes of clothing or less, at the
              facility administration’s discretion and physical plant storage availability.

        H.    Staff must ensure youth personal clothing is cleaned and, when
              necessary, disinfected before storage or allowing the youth to keep and
              wear personal clothing.

VIII.   LOCAL OPERATING PROTOCOL REQUIRED: NO
