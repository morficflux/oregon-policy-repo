I.      PURPOSE:

        This policy provides guidelines for OYA staff when notifying parents/guardians of
        youth incidents.

II.     POLICY DEFINITIONS: None

III.    POLICY:

        OYA recognizes the value in maintaining contact with parents/guardians of youth
        in its custody. When significant incidents involving youth occur, staff will provide
        timely notice to the youth’s parents/guardians.

       OYA has identified diversity, equity, and inclusion as an agency priority and
       initiative, with a goal to build a respectful, diverse, equitable and inclusive
       environment for youth, stakeholders, and staff that is free from harassment,
       discrimination, and bias. Staff must be aware of the parent’s/guardian’s cultural
       sensitivities when notifying them of significant incidents. Notifications must be
       made in the parent’s/guardian’s native language whenever possible.

IV.    GENERAL STANDARDS:

       A.     Incidents/Activities that Require Parent/Guardian Notification

              1.    Transfer between OYA facilities

                    a)     The sending facility staff must notify the parent/guardian of a
                           pending placement change prior to the youth’s transfer,
                           unless there is evidence that notification will disrupt the
                           safety of the transfer process.

                    b)     The receiving facility staff must notify the parent/guardian of
                           the youth’s transfer once the youth has arrived at the final
                           destination.

              2.    Transfer between community placements

                    The youth’s juvenile parole/probation officer (JPPO) must notify the
                    parent/guardian of the pending placement change prior to the
                    youth’s transfer, and confirm with the parent/guardian once the
                    youth has arrived at the final destination.

              3.    Prescription of a psychotropic medication

                    Notification is to parents/guardians of youth less than 14 years old.
                    Youth ages 14 and older must provide consent to this notification.
                    Resources about psychotropic medication may be provided to
                    parents/guardians upon request.

                    a)     For facility youth, this information must be relayed during the
                           youth’s MDT as described in OYA policy II-D-1.4 Medication
                           Management in OYA Facilities.

                    b)     For youth in substitute care placement, the notification must
                           occur as described in OYA policy III-D-3.0 Medication
                           Management in Substitute Care.

              4.    Preliminary and Formal Parole Revocation Hearings

                    a)     The JPPO or other OYA staff member must ensure notice of
                           a preliminary parole revocation hearing is given to the
                           youth’s parent/guardian (if applicable) prior to the youth’s
                           preliminary parole revocation hearing in compliance with
                           OAR 416-300-0050.

                    b)     The OYA staff member serving as the formal parole
                           revocation hearing committee chairperson must provide
                           notice to the youth's parent/guardian (if applicable) of the
                           formal parole revocation hearing. The notice must be given
                           within a reasonable time before the formal parole revocation
                           hearing is scheduled to occur in compliance with OAR 416-
                           300-0060.

              5.    Any incident involving a youth that requires emergency community
                    response.

              6.    Urgent or emergent medical care.

              7.    Unauthorized absence or escape from a close- custody facility.

              8.    Unauthorized absence from a community placement.

              9.    Suicide attempt.

              10.   Any form of abuse.

              11.   Youth life-threatening injury.

              12.   Youth death.

              13.   Other significant changes to a youth’s status.

       B.     General Notification Process (for events other than youth transfers,
              prescription of psychotropic medication, and unauthorized absence from a
              community placement)

              1.    OYA staff notification process

                    Staff must notify their supervisor or OD and the youth’s JPPO
                    within the first hour after the staff member becomes aware of the
                    incident. Notification must be oral (in-person or by phone).

              2.    A living unit manager, JPPO, or designee must contact the
                    parent/guardian no later than 24 hours of the incident contingent
                    on:

                    a)     Severity of the injury to the youth (if injury exists);

                    b)     Imminent risk to the safety and security of the youth or
                           facility; and

                    c)     Need for parental/guardian information or consent.

              3.    If the living unit manager or JPPO is not available, staff must
                    initially report the incident to the facility or Community Services
                    officer-of- the-day (OD).

                    The OD or designee will contact the parent/guardian and relay the
                    incident information.

       C.     Youth Death or Life-threatening Injury

              1.    Parent/guardian notification

                    a)     Parent/guardian notification of a youth death or life-
                           threatening injury must be as soon as possible to ensure the
                           family is notified by an OYA representative rather than the
                           media or a third party.

                    b)     For life-threatening injuries, the contact may be by
                           telephone. If a youth has died, the contact must be face-to-
                           face by a supervisor/manager and accompanying staff.

                           Phone contact is only allowed when face-to-face contact is
                           not possible or cannot be done in a timely manner.

                    c)     Great care, tact, and sympathy must be extended to the
                           family. For death notifications, details of the death must only
                           be relayed to the family by the investigating authority.

              2.    Monitoring and support after youth death

                    a)     As the primary case manager, the JPPO is responsible for
                           monitoring events and interacting with the family after the
                           immediate crisis is over and the family is still grieving.

                    b)     The circumstance of each death is unique, and care must be
                           given to ensure the wishes of the family are considered. The
                           JPPO may consult the family to determine how best to
                           assist them during this difficult time.

                    c)     OYA may pay the cost of funeral, burial or cremation
                           expenses for a youth who dies while in OYA legal custody.

                           Refer to OAR 416-050 (Funeral and Burial Expenses) for
                           guidance.

       D.     Documentation

              Staff must document all parent/guardian contacts and notifications in the
              youth’s JJIS notebook.

V.     LOCAL OPERATING PROTOCOL REQUIRED: NO
