I.     PURPOSE:

       This policy describes how OYA establishes and maintains Trust Accounts for youth
       in its custody.

II.    POLICY DEFINITIONS:

       Juvenile Justice Information System (JJIS): The Juvenile Justice Information
       System (JJIS) is a statewide-integrated electronic information system designed,
       developed, and implemented to support a continuum of services and shared
       responsibility among all members of the juvenile justice community. In a
       collaborative partnership between the Oregon Youth Authority (OYA) and Oregon's
       county juvenile departments, JJIS is administered by the State of Oregon through
       OYA.

       Maintenance Account: A sub-section of a youth’s Trust Account used to account
       for funds that may be used to pay for the youth's cost of care.

       Special Account: A sub-section of a youth’s Trust Account used to account for
       funds received for a youth for purposes other than paying for the youth's cost of
       care.

       Trust Account: A youth-specific trust account established and held for an OYA
       youth for safekeeping, management and maintenance in accordance with OYA’s
       mission, federal and state laws, and OYA rules and policy.

       Youth: A person placed in OYA legal or physical custody, who is supervised by OYA
       staff either in a close-custody facility or on parole or probation status in the
       community. Such persons include youth placed in the legal custody of the Oregon
       Department of Corrections (DOC) or other agencies, and placed in OYA physical
       custody.

III.   POLICY:

       OYA has established a Trust Account for youth when they are placed in OYA
       custody. This Trust Account includes both a Maintenance and Special Account in
       which all revenue received is recorded and disbursed according to federal and state
       laws, and Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR chapter 416 division 260).

       OYA has identified diversity, equity, and inclusion as an agency priority and
       initiative, with a goal to build a respectful, diverse, equitable and inclusive
       environment for youth and staff that is free from harassment, discrimination, and
       bias. Staff must ensure youth trust fund accounts are managed in an unbiased,
       equitable manner in accordance with this policy.

IV.    GENERAL STANDARDS:

       A.      Maintenance Accounts

               1.    Central Accounting staff must deposit all money received that may be
                     used to pay for a youth’s cost of care into the youth’s Maintenance
                     Account. All sources are considered available for the youth’s care
                     unless otherwise specifically designated. Sources of money include,
                     but are not limited to:

                     a)     Federal benefits such as Social Security (SS), Supplemental
                            Security Income (SSI), veteran’s or railroad benefits;

                     b)     Court-ordered support;

                     c)     Voluntary payments sent for youth support; including money a
                            youth possesses when taken into custody or sent to the youth
                            after placement in OYA custody; and

                     d)     Interest earned on the Maintenance Account.

               2.    Maintenance Accounts include subaccounts to segregate money and
                     interest earned by source, as follows:

                     a)     Social Security (SS);

                     b)     Supplemental Security Income (SSI);

                    c)    Supplemental Security Income Dedicated (SSI Dedicated);

                    d)    Court-ordered support (Child Support); and

                    e)    Other sources.
                          Staff will consider all sources not otherwise specifically
                          designated available for the youth’s care.

               3.   Money in Maintenance Accounts is available to pay for a youth’s
                    current needs, as follows:

                    a)    Trust Fund Applications

                          Central Accounting staff electronically compare the amount
                          OYA paid for the youth’s cost of care to the balance in the
                          Maintenance Account. Central Accounting staff must -

                          (1)    Apply the entire Maintenance Account balance to the
                                 cost of care if the cost of care meets or exceeds the
                                 balance; or

                          (2)    Deduct the cost of care from the account leaving the
                                 balance for future use if the cost of care is less than the
                                 Maintenance Account balance.

                    b)    Staff must leave excess benefits and support in the
                          Maintenance Account to meet the youth’s care and needs and
                          may not transfer balances to the youth’s Special Account.

                    c)    Manual Maintenance Account Payments

                          Staff may allow youth in OYA custody in a Developmental
                          Disability (DD) or an unpaid relative placement or returned
                          home to have monthly needs met through their Maintenance
                          Accounts until representative payees are changed.

                          (1)    The disability analyst (DA) or the youth’s juvenile
                                 parole/probation officer (JPPO) must determine if a
                                 financial need exists, document the need in the youth’s
                                 case file, and complete a Trust Disbursement Request
                                 (YA 2218) for each month money is forwarded to the
                                 unpaid placement.

                          (2)    JPPOs must notify a DA of the youth’s address change.
                                 The DA will notify the payor organization. The funds are
                                 forwarded, as follows:

                                 (i)     Social Security benefits will not exceed the
                                         monthly benefit amount;

                                 (ii)    SSI benefits are returned to the Social Security
                                         Administration (SSA);

                                    (iii)   Voluntary support is limited only by financial
                                            need;

                                    (iv)    Court-ordered support may not be used for this
                                            purpose.

                      d)     Retroactive Lump Sum Payments of SS and SSI Benefits
                             Staff may apply retroactive lump sum benefits as follows:

                             (1)    Federal benefits (SS and SSI) may be used for current
                                    and foreseeable future needs. Application of benefits
                                    against prior expenditures may be requested of the
                                    federal agency; and

                             (2)    Retroactive court-ordered support benefits may be
                                    applied back to the court order date or date of paid
                                    placement, whichever is later.

       B.      Special Accounts

               Staff must deposit money received for purposes other than paying for a
               youth’s cost of care into a youth’s Special Account to be used to pay toward
               the youth’s court-ordered financial obligation(s), and obligations incurred by
               the youth.

               Sources of money may include:

               1.     A youth’s earnings, stipend, or incentive pay;

               2.     Restricted money gifts;

               3.     Restricted inheritances;

               4.     Tribal monies;

               5.     Other sources of money including SAIF (State Accidental Insurance
                      Fund) payments and legal settlements;

               6.     Money brought with the youth when entering OYA custody;

               7.     Interest earned on the account based upon earnings made by the
                      State Treasurer’s Office on investment of all trust money held; and

               8.     College grant monies.

       C.      Federal Benefits

               1.     Disability analysts (DA) are responsible for applying for federal
                      benefits on behalf of youth in substitute care placement. A DA must:

                      a)     Contact the federal agency to determine a youth’s entitlement
                             for benefits. If the youth is entitled, The DA must submit an
                             application for OYA to become the representative payee; and

                    b)     Advise federal agencies to use the OYA Central Office address
                           for receipt of correspondence and benefits.

               2.   Monitoring for SSI and Title XIX (Grants to States for Medical
                    Assistance Programs) Eligibility

                    Youth who are eligible for SSI are also eligible for Title XIX benefits.

                    a)     Because youth are ineligible for SSI benefits when housed in
                           facilities, the DA must request the SSA suspend benefits to
                           these youth.

                           The DA may run a “Youth in Close Custody Receiving SSI”
                           JJIS report to identify youth drawing SSI who are housed in
                           facilities.

                    b)     The trust accountant must monitor youth accounts to ensure
                           those receiving SSI benefits have Maintenance Account
                           balances below $2,000.

                           A youth becomes ineligible for SSI benefits when the youth’s
                           Maintenance Account reaches an asset limitation of $2,000 on
                           the first day of a calendar month.

                           (1)     Exceptions to the limit are:

                                   (i)     Lump sum benefits received from SSA for back
                                           SSI benefits. OYA is allowed six months to
                                           reduce the Trust Account balance below the
                                           asset limitation.

                                   (ii)    Funds in a separate irrevocable trust account.

                                   (iii)   Court-ordered support to satisfy state debt
                                           incurred when a youth is in an OYA-paid
                                           placement is not considered income for SSI
                                           benefit determinations.

                           (2)     The trust accountant will monitor Maintenance Accounts
                                   by reviewing a monthly report of balances over $700.
                                   The trust accountant must review accounts for the
                                   following:

                                   (i)     Expenditures made by OYA for which
                                           reimbursement was not made;

                                   (ii)    Foreseeable future expenditures against which
                                           benefits may be applied; and

                                   (iii)   Whether the youth is no longer in OYA custody. If
                                           a youth is no longer in OYA custody, a final
                                           accounting will be made and money disbursed to
                                           the source or the youth.

                             (3)    When a youth’s Maintenance Account reaches $2,000
                                    and the youth receives SSI, the DA will contact the SSA
                                    and ask that benefits be suspended until the Trust
                                    Account balance is reduced.

                             (4)    SSI money received after reaching the asset limitation
                                    must be returned to the SSA until the balance is
                                    reduced below the $2,000 limitation. Once the balance
                                    is below the limit;

                                    (i)     The DA will notify the SSA to reinstate benefits.

                                    (ii)    All correspondence relating to overpayments
                                            must be forwarded to the trust accountant, who
                                            will resolve payor organization overpayment
                                            claims.

       D.      Receipt and Deposit of Trust Money

               Staff must forward all deposit receipts to OYA’s Accounts Receivable (A/R)
               clerk (except when facilities make their own deposits) to be deposited into the
               youth’s Trust Account.

               Facilities making their own deposits must send deposit details including a
               signed copy of the YA 2202 (Deposit Register Form) to the A/R clerk. The
               A/R clerk will credit those deposits to appropriate accounts.

               1.     Federal benefits: Upon receipt of a federal benefit check, the DA will
                      work with the youth to determine the appropriate account for the funds.

               2.     Court-ordered support: Information on the amount of court-ordered
                      support is transmitted daily from the Department of Justice, Division of
                      Child Support, to OYA. Accounting staff must verify the amounts and
                      release them into each corresponding youth’s Trust Account.

               3.     Special Account

                      a)     Staff may only deposit money received from a person listed in a
                             youth’s JJIS “Persons” tab as an approved visitor or phone
                             status into the youth’s Special Account.

                      b)     Money received from a person not listed in the youth’s JJIS
                             “Persons” tab as an approved visitor or phone status may not
                             be deposited and must be returned to that person.

                      c)     Staff must complete a Rejection of Youth Correspondence form
                             (YA 4205), mark “Other” for rejection reason with a description
                             (e.g., Returning $25 check per OYA policy I-B-3.0). Staff must
                             distribute the form according its instructions.

                      d)     Any exception must be authorized by the youth’s JPPO or living
                             unit manager.

                     e)      College grant money must be deposited and tracked
                             separately.

               4.    Interest: Interest is calculated and posted by JJIS to each Trust
                     Account, by source, based upon the daily balance in each account.

       E.      Withdrawals

               1.    OYA manages withdrawals closely to ensure proper authorizations
                     and approvals are obtained. Staff may only disburse funds from Trust
                     Accounts to a person listed in a youth’s JJIS “persons” tab as an
                     approved visitor.

               2.    Disbursement to OYA staff, other youth, or other youth’ family
                     members must be authorized by the superintendent, camp director, or
                     field supervisor.

               3.    Any disbursement written for more than $25.00 must be approved by
                     the youth’s JPPO or MDT prior to the purchase. The approval may be
                     verbal or in writing.

               4.    Staff must document each withdrawal from a Trust Account on a
                     Trust Disbursement form (YA 2218).

                     a)      Staff must store supplies of blank YA 2218s in locked storage.

                     b)      Facility staff must enter the YA 2218s into the youth’s Trust
                             Account.

                     c)      Facility staff must send original white copies of completed and
                             voided YA 2218s to the trust accountant.

                     d)      Staff must keep remaining copies of completed and voided YA
                             2218s on file numerically.

               5.    Authorization of the YA 2218

                     Authorization of the YA 2218 is required by obtaining the following
                     signatures:

                     a)      Youth placed in the community: The youth, the JPPO, and the
                             field supervisor;

                     b)      Youth placed in facilities: The youth and management staff (i.e.
                             unit supervisor, program director or designee); or

                     c)      Youth placed or being placed soon in a Developmental
                             Disability placement: The DA; or

                     d)      OYA trust accountant and accounting manager.

               6.    Approval of the YA 2218

                     Approval of the YA 2218 is documented with the following signatures:

                     a)     Youth placed in the community: Field supervisor;

                     b)     Youth placed in facilities: Management staff (e.g., unit
                            supervisor, program director, superintendent, camp director, or
                            designee); and

                     c)     The accounting manager or an assistant director.

               7.    The YA 2218 may not be authorized and approved by the same
                     individual.

               8.    The YA 2218 must include additional approval for use of college grant
                     money.

               9.    There must be sufficient funds in the youth Trust Account to reimburse
                     a revolving fund when funds are disbursed through a revolving fund
                     account.

                     The youth’s JPPO, field supervisor, facility management staff or other
                     designee must verify sufficient funds are available in the Special
                     Account prior to authorizing payment.

               10.   If sufficient funds are not available and the Trust Account is
                     overdrawn, staff must suspend the Trust Account until enough money
                     is in the Trust Account to cover the overdraft.

                     If the Trust Account remains overdrawn at the time the youth leaves
                     the facility or OYA custody, staff must set up an accounts receivable
                     and bill the youth for the outstanding balance.

               11.   If payment is made under the wrong youth’s JJIS number and a
                     payment is posted to the wrong Trust Account, staff must make a
                     reverse application.

                     a)     Staff must send a written document, hard copy or e-mail, to the
                            trust accountant stating the circumstances of the
                            misapplication, month(s) involved, amount(s), incorrect JJIS
                            number, correct JJIS number, name of the payee, payee
                            number and youth's name.

                     b)     The trust accountant will make corrections upon receiving the
                            staff’s documentation.

               12.   Staff may only transfer money between the Maintenance and Special
                     Accounts when money was erroneously credited to the wrong account.
                     Staff must document the transfer.

       F.      Financial Accounting

               1.    Staff must complete financial accounting as required for legal
                     proceedings, audits, etc. In addition, staff will complete financial
                     accountings when a Maintenance Account balance exists and OYA
                     custody is terminated.

               2.    Staff must complete financial accountings based on a “Trust Account
                     Balance Detail” JJIS report.

                     The report shows receipt and disbursement details. Staff must
                     compare receipts and disbursements on the report with JJIS screens.
                     Staff must investigate deviations and adjustments made to bring JJIS
                     Screens and the Trust Account Balance Detail into agreement.

       G.      Clearing a Trust Account

               When a youth’s OYA custody is terminated, the youth’s available Trust
               Account funds will be disbursed and accounts cleared using the following
               guidelines.

               1.    Maintenance Account

                     Central Accounting staff must analyze balances based on a closing
                     financial accounting to determine offset of cost of care.

                     a)     The trust accountant must apply trust funds in the following
                            order when part or all of the Maintenance Account balance is
                            used to offset the cost of care:

                            (1)    Court-ordered support;

                            (2)    Social Security;

                            (3)    Voluntary support;

                            (4)    Veterans;

                            (5)    SAIF (State Accident Insurance Fund);

                            (6)    Civil Service Annuities;

                            (7)    Military Allotments;

                            (8)    Railroad;

                            (9)    Other;

                            (10)   SSI.

                     b)     If part or all of the balance in the Maintenance Account is not
                            used to offset the cost of care, the source of the income will be
                            determined and managed as follows:

                            (1)    SSA and SSI

                                   Conserved funds must be returned to the payor
                                   organization when OYA custody terminates. OYA may
                                   not disburse excess SSI or SS benefits to the youth or
                                   guardian without written authorization from the SSA.

                                  (i)     If the youth or guardian will endure extreme
                                          financial hardship, the JPPO must contact the
                                          SSA by telephone or letter stating the
                                          circumstances, claim number, youth’s name, and
                                          amount of excess benefits.

                                  (ii)    If written authorization to release benefits to the
                                          youth or guardian is received from the SSA, the
                                          JPPO will initiate a YA 2218 payable to the
                                          appropriate person. The written authorization and
                                          the YA 2218 must be sent to the trust
                                          accountant.

                           (2)    Child Support

                                  The support amount must be applied to the youth’s cost
                                  of care if the balance includes payments received after
                                  the youth left OYA custody and the payments are for
                                  arrearages during a period when the youth was in care.

                                  If OYA is not entitled to the child support, the trust
                                  accountant will notify the Department of Justice Division
                                  of Child Support (DCS). If DCS cannot recover the
                                  overpayment, the money will be forwarded to the youth.

                           (3)    Other Support

                                  Money received as voluntary support intended for
                                  maintenance of the youth in an out-of-home care
                                  placement will be disbursed to the parent/guardian or
                                  the youth when OYA custody is terminated.

                2.   Special Accounts

                     a)    When a youth’s legal custody is terminated, the facility or
                           youth’s JPPO must disburse the youth’s Special Accounts
                           balance to the youth or the youth’s designee.

                           College grant money will be disbursed as appropriate.

                     b)    DOC youth leaving OYA physical custody

                           (1)    When a Department of Corrections (DOC) youth leaves
                                  OYA physical custody, facility management staff must
                                  direct the disbursement of the DOC youth’s Special
                                  Account balance.

                           (2)    When a DOC youth leaves OYA physical custody to
                                  DOC physical custody and has college grant money, the
                                  college grant money must be sent to DOC payable to
                                  the youth and be labeled as “College Grant money.”

                     c)    Staff must ask the youth to sign the YA 2218.

                d)   Staff will complete the YA 2218 by –

                     (1)    checking the box for “Close Trust Account;”

                     (2)    entering a current address in the mailing address field;
                            and

                     (3)    assuring the form is countersigned by facility
                            management staff (if leaving an OYA facility).

                e)   Staff may release funds to the extent a balance is retained
                     adequate to cover potential outstanding trust disbursement
                     requests.

                f)   Staff may not release funds earlier than six weeks after a youth
                     leaves an OYA facility.

                     If a youth has need of their trust funds prior to the six-week
                     waiting period, staff may release a portion of the funds leaving
                     a balance to cover potential outstanding trust disbursement
                     requests. Balances below $5 will not be disbursed.

                g)   The trust accountant must disburse Special Account balances
                     which are unclaimed and abandoned two years after a youth’s
                     OYA custody is terminated, in accordance with the Uniform
                     Disposition of Unclaimed Property Act.

                     (1)    The trust accountant must return Special Account
                            balances of at least $5 to the youth.

                     (2)    A diligent attempt must be made to locate the youth.

                            (i)     At a minimum, the trust accountant must send a
                                    letter to the last known address stating there is a
                                    Trust Account balance, giving 30 days from the
                                    date of the notice to claim the account balance.

                            (ii)    The trust accountant will forward the Special
                                    Accounts balance to the Division of State Lands
                                    if the notice is returned with no forwarding
                                    address or 30 days passes without a claim.
