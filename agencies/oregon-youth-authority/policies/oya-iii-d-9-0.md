I.     PURPOSE:

       The purpose of this policy is to provide guidance to OYA staff in assisting youth
       who are on parole or probation obtain acceptable identification or a driver
       license/permit when authorized.

II.    POLICY DEFINITIONS:

       Substitute care: Residential placements in the community that provide 24-hour-
       a-day care or treatment, excluding a relative’s home. Such placements include,
       but are not limited to, foster care (including relative foster care) proctor care, or
       contracted residential treatment programs.

III.   POLICY:

       OYA has identified diversity, equity and inclusion as an agency priority and
       initiative with a goal to build a respectful, diverse, equitable, and inclusive
       environment for youth and staff that is free from harassment, discrimination, and
       bias. Data shows youth of color and LGBTQ+ youth are disproportionately
       represented in the juvenile justice system. While OYA is only one part of that
       system, we play a critical role in addressing the historical and systemic inequities
       it perpetuates. All youth must have equal and equitable access to privileges and
       programs, such as the opportunity to get a driver license/permit.

       Youth in OYA custody may need to obtain acceptable identification or a driver
       license/permit in order to meet case plan goals that promote change, develop or
       improve skills, and support transition. This includes obtaining an updated
       identification when youth legally change their name or gender.

       A youth may legally obtain a driver learning permit in Oregon at age 15 and a
       driver license at age 16. OYA recognizes situations may require a youth have a
       valid driver license/permit and driving privileges.

       A youth may obtain a driver license/permit and driving privileges only with
       specific authorization to do so.

IV.    GENERAL STANDARDS:

       A.     Youth driver license/permit

              1.     A youth's juvenile parole/probation officer (JPPO) must ensure the
                     youth discusses plans to obtain a license/permit with the JPPO and
                     the youth's parents, legal guardian, or caretaker.

                     When a youth is under age 18, the JPPO must obtain written
                     permission (using a YA 3030 Driver Exemption Authorization form)
                     from the youth’s parents/guardian for the youth to obtain a driver
                     license/permit.

              2.     Completion of a Driver Exemption Authorization (YA 3030) is
                     required before authorization for a driver license/permit is granted.
                     A copy of the completed YA 3030 must be retained in the youth’s
                     case file.
              3.     The JPPO must ensure the youth meets all Driver and Motor
                     Vehicle Services Division (DMV) legal criteria and requirements
                     before obtaining a license/permit.
       B.     Youth driving privileges

              1.     OYA may grant a youth driving privileges to drive to work,
                     treatment, or other necessary purposes as outlined in the youth's
                     case plan and documented on the Parole and Probation
                     Agreement.

                     a)       JPPOs must consult with the field supervisor and any
                              involved treatment team, parent/guardian or other supportive

                              adults, when determining whether to request a youth be
                              granted driving privileges by OYA.

                     b)       A youth must have permission from the youth’s JPPO, the
                              field supervisor, chief of parole/probation operations or
                              Community Services assistant director, and the youth’s
                              parent/guardian (if a minor) prior to obtaining driving
                              privileges.

              2.     Driving privileges are generally not granted to youth while in
                     substitute care.
                     An exception may be granted if approved by the Community
                     Services assistant director or designee. The Community Resources
                     or Foster Care manager must be notified of the exception.
                     a)       Youth are prohibited from driving a vehicle owned by a
                              substitute care provider or OYA staff.
                     b)       OYA must receive written consent from the substitute care
                              provider that they agree with the youth having driving
                              privileges while in their care.
                     c)       The YA 3030 may also provide authority for the substitute
                              care provider to deny immediate driving privileges for up to
                              two business days at any time. Any denial of driving
                              privileges longer than two business days must be reviewed
                              and approved by the youth’s JPPO.
              3.     Specific driving requirements and restrictions must be documented
                     on a Driver Exemption Authorization (YA 3030). A copy of a
                     completed YA 3030 must be retained in the youth’s case file and
                     imported into JJIS.

              4.     Staff must ensure the youth meets all DMV requirements prior to
                     and while operating a motor vehicle.

                     JPPOs must ensure youth who obtained their driver
                     licenses/permits prior to OYA custody are granted driving privileges
                     by OYA before they resume driving.

              5.     The youth's JPPO must ensure the youth has written proof of
                     automobile insurance before operating a motor vehicle.

                     A copy of current proof of automobile insurance must be retained in
                     the youth’s case file.

              6.     If OYA grants a youth driving privileges, the youth's JPPO must
                     ensure driving privileges are specified in the youth’s Juvenile
                     Parole/Probation Agreement.
              7.     Staff must ensure youth are not granted permission to drive a
                     foster parent, OYA staff, or state-owned vehicle.

              8.     Staff may seek an exception for youth in foster care to obtain a
                     driver license by completing a YA 5012.
              9.     Driving privileges may be revoked at any time. Reasons may
                     include, but are not limited to, the youth not following the conditions
                     of the driving agreement in the YA 3030, a change in placement, or
                     substance use.

V.     OYA GENERAL PROCEDURE REQUIRED: YES

       The OYA general procedure addresses the following:

       A.     Guidelines to process a youth's request for driver license or permit;

       B.     Guidelines to rescind or deny a youth's driving privileges; and

       C.     Documentation of decisions about driving privileges.

VI.    LOCAL OPERATING PROTOCOL REQUIRED: NO
