# Controlling Portable and Removable Storage Devices (DAS Statewide Policy 107-004-051)

## At a glance

Requires each agency to physically control and protect portable and
removable storage devices (palmtops, laptops, mobile phones, flash drives, diskettes,
CDs/DVDs) and the sensitive information stored on them. Effective 07/30/07; established
under 2005 Oregon Laws Chapter 739 and OAR 125-800-0005/-0010/-0020 (renumbered to
128-030 effective 5/1/2026).

## Key provisions

(from visual reading of the scanned source)

- Each agency adopts policy and procedures identifying approved device types, governing
  personally-owned devices, and tracking devices.
- Each agency identifies what information may be stored on portable/removable devices and
  approved securing methods appropriate to sensitivity; sensitive information requires
  physical and technical safeguards (encryption, tamper-evident packaging, locked
  storage).
- Disposal: removable media contents permanently destroyed or rendered unrecoverable per
  retention requirements; storage devices with sensitive information physically destroyed
  or securely overwritten (see [DAS 107-011-050_PR](../procedures/das-107-011-050_pr.md) for e-waste
  disposal — renumbered from Policy 107-009-0050, the number this document originally
  cited).
- Agencies may exceed but must at minimum achieve the policy's security objectives;
  agencies had one year from the effective date to comply.
- Applicability mirrors the 125-800-0020 pattern: Executive Branch agencies per ORS
  174.112, with exceptions per ORS 182.122/182.124 and OAR 125-800-0020(3)–(4).
