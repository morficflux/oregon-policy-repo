  PURPOSE

  To establish protocol for flying the United States flag, the Oregon State flag and the National League of
  Families’ Prisoners of War or Missing in Action (POW/MIA) flag simultaneously at public buildings.

  APPLICABILITY

  All state agencies that own or operate public buildings as defined in this policy, including county courthouses,
  county, municipal, school district and special district buildings.

  DEFINITIONS

  Agency: the state agency that owns, occupies or controls a public building.

  Municipal: cities, towns and municipal corporations.

  Public building: state institutions and state agency headquarters upon which the Department of Administrative
  Services (DAS) determines it is suitable to display the Oregon State flag; county courthouses; and all other
  county, municipal, school district and special district buildings upon which or near which it is customary and
  suitable to display the United States flag. In a complex or campus of multiple state buildings refer to Section
  1e in General information. Privately owned buildings do not fall under the requirements of this policy.

  EXCLUSIONS AND SPECIAL SITUATIONS

  Memorial Day exception noted in section two.

  GENERAL INFORMATION

    1) General
       a) When flagpoles or other suitable infrastructure exist at principal public buildings, the body having
          custody of each public building must display three flags simultaneously: The United States flag,
          Oregon State flag and National League of Families’ POW/MIA flag. The flags must be flown during
          the hours when the United States flag is customarily displayed, and at other times as seems proper,
          except in unsuitable weather. If it is not possible to fly all three flags simultaneous, the agency body
          must fly the United States flag and the Oregon State flag.

        b) Field offices are not considered principal buildings of state government, and no requirement exists to
           fly flags at field offices. However, the DAS suggests that state agencies lean in favor of flying the
           flags upon or near the building, or indoors.

        c) After January 1, 2016, new construction of state institutions, courthouses and all other state-owned
           principal buildings must include sufficient infrastructure to properly display all three flags
           simultaneously: United States flag, Oregon State flag and National League of Families’ POW/MIA flag.

        d) After January 1, 2018, new construction of all county, municipal, school district and special district
           buildings, upon which or near which it is customary and suitable to display the United States flag,
           must include sufficient infrastructure to properly display all three flags simultaneously: United States
           flag, Oregon State flag and National League of Families’ POW/MIA flag.

        e) In a complex or campus of multiple state buildings, one set of flags for the campus is adequate. Fly
           the flags upon or near whichever building is considered:

                 A. The major building of the headquarters functioned at the complex or campus.
                 B. The largest building.
                 C. The main administration building.
                 D. The building which is most centrally located or most appropriately located for a flag display.

        f) Although state agencies are not required to fly flags at privately-owned leased offices, DAS suggests
           that agencies consider displaying the flags indoors, especially if a leased building houses multiple
           agencies.

        g) Handle all flags with due respect. Refer to “other flag protocol” in section three for resources for
           handling flags.

        h) Display flags only from sunrise to sunset on buildings and on stationary flagstaffs in the open.
           However, when a patriotic effect is desired, the flags may be displayed 24 hours a day if properly
           illuminated during the hours of darkness.

        i)   Cost for flags, poles, necessary appliances and care are to be paid out of the funds available for
             public building maintenance according to ORS 186.120.

    2) Half-staff: Agencies fly flags at half-staff when ordered by the Governor. The Governor issues a press
       release, which DAS emails to subscribers of its Flag Notification listserv. The recipients of the email are
       responsible for notifying their sub-units.

        Memorial Day exception: Agencies displaying the flag on Memorial Day do not need an order to display
        the flag at half-staff until noon. However, flags flown at half-staff until noon on Memorial Day must then
        be raised to the top of the staff for the afternoon.

    3) Other flag protocol
          a) U.S. Code, Title 4, chapter 1, Section 7: The Flag (text provided by Cornell University law school).

             b) View Public Law 94-344 – July 7, 1976, 90 STAT. 810

    4) Flagpole height and flag size guidelines
       The installation site will help determine the proper flagpole composition and height. Check with city or
       county planning departments to verify the municipality’s requirements before deciding on the height of a
       flagpole. The wall thickness of the flagpole is the major factor in determining how strong and how
       susceptible to wind a flagpole is. Flagpoles have limits to the size of flag which can be safely flown.
       Flagpole and flag size guidelines:

               Height of Flagpole                      Minimum Flag Size            Maximum Flag Size
                    20 feet                               3 feet x 5 feet             5 feet x 8 feet
                    60 feet                              8 feet x 12 feet            12 feet x 18 feet
                    70 feet                             10 feet x 15 feet            15 feet x 25 feet
                    80 feet                             10 feet x 19 feet            20 feet x 30 feet
