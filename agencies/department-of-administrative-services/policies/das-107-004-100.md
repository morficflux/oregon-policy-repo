# Transporting Information Assets (DAS Statewide Policy 107-004-100)

## At a glance

Requires agencies to use appropriate security controls when physically
transporting sensitive information assets (tape, disk, paper) beyond a facility's
boundaries. Sensitivity determination is governed by Policy 107-004-050 (Information
Asset Classification). Effective 01-31-08; established under 2005 Oregon Laws Chapter 739
and OAR 125-800-0005/-0010/-0020 (renumbered to 128-030 effective 5/1/2026).

## Key provisions

(from visual reading of the scanned source)

- Each agency sending, receiving, or transporting confidential/sensitive information is
  responsible for protecting it in transit; the information owner identifies sensitive
  information and ensures protection.
- Guidelines cover: carrier considerations (approved carriers, identification checks,
  security/liability contract language, locked and logged vehicle transport); packaging
  (physical protection, tamper-evident packaging, content manifest, secure labeling);
  storage at each end and in the vehicle; chain-of-custody logging with signatures; and
  controls such as locked containers, encryption, hand delivery, split consignments, and
  secure lockboxes.
- Applicability mirrors the 125-800-0020 pattern: Executive Branch agencies per ORS
  174.112, with the standard exceptions.
