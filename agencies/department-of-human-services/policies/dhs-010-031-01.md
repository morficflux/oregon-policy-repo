# Completing Records Requests from Federal Immigration Authorities (ODHS Policy 010-031-01)

                                           DIRECTOR'S OFFICE

Process steps
Title:                 Completing records requests from federal immigration authorities

Related to:            ODHSOHA 010-031

Effective date:        05/01/2025

Purpose
This document provides a process for agency staff to use in the event staff receive requests, either
written or in person, from a federal immigration authority for the purpose of immigration enforcement.

Federal immigration authorities include:
   1. United States (US) Department of Homeland Security.
   2. US Immigration and Customs Enforcement.
   3. US Citizenship and Immigration Services.
   4. US Customs and Border Protection.
   5. Other federal immigration agencies or officials.
   6. Successor agencies to those listed.
   7. Entities that have delegated authority to detect, investigate or enforce violations of immigration
      law.

Immigration enforcement means an activity that has as its purpose the apprehension or identification
of an individual to:
    1. Subject the person to civil immigration arrest or detention, removal or deportation, removal or
       deportation proceedings,
    2. Criminally prosecute the person for offenses related to federal law regarding immigration
       status.

Process steps
1. If ODHS staff receive a request for records or information related to the citizenship, immigration
   status, or country of birth of a client, employee, volunteer, or any Oregonian, staff:
   a. Remains calm and professional.
   b. Asks for and writes down the full name and agency of the person making the request.
   c. Asks for and writes down information about the person, including badge number and a
       business card, if possible.
   d. Asks to make copies of any presented documents such as subpoenas, warrants, or court
       orders.
   e. Asks the purpose of the request.
   f. Immediately reports to the staff person’s supervisor or manager.

2. Requests may be verbal, written, subpoena, court order or warrant.
3. After notifying their manager or supervisor, the staff person who received the request, or the
   manager or supervisor contacts the ODHS Office of Resilience and Emergency Management
   (OREM) Staff Duty Officer by calling 503-373-2366. OREM staff are available 24 hours per day,
   seven days per week.
4. Documents related to the request should be emailed to OREM.Operations@odhsoha.oregon.gov.
5. OREM reviews the information and contacts the ODHS Legal Unit.
6. The ODHS Legal Unit consults the Oregon Department of Justice to ensure the appropriate
   response
7. The ODHS Legal Unit provides staff with additional instructions, if necessary.
8. The ODHS Legal Unit responds to the request, as appropriate.
9. The OHDS Legal Unit completes the Sanctuary Violation Reporting Form.
