# Drug and Alcohol Testing of Employees (DOC Policy 20.5.18)

I.         PURPOSE

           To help ensure the workplace is free from the effects of drug and alcohol abuse.

II.        DEFINITIONS

           A.   Controlled Substance: Defined in accordance with ORS 475.005(6). Prohibited
                drugs or controlled substances include marijuana, cocaine, opiates,
                phencyclidine (PCP), amphetamines and substances specified in schedules I
                through V of Section 202 of the Controlled Substances Act, 21 USC 811, 812 and
                as defined in 21 CFR 1300.11 through 1300.15 unless authorized by a legal
                prescription or are exempt from federal or State law.
                Note: Marijuana is still considered a controlled substance within the definition of
                this policy

           B.   Reasonable Suspicion (of prohibited drug use, possession, manufacture,
                distribution, or selling, or for prohibited alcohol use): Means a belief, based on
                specific and articulable facts, that an employee may be engaged in prohibited drug
                use, possession, manufacture, distribution, or selling, or prohibited alcohol use.
                Reasonable suspicion may be established through one or more of the following:

                1.   Direct observation of abnormal behavior or impairment to mental or physical
                     behavior or performance (for example, slurred speech, alcohol on the breath,
                     difficulty in walking or performing job activities, etc.)

                2.   Direct observation of use, possession, etc.

               3.   The opinion of a medical professional (doctor, nurse or other related
                    professional practitioner qualified by education or experience) employed at
                    the work site where an employee is using a prohibited drug.

               4.   Documented or verified information concerning the workplace manufacture,
                    distribution, selling, possession or use of a prohibited drug, or use of alcohol
                    on the job or before reporting for duty.

               5.   A work-related accident in conjunction with an above basis for a reasonable
                    suspicion.

          C.   Under the Influence (of controlled substances): Means being affected by
               controlled substances, including marijuana or alcoholic intoxicants in a manner
               that encompasses not only all commonly recognized conditions and degrees of
               impairment and intoxication, but also any condition that diminishes the
               employee’s clarity of intellect or control that they would otherwise possess.

III.     POLICY

         In maintaining an alcohol-free and drug-free workplace, the Department of Corrections
         implements a program of drug and alcohol testing of employees. The presence or
         treatment of a substance use problem will not excuse an employee from meeting
         performance, safety, or attendance standards, or following other department
         instructions.

          A.   General Testing Provisions

               In addition to specific testing provisions for employees who are required to possess
               a Commercial Driver’s License (CDL) in the performance of their duties (see DOC
               Policy 20.5.19), and specific testing provisions for certain represented employees
               (see applicable collective bargaining agreements), the following testing provisions
               are implemented:

               1.   Management and executive service employees are subject to reasonable
                    suspicion drug and alcohol testing. Refusal to submit to such testing may
                    result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

               2.   Any employee who is not otherwise subject to mandatory reasonable
                    suspicion testing may be requested to voluntarily submit to reasonable
                    suspicion drug and alcohol testing.

                    a.     Unless otherwise specified by policy or a collective bargaining
                           agreement, an employee’s refusal to submit to testing will not, by itself,
                           result in disciplinary action.

                    b.     The employee may still be subject to discipline for any inappropriate
                           workplace behavior, if such discipline is appropriate and applicable.

          B.   Prohibited Conduct

               The following conduct is prohibited:

               1.   Buying, selling, or providing, or possession for the purpose of buying, selling,
                    or providing controlled substances while on department property or in any
                    vehicles or equipment used for department business, or during work hours,
                    including paid rest and meal periods.

               2.   Possession of any controlled substance while on department property or in
                    any vehicles or equipment used for department business at any time,
                    including rest and meal periods. Substances that have been legally prescribed
                    for an employee’s own use are excluded.

               3.   Being under the influence of any controlled substance or having such
                    substances present in the body while on department property or in any
                    vehicles or equipment used for department business at any time, including
                    rest and meal periods.

                    a.     An employee has controlled substance “present in the body” when the
                           employee tests “positive” in blood or urine tests administered by the
                           department for drug and alcohol testing.

                    b.     An employee shall be deemed to test “positive” for cannabinoids
                           (marijuana or hashish) if their urine test indicates 50 nanograms of
                           tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) metabolites per ml or more.

                    c.     Substances that have been legally prescribed for an employee’s own use
                           are excluded.

               4.   Being under the influence of alcoholic intoxicants, or consuming alcoholic
                    intoxicants while in any vehicles or equipment used for department business
                    at any time, or on department property during work hours, including rest and
                    meal periods.

               5.   Being at work with a blood alcohol content that reaches or exceeds .02% by
                    volume of alcohol in the blood.

               6.   Abusing any substance which is lawfully prescribed by regularly taking it in
                    excessive quantities or by unlawfully obtaining it for purposes of abuse.

          C.   Discipline and Other Action

               1.   Prohibited conduct described in Sections (B)(1), (B)(2), and (B)(3) above shall
                    result in termination.

               2.   Prohibited conduct described in Sections (B)(4), (B)(5), and (B)(6) shall result

                    in actions specified in Section E. below.

               3.   Correctional Series staff and Parole and Probation staff certified by the
                    Department of Public Safety Standards and Training (DPSST) are also subject
                    to discipline set by the Commission of Statewide Law Enforcement Standards
                    of Conduct and Discipline under OAR Ch. 265.

          D.   Reasonable Suspicion Testing

               1.   Where the department has a reasonable suspicion that an employee subject
                    to mandatory reasonable suspicion testing is under the influence of any
                    alcoholic intoxicants or controlled substances, or has a controlled substance,
                    present in the body, the department:

                    a.     May require the employee immediately consent and submit to field and
                           impairment tests and sampling (blood, urine or breath alcohol test) at an
                           approved laboratory. A refusal to consent and submit to any of these tests
                           shall be deemed the same as a positive test result.

                    b.     Shall pay for the costs of the tests.

               2.   When a represented employee is informed that the department has a
                    reasonable suspicion that the employee is under the influence, they are:

                    a.     Required to consent and submit to such test or searches as described in
                           Section G. of this policy; or

                    b.     If not required to voluntarily submit to such testing, they may request the
                           presence of an official union or association representative to witness the
                           tests or searches.

                           A. The test or searches may not be unduly delayed to wait for a
                              representative.

                           B. The absence of a representative shall not be grounds for the employee
                              to refuse to consent and submit to such tests or searches.

                           C. The presence of a representative shall not disrupt or interfere with the
                              tests or searches.

                           D. The department shall make every reasonable effort to provide such
                              representative.

               3.   Before a supervisor, acting on behalf of the department under this policy, may
                    require an employee to consent and submit to any test(s) specified in this
                    section, the supervisor must first obtain concurrence from the supervisor’s
                    department head or designee that the information available to the department

                      about the subject employee is sufficient to form the basis of a reasonable
                      suspicion that prohibited conduct will be established as a result of such
                      test(s).

            4.        The employee shall give consent to a blood, urine or breath alcohol test by
                      signing a consent form (CD1399 for Executive Service, Management Service,
                      and AFSCME Security employees; CD1400 for AOCE Represented
                      employees). The form shall contain the following information:

                      a.    Employee’s consent to release test results to the department.

                      b.    The procedure for confirming an initial positive test result for a
                            controlled substance.

                      c.    The consequences of a confirmed positive test result for a controlled
                            substance.

                      d.    The consequences of a positive test for alcohol, including one at or
                            above .02% by volume of alcohol in the blood.

                      e.    A listing provided by the employee of legally prescribed and over-the-
                            counter medications which may be in the employee’s body. At the
                            employee’s option, this information may be submitted in a sealed
                            envelope to be opened only by the lab’s Medical Review Officer if the
                            test result is positive.

                      f.    Acknowledgment of the employee’s right to explain a confirmed
                            positive test result for a controlled substance, or a positive test for
                            alcohol to the lab’s Medical Review Officer.

                      g     Consequences of refusing to consent to the blood, urine, or breath
                            alcohol test.

                 5.   Signed consent forms are sent to the Designated Employee Representative
                      and to the Human Resources Business Partner. The Designated Employee
                      Representative will maintain the record for 5 years.

                 6.   The department shall contract for drug testing services with a state-licensed
                      laboratory which shall:

                      a.    Be required to follow all state and federal testing protocols.

                      b.    Use a drug testing process that is scientifically proven to be at least as
                            accurate and valid as urinalysis using an immunoassay screening test,
                            with all positive screening results being confirmed utilizing gas
                            chromatography or mass spectrometry before a sample is considered
                            positive.

                    c.     Use an alcohol testing process that is scientifically proven to be at least
                           as accurate and valid as:

                           A. Urinalysis using an enzymatic assay screening test, with all positive
                              screening results being confirmed utilizing gas chromatography
                              before a sample is considered positive; or

                           B. Breath sample testing using breath analyzing instruments which meet
                              National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) or Substance Abuse and
                              Mental Health Services Administration (SAMSHA) testing standards.

               7.   If a blood or urine test is confirmed as positive, the department will instruct the
                    laboratory to retain the blood or urine sample for a period of not less than 30
                    calendar days from the date the test results are communicated to the
                    employee for the purpose of allowing the employee to conduct an
                    independent test of the sample at their own expense at a laboratory approved
                    by the State of Oregon.

               8.   The procedure to obtain, handle, and store blood and urine samples and to
                    conduct laboratory tests shall be documented to establish procedural
                    integrity and chain of evidence.

                    a.     Such procedures shall be administered with due regard for the
                           employee’s privacy and the need to maintain confidentiality of test
                           results to an extent which is not inconsistent with the needs of this policy:

                    b.     The employee shall be notified of the results of all tests conducted
                           pursuant to this policy; and

               9.   All facts and circumstances upon which the reasonable suspicion testing is
                    based shall be documented and given to the employee when they are notified
                    of the test results.

          E.   Consequences of Test Results

               1.   Non-Positive Test Results: When test results do not positively establish the
                    employee has engaged in prohibited conduct as described in Sections (B)(4),
                    (B)(5), and (B)(6) of this policy:

                    a.     No further action against the employee related to an alleged violation of
                           those sections shall result.

                    b.     The employee shall be informed of such test results in writing.

                    c.     No record of the test shall be placed in the employee’s official personnel
                           file in Workday.

                           Supervisor’s working files may contain records of the observations which
                           led to the reasonable suspicion testing but not records of the test itself.

                    d.     The behavior which necessitated the test may be grounds for disciplinary
                           action in accordance with the applicable labor agreement or department
                           policy.

                           If the employee subsequently demonstrates similar behaviors, records of
                           the observations which led to the reasonable suspicion testing, but not
                           records of the test itself, may be relied upon by the employer in
                           disciplinary proceedings.

               2.   Positive Test Result of .040 or Higher- No Previous Prohibited Conduct:
                    Employees who test positive with test result of .040 or higher and have not
                    previously committed prohibited conduct specified in Sections (B)(4), (B)(5),
                    and (B)(6) in this policy:

                    a.     Shall immediately submit to a medical evaluation by a doctor, selected
                           and paid for by the department, to determine the extent of the employee’s
                           use of, and dependence on, the applicable substance(s) and, if
                           necessary and appropriate, recommend a program of treatment,
                           including but not limited to rehabilitation and counseling to prevent
                           future use; and

                    b.     If a program of treatment is recommended by the doctor, the employee
                           shall enroll in it immediately.

                           A. Failure by the employee to enroll in the recommended program or to
                              complete it successfully may result in termination from employment.

                           B. The cost of such treatment shall be at the employee’s expense except
                              as it may be covered by insurance.

                           C. The employee may take paid leave or leave without pay for the period
                              of treatment.

               3.   Positive Test Results - First Instance of Prohibited Conduct:

                    a.     The first instance of an alcohol test result of .02 to .039 shall not be
                           considered a positive test result for alcohol for the purpose of requiring a
                           medical evaluation by a doctor. It will, however, require that the
                           employee be removed from duty until the employee’s next scheduled
                           shift. An employee may use vacation or sick leave benefits for this time
                           period.

                    b.     The first instance of an alcohol test result of .04 or greater will subject the
                           employee to all provisions of this policy.

               4.   Positive Test Results - Previously Committed Prohibited Conduct: Employees
                    who test positive and have previously committed prohibited conduct specified
                    in Sections (B)(4), (B)(5), and (B)(6) and subsequently are found to have
                    committed such prohibited conduct a second time within three years,

                    a.     Shall be subject to discipline up to and including termination.

                    b.     The level of discipline imposed for subsequent instances of such
                           prohibited conduct beyond three years may be termination but shall be
                           determined on a case-by-case basis.

          F.   Voluntary Rehabilitation

               The primary objectives of the department’s drug and alcohol policy are to maintain
               employee performance and good health and a safe work environment.

               1.   If, prior to a requirement by the department that the employee submit to any
                    of the tests specified in Section E. of this policy, the employee notifies a
                    supervisor they have drug or alcohol problems that require treatment, then in
                    that event the employee;

                    a.     Shall immediately submit to a medical evaluation by a doctor, selected
                           and paid for by the department; and

                    b.     Shall enroll in a treatment program recommended by the doctor.

                    c.     The employee may seek such evaluation and treatment from the
                           employee’s own doctor, at the employee’s expense.

                           The employee shall notify the employer of the name of the doctor.

                    d.     An employee who enters rehabilitation and successfully completes
                           rehabilitation under the terms of this paragraph shall not be subject to
                           discipline.

                           A. The cost of such treatment shall be at the employee’s expense except
                              as it may be covered by insurance.

                           B. The employee may take paid leave or leave without pay for the period
                              of treatment.

               2.   If an employee has previously enrolled in a voluntary rehabilitative treatment
                    described above and subsequently again volunteers for such treatment in
                    advance of being required to submit to any of these tests specified in Section
                    E. of this policy, then the employee:

                    a.     Shall immediately submit to a medical evaluation by a doctor, selected
                           and paid for by the department; and

                    b.     Shall successfully complete the treatment program recommended by the
                           doctor.

                    c.     The employee may seek such evaluation and treatment from the
                           employee’s own doctor, at the employee’s expense.

                           The employee shall notify the employer of the name of the doctor.

                    d.     If the employee fails to complete the treatment program successfully,
                           they shall be subject to discipline up to and including termination.

                           A. The cost of such treatment shall be at the employee’s expense except
                              as it may be covered by insurance.

                           B. The employee may take paid leave or leave without pay for the period
                              of treatment.

          G. Searches

               1.   The department reserves the right to conduct searches of department
                    equipment or facilities generally for any reason and may search anything or
                    area in which the employee has an expectation of privacy (such as desk,
                    locker, outer garment clothing or personal property) to the extent permitted by
                    law.

               2.   Refusal by the employee to submit to a lawful search shall result in
                    termination.

               3.   Strip searches and frisk searches shall be undertaken in the event of a criminal
                    investigation and only for probable cause as determined by the investigating
                    law enforcement department.

          H.   Consequences of Search Results

               1.   Reasonable suspicion searches which do not reveal the presence of alcohol
                    or controlled substances (excluding any substance lawfully prescribed for the
                    employee’s use which has not been obtained for the purpose of abuse), shall
                    result in no further action against the employee related to an alleged violation
                    of Section (B)(2).

                    The employee shall be informed of such search results in writing.

               2.   Searches which reveal the presence of alcohol or controlled substances, (but
                    excluding any substance lawfully prescribed for the employee’s use which has
                    not been obtained for the purpose of abuse) shall result in those
                    consequences specified in Sections (C) and (E)(3) as though a positive blood
                    or confirmed urine test had been administered.

          I.   Training for Supervisory Personnel

               Supervisory personnel shall receive training to recognize and deal effectively with
               substance abuse in the workplace.

IV.      IMPLEMENTATION

         This policy will be adopted immediately without further modification.

                  Executive Service, Management Service, and AFSCME Security

Per Department of Corrections Policy 20.5.18 Drug and Alcohol Testing of Employees and the applicable Collective
Bargaining Agreement, employees shall give consent to a blood, urine, or breath alcohol test by signing this consent
form. This consent form provides the employee being tested the following information:

A.      Your signature represents consent to release the test results to the Department of Corrections.

B.      An initial positive test result for a controlled substance will be confirmed using gas
        chromatography/mass spectrometry before a sample is considered positive.

C.      The consequences of a confirmed positive test for a controlled substance:

        1.       If you have not previously committed prohibited conduct relating to drugs or alcohol, you shall
                 immediately submit to a medical evaluation by a doctor selected and paid for by the department. The
                 evaluation will determine the extent of your use of, and dependence on, the applicable substance(s)
                 and, if necessary, recommend an appropriate program of treatment, including but not limited to
                 rehabilitation and counseling to prevent future use. If the doctor recommends a treatment program,
                 you shall enroll in it immediately. Failure by you to enroll in the recommended program or to
                 complete it successfully shall result in your termination from employment.

        2.       If you test positive and have previously committed prohibited conduct and subsequently are found to
                 have committed prohibited conduct a second time within three years, you shall be terminated. The
                 level of discipline imposed for subsequent instances of such prohibited conduct beyond three
                 years may be termination but shall be determined on a case-by-case basis.

D.      The consequences of a positive test for alcohol: the first instance of an alcohol test result of
        .02 - .039 shall not be considered a positive test result for the purpose of requiring a medical evaluation by
        a doctor. It will, however, require that you be removed from duty until your next scheduled shift. You may
        use vacation and sick leave benefits for this time period. An alcohol test result of .04 or greater will subject
        you to C (1) and (2) above.

E.      If you receive a confirmed positive drug test, a Medical Review Officer (MRO) will call you. You will be asked for
        a listing of legally prescribed and over-the-counter medication which may be in your body. It is your option to
        provide this information.

F.      You have the right to explain a confirmed positive test result for a controlled substance or a positive test for
        alcohol to a Medical Review Officer (MRO).

G.      Represented staff have the right to union representation during preliminary interview and any follow-up
        investigation.

H.      The refusal to consent and submit to any of these tests shall subject you to immediate termination.
